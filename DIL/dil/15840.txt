<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="15840">1 dī ̆-, dē̆-</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 1 dī ̆-, dē̆- or <a class="reference" href="15840">dil.ie/15840</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                dí-, de-, dí-, dé-, de-, dim-, di-</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   

    <p class="result">
      <span class="trans">Negative <span class="pos prefix" style="color: #ff8000">prefix</span> before nouns, adjj., participles, 
verbals of necessity, and verbs:</span> 
        di- cech la cein is diultad, 
alaill is aidbliugud, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm.Y" title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm. Y</strong></span> 418
        
      . Before consonants usually 
dí- (díchoisc, dígraiss, dílmain, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>). <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> déinmech (dí-gnímach). 
de- + ro gives a new prefix <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=der-">der-</a></span>, <span class="lbl">q.v.</span> Before vowels the form 
varies. dí-: díármithe, díainme, díaisndísiu, (all from O.Ir. 
 
glosses). díorpus (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=orba">orba</a></span>), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Trip.2" title="eDIL Bibliography">Trip.²</strong></span> 1525
      . With elision: dairmitiu, 
dermitiu (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 airmitiu">1 airmitiu</a></span>), denim (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainim">ainim</a></span>, also díainim <span class="ediltr">unblemished</span>), 
deithbir (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aithber">aithber</a></span>), delam (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=alam">alam</a></span>), deinmne (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainmne">ainmne</a></span>), dergnaid 
(<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=&#xE9;rgnaid">érgnaid</a></span>), dindell (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=indell">indell</a></span>) Before f-: dífulacht, diulacht 
(<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 fulacht">1 fulacht</a></span>), díḟulaing, díulaing (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=fulang">fulang</a></span>), díḟuráil (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=fur&#xF3;il">furóil</a></span>), dífholaig (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=fulach">fulach</a></span>). Diarmait, Dermait (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=format">format</a></span>) (the earliest exx. are 
 
written thus but in later lang. Díarmait). dē̆olaid (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=folad">folad</a></span>) 
 
(deolid in Wb.: Eogain, LL). dē̆orad (dí-fo-rath) (no early 
rhyming ex., deóra: feóla, Met. Dinds.). dé-, de- in nonce-compds. to translate Latin <span class="frn ">de-</span>: déainmmneichthech <span class="frn ">denominatiuum</span>, dechomsuidigthe <span class="frn ">decompositum</span>. désetaige gl. 
(<span class="frn ">vita) devia</span>. See 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> §§ 831
      , 
        873
      . Lenites follg. 
initial. cenn, however, has its c mutated to g, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> dícenn, 
dígenn <span class="ediltr">headless man</span> (see O'Rahilly, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xiii 140
      ). Before b 
the prefix takes the form dim-; dimbáeth (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 b&#xE1;eth">1 báeth</a></span>), dimbág, 
dimblad, dimbláith, dimblas, dimbocc, dimbraig (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=br&#xED;g">bríg</a></span>), dimbríg, dimbras, dimbuaid, dimbuan, dimda (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=2 buide">2 buide</a></span>). 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> ii 
214
      , regards this as a development of di- before ml, mr 
spreading by analogy. 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> p. 543
      , suggests that 
it may be a different prefix. Note also dimmolaid (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=molaid">molaid</a></span>) 
 <span class="ediltr">disparages</span>. Most freq. used with nouns to form adjectival 
compds. In these compds. o-stems and ā-stems generally take 
i-inflection, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> díaistir (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aister">aister</a></span>), díaitrib, díáraig, díchéill, 
díchomlaind, díchuind, díradairc. Occasional exceptions are: 
díchomond (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=commann">commann</a></span>), díchondelg, and díoḟaosam (also diḟaessaim). As a number of these compds. were indistinguishable 
from the gen. sg. of noun preceded by dí- they are treated in 
the later language simply as attributive genitives, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> 
        daíne 
dochla díchumaing, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 3472
        
       (see Bergin, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xii 229
      ). i-stems remain unchanged but gs. tends to be used in the later 
language, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> díainim, dícheist, díḟuráil but díaithgina, 
dichollna (colainn). u-stems remain unchanged (as far as the 
exx. serve), <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> dígrád (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=gr&#xE1;d">grád</a></span> <span class="ediltr">love</span>), dínním (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=sn&#xED;m">sním</a></span>), díṡolus. 
Nominal compds. formed from o- and ā-stems vary in inflexion, though on the whole they appear to resist the change to 
i-inflexion, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> díardain and díardan (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ord(d)an">ord(d)an</a></span>) <span class="ediltr">anger</span> (but 
always díordain <span class="ediltr">angry</span>), díchretem and díchretim (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=creitem">creitem</a></span>) 
 <span class="ediltr">disbelief</span>, díchíall <span class="ediltr">lack of sense</span> (always díchéill <span class="ediltr">senseless</span>), dígrád 
 <span class="ediltr">dishonour</span> (always dígráid adj.), díthrub, -thrab. Other stems 
remain unaffected: dígním, díchruth, deinmne (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainmne">ainmne</a></span>), 
dígallrae. The adj. gand gives dígaind lit. <span class="ediltr">not scanty</span>. Adjj. 
in -ech show no change and may be derivatives rather than 
true compds.: díchoimsech, díḟoirithnech, díthaithmech. 
Otherwise there is no change. With partt., verbals of necessity, and verbs: díáirmide, díaisneithe, díaisneisi <span class="ediltr">unspeakable</span>, 
díbrethe <span class="ediltr">not to be borne</span>, díerrethe <span class="ediltr">not to be paid</span>; díchenglaid, 
díchlannaid, dimmolaid.</p>
