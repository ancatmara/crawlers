<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="14760">dattae</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. dattae or <a class="reference" href="14760">dil.ie/14760</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2019</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                datta, data, dathta</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos adj" style="color: #ff8000">adj</span> 
      <span class="itype io-i&#x101;">io-iā</span>, datta, data, late MSS also dathta (daththa 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/23N15" title="eDIL Bibliography">23 N 15</strong></span>, 164. 13
      , 18th century MS); rhymes with brecnata 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 3933
      , 
        dínerta, 
          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 2831

        bratta,

      , 
        curata,

394. 3
        
      , 
        saṁradhta,

       , 
        fata, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MacCarthy" title="eDIL Bibliography">MacCarthy</strong></span> 206.
5
        
      ; 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 129 b 21
      ; 
        157 a 9
      . COMP. dattu, rhyming with 

        maccu, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 142 a 12
        
      ; 
        datta, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 274. 21
        
      .—dattae has 
the appearance of an adj. deriv. of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 dath">1 dath</a></span> or of a <span class="pos past-pc." style="color: #ff8000">past pc.</span> of 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=dathaid">dathaid</a></span>; but apparently the feeling of its connection with 
dath was early lost, as the marked divergence in meaning 
shows. For semantic development see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;tudesCelt." title="eDIL Bibliography">Études Celt.</strong></span> xxxv 168
      . Apart from the phrase `is dattae lim' (already 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 8 a 6
      ), it mostly occurs in chevilles and stereotyped 
phrases, very often, as it seems, introduced to alliterate with 
a neighbouring noun in d-. It seems mainly a literary 
word, used by Mid.-Ir. writers in the vague sense of súairc 
and cáin, by which it is occasionally replaced even in comparatively old MSS (Rawl.).</p>
