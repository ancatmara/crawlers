<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="33229">no co, na co, nó co</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. no co, na co, nó co or <a class="reference" href="33229">dil.ie/33229</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                na co, nó co</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos part" style="color: #ff8000">part</span> mod. Ir. nó go <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">until</span>
      </span>. The orig. form is 
na &gt; no, but exx. of nó occur fairly early, e.g. 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 9225.
      
    </p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
Acc. to 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> ii 318
      -
        319
      , a development of O.Ir. na co n- 
used with the subj. mood to join a conditional clause to a 
neg. main clause, the orig. sense being `<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">unless</span>
      </span>', passing by 
degrees into that of `<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">until</span>
      </span>'; the development was prob. influenced by the independent use of the conj. co n- `<span class="quote">until</span>'. 
During the Mid.Ir. period na co (no co,  nó co) comes to mean 
simply <span class="ediltr">until</span> and to be used with an affirm. main clause, and 
with the indic. mood (generally with a pret. to denote an 
actual fact which occurred at the end of a period indicated in 
the main sent.).</p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) with subj. follg. neg. main clause: na co risat fir hErend 
. . . ni ricub sa arís in fer <span class="ediltr">I will not go to the man again unless</span> 
(<span class="ediltr">until) the men of Ireland go</span>,

          4939
         =

get [the bull] by force, they shall not get it as a gift</span>,
