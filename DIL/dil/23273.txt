<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="23273">2 for, far, bar, uar</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 2 for, far, bar, uar or <a class="reference" href="23273">dil.ie/23273</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2019</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                FOR, FAR, BAR, BAR, BUR, BHUR, f-, b-</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   

    <p class="result">
      <span class="per 2">2</span> <span class="number pl.">pl.</span> poss. <span class="pos pron." style="color: #ff8000">pron.</span>, nasal. <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 sar">1 sar</a></span>, 
sethar, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=fathar">fathar</a></span>. See 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> p. 277
      , 
        283
      . No discrimination in use of FOR and FAR is apparent in the Glosses; 
BAR seems confined to position after the preps. DI, DO, FO, 
FRI, TRI, I N-, but the f- forms are also found in this position 
occasionally. In Mid.-Ir. MSS. all three forms are found 
with and without prep. but BAR, BUR (in modern spelling 
BHUR) eventually prevails. In Mid. Ir. MSS. the forms 
are commonly abbreviated : f-, b-.</p>
