<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="33757">2 ol</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 2 ol or <a class="reference" href="33757">dil.ie/33757</a></span><br/></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   


    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos conj." style="color: #ff8000">conj.</span> <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">for</span>, <span class="def">inasmuch</span> as, <span class="def">since</span>
      </span>; folld. by absol. form of vb.

(rel. form in 3 s. cop.). In 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 29c10
      : ol dun-aircibed (gl. quod

erat . . . illatura), it seems used to translate the rel. pron.

<span class="frn ">quod</span>; <span class="lbl">cf.</span> also 
        3a13
       quoted below. Confined to O.Ir. and early

Mid.Ir., and most freq. used with cop.</p>
  
  
    <p class="result">With cop.: ol, gl. quod (= <span class="ediltr">because</span>), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 216b7
      . ol as rann

<span class="ediltr">because it is a part</span>, 
        25b8
       (gl. quod syllaba pars est). is mo ro

chéess Crist . . . ol is amein léic uait inna tuari no cari <span class="ediltr">since it

is so, cast from thee</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 6c8
      . ol is amein attáa <span class="ediltr">for it is thus

he is</span>, 
        32a6
       (gl. obsecro te pro meo filio, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/NT" title="eDIL Bibliography">Philem.</strong></span> 10
      ); <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        13a13
      .

olais [<span class="lbl">leg.</span> ol as] airchenn teicht do écaib <span class="ediltr">since going to death is

certain</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> ii 296.9 
      = 
        uair is airchenn, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ACL" title="eDIL Bibliography">ACL</strong></span> iii 215 z
        
      . ol

am triamain tróg-sa <span class="ediltr">for I am wretched</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> Prol. 26. 
      
      
        do

rígrad no molur | ol is tu mo ruiri, 
          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 14
        
       (ar <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>). duit-siu, ol it

fissi, | adsluindiu a taissi <span class="ediltr">for they are meet to be known</span>, <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 
        Ep.
335
       (ol at <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>). ol ní tiamdae tim-som, | ní émsither annsom

<span class="ediltr">since he is not slow,</span> <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Prolegomena" title="eDIL Bibliography">Prolegomena</strong></span> 259. 
      
      finta dún . . . ol at eola ecnai

<span class="ediltr">for thou art versed in lore</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xi 109 § 16 
      =

      [eala

ecna, fcs.]. With other verbs: cid armad machdad anísin ol

atá lee dano a n-iṅgnad so <span class="ediltr">since it has this marvel</span>,

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 3a13 
      (gl. quod opusculum . . . si transtuleris).

ná tuc an cláen for mo lighe; ol ma doghne, bid ifirnn duit

<span class="ediltr">for if thou do so</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Anecd." title="eDIL Bibliography">Anecd.</strong></span> i 72 § 197 
      (where ol is perh. to be

connected with the cop. bid). See 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thurn." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thurn. Hdb.</strong></span> § 476
      , 
        §893
      

and 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wortk." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wortk.</strong></span> 32.
      
    </p>
  
  
    <p class="result">In the follg. exx. from Fél. Stokes sees a different word,

which he equates with O'Donovan's ol .i. ocas: in rígrad

doruirmius | ol is loimm de romuir `<span class="quote">and it is (only) a sip from

an ocean</span>' (?), 
      
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> Ep. 42. 
      
      céni eslind lib-si . . . ol am dedblén

truag-sa `<span class="quote">though you have no danger, yet I am a wretched

weakling</span>', 
        399
       (possibly ol am is rather to be connected with

words of the preceding stanza: atá sund . . . dedblén, céni <span class="lbl">etc.</span>

being parenthetical).</p>
