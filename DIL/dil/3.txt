        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> ii 339

        40

      <span class="gen m.">m.</span> and <span class="gen n.">n.</span> 
(leniting), <span class="per 3">3</span> <span class="number s.">s.</span>
      <span class="gen f.">f.</span> (geminating) and <span class="per 3">3</span> <span class="number pl.">pl.</span> of all genders (nasalising). When a numeral intervenes between poss. and noun 
the usage fluctuates. The older is represented by 
        a nda ara. 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 4729

such as adám <span class="trans">my two</span>, adád <span class="trans">your two</span>, adár <span class="trans">our two</span>. Similarly 
with <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=tr&#xED;">trí</a></span>. See 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xi 146 ff.
       and the numerals <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=d&#xE1;">dá</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=tr&#xED;">trí</a></span>.</p>
