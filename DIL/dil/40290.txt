<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="40290">techtugad</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. techtugad or <a class="reference" href="40290">dil.ie/40290</a></span><br/></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype u">u</span>, <span class="gen m.">m.</span> <span class="pos vn." style="color: #ff8000">vn.</span> of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=techtaigid">techtaigid</a></span>. In Laws Comm.</p>
   
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) 
<span class="trans">name of the process of <span class="def">taking possession</span> of <span class="def">land</span>, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>
        </span>, see 
the tract Din Techtugad, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iv 2
        -
          64
        . The claimant entered a 
certain distance on to the land, taking with him animals 
(for grazing) and witnesses (the word somet. designates 
these rather than the actual process: acht t.<span class="oRef">¤</span> do breith 
`<span class="quote">provided that he has brought the means of taking possession</span>,' 

          4.15
        ). The procedure was divided into three periods of ten 
days: 
          a forba na cét dechmaide ... na dechmaide medonchi 
... na dechmaide deigenaighe, 
            3.23
          
        -
          4.4
        , the distance travelled 
and the number of the accompanying animals and witnesses 
increasing with each period. The requirements varied 
somewhat according as the claimant was a man or a woman: 

          brig ... isi conmididhar banteallach .i. isi ro meisemnaigestar in t.<span class="oRef">¤</span> banda, 
            16.1
          
        -
          10
        . 
          ni bi i mbantellach acht co 
coirib, ┐ losat, ┐ criathar ... .i. ni dligtech do breith do 
techtughadh ferainn acht cairig ..., 
            i 148.1
          
        , 
          12
         text and 
Comm. Possession was established at the end of the third 
period: is na teora dechmaid ata dligedh in techtaigthe ... 
dlegair de a crod uile do breith ind `<span class="quote">in three times ten days 
the legal right of possession is to be conceded ... it is required 
of him</span> (<span class="ediltr">he has the right ?) to bring all his cattle there</span>', 
          iv 20.12
        . 
In some cases (for grazing purposes only ?) the process was 
unnecessary: tir dibad coitcind, uair noco neicen apad 
teora ndechmad imesein, na t.<span class="oRef">¤</span> do breith indti; but land on 
loan or rented (?) had to be recovered in this way: 
          in 
ferand do berar ar óin no ar fochraic, is ind berair in t.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
          , 

            22.37
          
        . 
          for oin tucadh in ferann sunn; ┐ atathar iar re na 
hona 'g-a techtadugh, 
            v 496.y
          
        . Where no grazing was 
available, only witnesses were required: 
          in ferann ac na 
bi faichthi re breith cethra ind da t.<span class="oRef">¤</span>, amhuil ata Dun Araill, 
uair is daine berar da t.<span class="oRef">¤</span>-side, 
            iv 24.1
          
        . For further details 
see Laws Gloss.</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) <span class="trans">More widely <i>act of</i> <span class="def">taking possession</span>, <span class="def">appropriating</span> 
(legally or illegally):</span> a t.<span class="oRef">¤</span> na heaguilsi dóib sin uada `<span class="quote">these 
are due of him for taking lawful possession of the church</span>,' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> 
iii 76.14
        . flaith laiges tren-lige .i. beres t.<span class="oRef">¤</span> a ferann in ceile 
(of a chieftain who seeks to obtain possession of land by 
laying heavy burdens on the tenant), 
          v 360.4
        . masa t.<span class="oRef">¤</span> 
rucad isin ferann, is fiach techtaighthi tire ... ind ... Fiac 
techtaithe se ba do na huaislib `<span class="quote">the fine for (unlawfully) 
taking possession of land</span>,' 
          iii 146.z
        -
          148.5
        . diamudh di-a t.<span class="oRef">¤</span> 
doib fein, ar fath dichelta `<span class="quote">if it be to possess it for themselves</span>' 
(of goods rescued from fire), 
          v 300.13
        . im cach mbandte 
.i. im cach techtughad mbanda `<span class="trans">
          <span class="def">female property</span>
        </span>,' 
          i 144.18
        . 
Hence the phrases ar a mbi t.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
         <span class="trans">which is <span class="def">in private ownership</span>
        </span>, 
ar na fuil t.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
         <span class="trans">which is <span class="def">common property</span> (of <span class="def">land</span>, <span class="def">grazing</span>, and 
various articles):</span> in murain ara mbi techtughadh `<span class="quote">the 
sea-grass which has been appropriated</span>,' 
          i 170.24
        . cid ferann 
aili ar a mbi tectugad dechsat na hindilli `<span class="quote">another land 
lawfully occupied</span>,' 
          iv 140.3
        . ma ta teactugadh ar a claradh 
no ar chaelach na caelle `<span class="quote">have been appropriated</span>,' 
          186.18
        . 

          cia beth t.<span class="oRef">¤</span> ar in fid, noco dlegar a bith ar in fiad, uair is 
coitcenn do in cach inad, 
            v 484.21
          
        . ethar coitchenn ... ar 
na fuil t.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
         `<span class="quote">of which there is no private ownership</span>,' 
          iii 208.23
        . 

          fer na tulach naibind for na fuil t.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
          , 
            296.13
          
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
Various: 
          duine chindtaib a teallaig fo t.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
          , 
            v 462.21
          
         (text, 
but perh. interpolated gloss). mana roib a fis aici tellach 
techtaigi `<span class="quote">knowledge of entry for possession</span>,' 
          v 462.25
        . 
See <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=2 tellach">2 tellach</a></span>. 
          il-lúan brātha ic bertuccadh | techtogad 
roghabsator, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> vii 309.13
          
        .</p>
