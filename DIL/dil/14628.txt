    <p class="result"> 
Attar in the old text 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Anecd." title="eDIL Bibliography">Anecd.</strong></span> iii 59
      = atar (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> addar) as 
the frequent writing adar proves. Ata (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> adda) and inda 
are parallel forms; but they do not, as Quiggin (Vorton. 
Wörter p. 10) thinks, both render a spoken †edàr. The 
interchange between Wb ata: Mil inda recalls that between Tur, Sg, Laws, LU adás: Mil indás (
        adoasa, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Tur." title="eDIL Bibliography">Tur.</strong></span> 25a
        
      ; 
adaas quam est 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 190 a 4
      ; 
        adas, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 370. 1
          <span class="lbl">f.b.</span>
        
      ; 
        ná bad 
lia a chénel ata buiden, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Trip." title="eDIL Bibliography">Trip.</strong></span> i 148. 7
        
      ; am anáithiu atæsiu 
LU, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> ii2 213 l. 18
      ). For the use of in compare perhaps 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 5 b 34
      : si enim Deus naturalibus ramis non pepercit, 
gl. .i. indat <span class="frn ">iudei</span>
       (<span class="ediltr">the branches) which the Iudaei are (which 
the I. form)</span>. The short form da may be original or 
shortened from ata, the stress falling on the ultimate (noch 
da lim, Wb, almost as is-na-naicci, cach-la-sel and the like); 
in the Carm. Mil. it interchanges with inda to suit the 
metre; similarly dar and indar in SR. The usual explanation of the Mid.-Ir. indar as influenced by the 
deponent verb is wrong, as the form seems to have arisen 
at a time when the deponent was already on the wane. 
Early occurrences are 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 1674
      : dar lia ba dia airchisecht, 
and 
        6079
      : indar la; it is quite possible, however, that 
the original had `da lia ba dia a.' and `inda.' The r 
originates from the Mid.-Ir. preposition ra, ri = Old-Ir. la, fri, 
the r becoming an integral part of the preceding da; indar 
la, then, is a contamination of inda-ra and inda-la: comp. 
 indarra Loegaire in the LU-version of the 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/FB" title="eDIL Bibliography">FBr.</strong></span> § 64
      . A 
similar development is shown by Mod.-Ir. <span class="frn ">dóigh</span>, <span class="frn ">maith</span> and 
<span class="frn ">aice</span>, which in common speech often become <span class="frn ">dóil</span>, <span class="frn ">mail</span> 
and <span class="frn ">aicil</span>: 
        <span class="frn ">an dóigh leat</span>? <span class="frn ">Is dóil</span>; <span class="frn ">an maith leat?</span> <span class="frn ">Is 
mail</span>
       (Kerry); <span class="frn ">i naicil</span> = <span class="frn ">i naice</span>.</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
Usually at the head of the sentence, sometimes preceded 
by `ocus,' or an emphatic `ised' referring to the following 
sentence as

      : ised dar lind is ara deggnímradaib 
rop áil leo olc dfagbáil dó. At the end of the clause: is 
cosmuil aicned na redland uile dar leinn, TBith., 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> ii 126.
5

ndúl dar lem gur fégadh run rí Éirenn, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MacCarthy" title="eDIL Bibliography">MacCarthy</strong></span> 434. 3
        
       (BB).

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCL" title="eDIL Bibliography">OCL</strong></span> 16. 5
