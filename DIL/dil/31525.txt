<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="31525">manchaine</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. manchaine or <a class="reference" href="31525">dil.ie/31525</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                mainchine, manchaine</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype i&#x101;">iā</span>, <span class="gen f.">f.</span>, (manach), also with slender vowel mainchine:</p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">the <span class="def">duty</span> or <span class="def">calling</span> of a <span class="def">monk</span> and in general <i>the</i> <span class="def">monastic</span> <span class="def">life</span>:</span> 

          ro éla o tustigib i manchuine, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l.2" title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.²</strong></span> lxiii 27
          
        . 
          co na-tarad i mod 
manchine amail cach manach fognama do Día, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> 92.28
          
        . 
geibh misi it' manchaine (= <span class="ediltr">let me be a monk under you</span>), 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Lism.L." title="eDIL Bibliography">Lism. L.</strong></span> 3492
        , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          2039
        . tri. l. ri teoir a máinchine aici-sium 
in Hi `<span class="quote">thrice fifty monks had he for contemplation</span>', 
          1018
        . 
          tanic 
Constans a manchaine ┐ ro gabh rige, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Anecd." title="eDIL Bibliography">Anecd.</strong></span> iii 66
          
         (= <span class="frn ">C. . . . 
quem ex monacho Caesarem fecerat</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Beda" title="eDIL Bibliography">Beda</strong></span> i 11
        ). an 
t-airdeaspoc . . . do dhol i mainchini <span class="ediltr">retired into a monastery</span>, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AFM" title="eDIL Bibliography">AFM</strong></span> iii 144.1
        . <span class="trans">By extension <i>a</i> <span class="def">community</span> of <span class="def">monks</span>:</span> 
          bui maincine mor ime [<span class="lbl">sc.</span> im Mochuda] occ lubair . . . deichneabar ar 
secht cedaibh dō isin mancine, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BNn&#xC9;" title="eDIL Bibliography">BNnÉ</strong></span> 300 § 1.
          
         <span class="trans">The <span class="def">monastic</span> 
<span class="def">profession</span>, <span class="def">monasticism</span>:</span> fear ro athnuigh manchine ┐ canonach[a] riagulla hecailsi nErenn (of St. Malachy), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CS" title="eDIL Bibliography">CS</strong></span> 346.9
        . 
<span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Lism.L." title="eDIL Bibliography">Lism. L.</strong></span> 3492
         above.</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">service</span> due to an <span class="def">abbot</span> or <span class="def">monastery</span> from a <span class="def">layman</span>
        </span>, <span class="lbl">esp.</span> 
of tribute in kind, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>, apparently = <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=2 mainches">2 mainches</a></span>. dobéram ar 
mainchine ┐ ar bhfoghnamh duit (said to Ciarán), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Lism.L." title="eDIL Bibliography">Lism. L.</strong></span> 
4233
        , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          4246
        . dobéra cach manchaine a chlain[n]e . . . dō 
<span class="ediltr">every one shall give the service of his children . . . to him</span> (<span class="lbl">sc.</span> 
Colmán), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BColm." title="eDIL Bibliography">BColm.</strong></span> 12.25
        . 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Trip." title="eDIL Bibliography">Trip.</strong></span> 558.27
        . 
          [a] mainchene féin . . . 
┐ a meic ┐ a oa ┐ a iarmoa co brath do Colum Cille ┐ do 
mhuinnter Daire, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AU" title="eDIL Bibliography">AU</strong></span> ii 188.15
          
        , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AFM" title="eDIL Bibliography">AFM</strong></span> iii 34.5
        ; 
          ii 714.7
        . dorat 
C. in mag ┐ a manchine do Chaillin `<span class="quote">the plain and its profits</span>' 
(? <span class="ediltr">his service</span>), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Fen." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fen.</strong></span> 180.8
        . dorat a mallachtain don ti . . . 
doberadh a mhancine o Chiaran <span class="ediltr">whosoever should take away</span> 
(<span class="ediltr">withhold) his dues from [the community of ] C.</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xxvi 370.29
        , 
<span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ACL" title="eDIL Bibliography">ACL</strong></span> iii 226.3
        . In 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Acall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Acall.</strong></span> 3506
        , 
          3707
        , 
          5900
        , 
          6650
         mainchine 
is the reading of F. for maicne of text (see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=maicne">maicne</a></span>).</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">c</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">service</span> <span class="def">due</span> from a <span class="def">tenant</span> or <span class="def">clansman</span> to his chief in return 
for stock</span>; acc. to O'Don. Sup. <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">service</span> rendered by <span class="def">manual</span> 
<span class="def">labour</span>
        </span> (as though &lt; Lat. <span class="frn ">manus</span>?), a restriction not borne out 
by texts. 
          cia measom do cāin tsaorraith? Manchuine ┐ 
ureirge, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> ii 194.2
          
         (glossed 
          .i. fer cacha somhuine do denam 
a dūine no a meithle . . . no a sluaigedh lais, 
            <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 9
          
        , <span class="lbl">i.e.</span> the 
tenant is bound to build, reap, and fight for his chief). 
          dligidh 
in flaith ceilsine . . . ar dē mīs fri manchuine, 
            ii 258.1
          
        5 (acc. to 

          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 31 Comm.
         for every heifer received as stock the tenant had 
to furnish a man for military service for two months in the 
year). 
          cōir mancuine fri sloiged fri dunad fri gell fri dail fri 
fuba fri ruba fri fognam do Dia, 
            iii 22.4
          
         (where the term 
comprehends every kind of service). See 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MannersandCustoms" title="eDIL Bibliography">Manners and Customs</strong></span> I pp. cxi-cxii
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">d</span>) <span class="trans">hence in wider sense <span class="def">service</span>, <span class="def">attendance</span> (on a person, in 
a <span class="def">calling</span>):</span> ri ┐ brethem ┐ dias i manchuine (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> the king 
travels accompanied by a brehon and two attendants), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> 
iv 334.23 Comm.
         
          scor fir mancuine (.i. in fer doni in m.<span class="oRef">¤</span> oibre), 

            v 490.2
          
        , 
          23
        . lucht manchuine <span class="ediltr">servants or attendants</span> (of a 
chief), 
          iii 510.3
        . In 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OMulc." title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Mulc.</strong></span> 865
         manchaine prob. = <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">attendance</span> or <span class="def">following</span> allotted to a `file' acc. to his grade</span>. lanfilidecht cach fir dib ┐ ceard manchani la cach fir dib (of the 
Fianna), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> viii 561.7
        , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          i 472.9
        . lēigis leabaid lāin mainc[h]ine rig Rōmān rūaid <span class="ediltr">he left a comfortable berth, the service 
of the strong king of the Romans</span> (of the Apostle Matthew) 

          x 46.18
        .</p>
