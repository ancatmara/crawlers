      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">in primary sense `a reunion', hence a <span class="def">popular</span> <span class="def">assembly</span>

or <span class="def">gathering</span>
        </span>, generally (though not exclusively) for games,

races, and similar contests, as distinguished from an `airecht'

or assembly for communal business; commonly transld.

`fair', though it does not seem to have been intended for com-

mercial purposes. The chief oenaige were held periodically at

fixed places. The word is often used of the place where the

assembly was held and hence figures in many place-names,

usually folld. by a gen. but occas. absol., <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> mod. Nenagh

(Co. Tipperary) &lt; an Aenach &lt; a n-oenach; fón Aenach,


          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AU" title="eDIL Bibliography">AU</strong></span> ii 376.15
         (= mod. Enagh. Co. Roscommon). See 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Hog.Onom." title="eDIL Bibliography">Hog. Onom.</strong></span> s.v. oínach
        .</p>
