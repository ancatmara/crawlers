<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="10554">1 com-</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 1 com- or <a class="reference" href="10554">dil.ie/10554</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2019</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                con-, con-gaib, cotam-, cot-, conid-, com, cum, comerchloithe, comimmaircide, com-imm-, comocus, com-imm-, coímm-, coímthecht, com, con, coindelg, congab, congnam, coingell, coinglecc, comglenn, cotarsnae, coitreb, cocad, cocur, coicéile, coicert, cutrummae, comchlante, cosnam, cob, cub, cobodail, cubaid, cubus, cobfolaid, cobfuil, cum, com, commess, co(i)m, combág, combáid, combráithres, combroid, commaídid, commám, congnam, cosnam, comnert, comgním, coicéile, comchéile, comlann, comlán, comlín, coimescar, com, cuitbiud, cuindchid, cuimrech, con-icc, com, can, com, comnart, com</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos prepositional" style="color: #ff8000">prepositional</span> <span class="pos prefix" style="color: #ff8000">prefix</span> in composition with verbs, nouns 
and adjj. 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> 502.4
      . Bergin, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xiv 153
      . 
In verbs in pretonic position con- (con-icc, con-gaib), with 
inf. pron. cotam-, cot-, conid-, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> 257
      -
        8
      , 

        503
      ). See also <span class="lbl">s.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=5 con-">5 con-</a></span>.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
Under the accent the treatment is as follows. Before 
vowels and r, l, n com, cum (under infl. of follg. sound) 
(comadas, comerchloithe, comimmaircide (earlier coímm- 
&lt; com-imm-, see below), comocus <span class="lbl">s.v.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=com(&#x1E1F;)ocus">com(ḟ)ocus</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=comr&#xE1;d">comrád</a></span>, 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=comlann">comlann</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=comnart">comnart</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=cumsanad">cumsanad</a></span>). com-imm- gives coímm- 
(
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> 112
      , 
        502
      ) (coímchloud, coímthecht <span class="lbl">s.v.</span> 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=coimitecht">coimitecht</a></span>). Before b com (combach), before d, g con 
(condalb, coindelg, congab, congnam. Note later lenition 
of g in coingell, coinglecc and the artificial comglenn in 
Ml.). Before t, c co, cu with voicing of follg. plosive (cotach, 
cotarsnae, coitreb, cocad, cocur, coicéile, coicert, cutrummae. 
Note the artificial comchlante in Sg.). Before s co (cosmail, 
cosnam). Before f (earlier *w) co (cóir), later cob cub 
(cobair, cobodail, cubaid, cubus, cobfolaid, cobfuil). Before 
m cu (cuman), later cum, com (cummasc, commess).</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
Later co(i)m is used as the general form before all initials 
(see exx. under coim-, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 com-">1 com-</a></span>), both the m and a follg. initial 
c p t d g r l n f s being lenited. Before b, m the older procedure is retained, mb ultimately falling together with mm 
to give m(m). But where the elements in a compd. remain 
clear and in late formations -mb- (-mmb-, mhmb-, -mbb- 
-mhbh-) is usually retained (combádud, combág, combáid, 
combráithres, combroid). So also somet. mhm (mhmh) 
for mm (commaídem, commaídid, commám).</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
In the early language the neutral quality of final -m was 
retained before palatals (comnart, congnam, cosnam, with 
re-composition comnert, comgním. <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> coicéile comchéile, 
comlann comlán comlín). Palatalization is later regular in 
this position (coimchrith coimescar). Where com was 
itself modified palatalization came in earlier (coicert, 
cuitbiud, cuindchid, cuimrech <span class="lbl">etc.</span>).</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
In old verbal compounds (con-dieig, con-icc) the semantic 
function of com can as a rule only be conjectured. In 
later formations with verbs (coimlenaid) and with adjj. 
com has the <span class="pos adverbial" style="color: #ff8000">adverbial</span> force of <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">together</span>, <span class="def">mutually</span>, <span class="def">equally</span>
      </span>, 
with nouns the <span class="pos adjectival" style="color: #ff8000">adjectival</span> force of <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">mutual</span>, <span class="def">equal</span>
      </span>. The last-mentioned are frequent in <span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> with adjectival function 
(comchenél), the type comnart <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">strong</span>
      </span> being rare. In all 
late compds. com may function as a mere intensive prefix 
<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">greatly</span>, <span class="def">very</span>, <span class="def">completely</span>, <span class="def">great</span>, <span class="def">complete</span> <span class="lbl">etc.</span>
      </span> Frequently 
the prefix serves merely to provide an alliterating word. 
For com with adjj. forming the equative see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=2 com-">2 com-</a></span>.</p>
