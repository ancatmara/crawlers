      <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">II</span> The combination mad-génair (<span class="per 3">3</span> <span class="number s.">s.</span> <span class="tns pret.">pret.</span> of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=gainithir">gainithir</a></span>) 
`<span class="quote">luckily was born</span>' comes in Mid.Ir. to mean <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">fortunate</span>, <span class="def">happy</span>
        </span> 
(for transition of meaning <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> Prol. 251
        , 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 90b12

and is used alike with a sing. or pl. subj., at first folld. by a 
rel. clause, later regarded as a pure adj. and used with copula 
or in neuter sense folld. by DO; rarely with neg.</p>
