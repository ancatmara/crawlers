    <p class="result">
      <span class="itype o">o</span>, <span class="pos npr." style="color: #ff8000">npr.</span> <span class="gen m.">m.</span> Also spelled Decull, Decell, 
Degell, Deigell; Deichell, Deighell. Possibly a cpd. of 
deg + gell, though in an old <span class="pos npr." style="color: #ff8000">npr.</span> like this one would expect 
rather †Dag-gell.—Oldest spelling Decull: <span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> 
        Decuill, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ardm." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ardm.</strong></span> 19 a 1
        
      : Cairell mac Ferguso maic Decuill; a Fergus 
mac Deichill is mentioned 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BB" title="eDIL Bibliography">BB</strong></span> 200 b 43
      ; 
        meic Decuil, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> ii 281. 14
        
      = 
        Decill, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ReevesAd." title="eDIL Bibliography">Reeves Ad.</strong></span> 247 <span class="lbl">n.f.</span>
        
       = Deigill 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BB" title="eDIL Bibliography">BB</strong></span> 212 b 24
      . For the broad -gg- <span class="lbl">cf.</span> -accai <span class="ediltr">sees</span> (beside 
-aicci). Mid.-Ir. Deichell and Deighill are, at least 
partly, to be judged as corrupt renderings of older 
Decell, Degell, which as a npr. had become obs. long 
before the Mid.-Ir. period. A genuine Ir. †Dechell or 
†Degell (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> Deghell) is not sufficiently supported.</p>
