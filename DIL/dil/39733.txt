<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="39733">tairléim</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. tairléim or <a class="reference" href="39733">dil.ie/39733</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                tairlimm, toirléime, tairling, tairlenga, tuir[r]ling</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">[<span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype n">n</span>, <span class="gen n.">n.</span>] <span class="pos vn." style="color: #ff8000">vn.</span> of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=do-airling">do-airling</a></span>. O. Ir. tairlimm. Later <span class="gen f.">f.</span>, 
<span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> 
        a thulach na toirléime
        , 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Miscell.Celt.Soc." title="eDIL Bibliography">Miscell. Celt. Soc.</strong></span> 412.z
        
      . In Mid. Ir. 
under the influence of the finite verb the form tairling 
<span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> tairlenga is common. Similarly <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=tarblaing">tarblaing</a></span>, <span class="lbl">q.v.</span> Mod. 
tuir[r]ling.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">Descending</span>, <span class="def">alighting</span>
      </span>.</p>
   
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) From horse or chariot: 
          fa 
thrí tairlimm do Díarmait co tanic Temair, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Trip." title="eDIL Bibliography">Trip.</strong></span> 88.17

          ro bíth in fer 
... oc t.<span class="oRef">¤</span> dó asa carput, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 9757
          
         = 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> vi 164.19
        . geiss di 
tochim cen tairlim `<span class="quote">to ride through it (Tailltiu) without 
alighting'</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> iv 154.126
        . a gabáil (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> a charm) 
rē ndul ar ech | ... is tiucfa slán re toirling <span class="ediltr">return to alight 
safely</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> x 345 § 7

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/DD&#xE1;na" title="eDIL Bibliography">DDána</strong></span> 46.5
          
        . 
          tuirling da n-eachuibh,
