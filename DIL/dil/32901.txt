<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="32901">3 ná, na</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 3 ná, na or <a class="reference" href="32901">dil.ie/32901</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                na, nád, an(n)á, an(n)a, arná, arna, con(n)á, cona</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos conj." style="color: #ff8000">conj.</span> introducing subord. neg. clause; in O.Ir. 
generally na, often alternating with nád (nad); folld. by ro, 
often nar (nár). When an inf. pron. follows, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=4 nach">4 nach</a></span> is used. 
Used in O. and Mid.Ir. in follg. cases:</p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) after the rel. adverbs amal, in tain, uair; after a n- <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">when</span>, <span class="def">while</span>
        </span> and after ara, co n-, introducing final or consecutive clauses (in these cases generally forming the compds. 
an(n)á, an(n)a; arná, arna; con(n)á, cona):</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
amal na fil i ndia (an)isiu <span class="ediltr">as this is not in God</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 14c24
        . 
amal naṅdéni mascul <span class="ediltr">as it does not make a masculine</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 
125a6
         (= nán?). in tan na hantai for cocertad Medba <span class="ediltr">since ye 
abide not by M.'s judgement</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/FB" title="eDIL Bibliography">FB</strong></span> 75.
         
          in tan toccraides don 
choimdid nech ┐ na denand a chomarli, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 4016.
          
         
          huare 
narbu derachtae, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 18d18
          
        . 
          uair ná dernsam cuibdi, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 
1853.
          
         an[n]ambaid buidig <span class="ediltr">when ye are not content</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 115b10
        . 

          annar-bu buidech som, 
            40d10
          
        . ánamtar tuidchissi (<span class="lbl">sic leg.</span>) 
 <span class="ediltr">when they were not led</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 6a13
         (&lt; a n-na-mbatar). See also 
arná, conná (under ar, co). Similarly after other conjunctions 
or rel. adverbs: daig na tísat <span class="ediltr">that they may not come</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 1013.
         

          o ro bitis . . . oc denum edpart do ┐ na tabrad freccra doib, 

            1958
          
        . After in (interrog.), nád is generally used in Glosses; 
but <span class="lbl">cf.</span> innabet o nach ainmmdiu <span class="ediltr">whether they are not from any 
nominal form</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 56b1
        . ina raibi Gaedelg resiu ro tobaidhe? <span class="ediltr">did not Gaelic exist?</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Auraic." title="eDIL Bibliography">Auraic.</strong></span> 13.
        
      </p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
Occas. with inf. pron.: arnam-tomnad námmin duine <span class="ediltr">that he should not suppose me not to be a human being</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 
17d23
        . hore nán rairigsiur <span class="ediltr">because I had not perceived it</span> (<span class="lbl">sc.</span> 
peccad), 
          3c26
        . huare nand chumgat <span class="ediltr">because they cannot do it</span>, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 94b3
        . aircein nant ro chomairleic som dunni <span class="ediltr">as long as he 
did not permit it</span>, 
          53d9
        . <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 3d13
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) after cid (ar) <span class="ediltr">why?</span> cid arnabu son inchoissised longud?
        <span class="ediltr">why was it not a word to express devouring?</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 56a13
        . 
          cid arna 
techta in psalm-sa titul?, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Hib.Min." title="eDIL Bibliography">Hib. Min.</strong></span> 11.365
          
        . cid arna toet?
        
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 1218.
         
          cid ná tabraid in cauradmir?, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/FB" title="eDIL Bibliography">FB</strong></span> 72.
          
         cid nab sin 
Fedelm-sa . . . cichsed ría cach mnaí? <span class="ediltr">why should it not be F.</span>, 
          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 22
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 8296
        , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          8318
        ).</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">c</span>) introducing a noun-clause, either expanding an idea 
implicit in a foregoing noun or pron. or dependent on a vb. 
of feeling, perceiving, saying: co n-amairis na-nda-tiberad 
dia doib <span class="ediltr">that God would not give it to them</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 97d10
         (with inf. 
pron.). is éiseo óen-ni moedim-se . . . na rag-sa fessin <span class="ediltr">that I 
will not go</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 1777.
         ro fail a mor-abba dam-sa sain . . . na 
fetar, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> <span class="ediltr">I have good reason for that, (namely) that I know not</span>, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CRR" title="eDIL Bibliography">CRR</strong></span> 3.
         o atchuala M. . . . na cindte na berdais breith fair
        <span class="ediltr">their determination not to pass judgement on him</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 611.
         

          issed is cóir dúin na ragbam accaind na hindmusa, 
            6123
          
        . 
          is 
aire sin nara chóir duit-siu tiachtain, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 3527
          
         (na rachóir,

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 17d23

          dar lim-sa na fail

         Esp. in reported speech: asberad som na mbu tressa dia 
hirusalem <span class="ediltr">that the God of Jerusalem was not stronger</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 53d6
        . 
atberim frib . . . na híb don chenel fhina-sa <span class="ediltr">that I will not 
drink</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 5055.
         
          asbert C. nárbu santach fair,

          a[t]bert 
nár mó a olc dhó-som,

         By extension, after a vb. of

          ro aitchiset na firu . . . naro indistis [na scéla], 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span>

      </p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
For the copula-form nár in answering a question in the 
negative, see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=3 n&#xE1;d">3 nád</a></span>.</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">d</span>) rarely introducing a final or consecutive clause (instead 
of arná, conná): tuargaib leiss . . . é go mbad ra áth a túaid ra

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 3967.

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/FB" title="eDIL Bibliography">FB</strong></span> 27.

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 6020

      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">e</span>) irregular in follg. exx.: (introducing a main clause) 
          ro
