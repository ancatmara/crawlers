<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="32580">mormaer</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. mormaer or <a class="reference" href="32580">dil.ie/32580</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2013</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                mórmaer, mormoir, mormair</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   


    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype o">o</span>, <span class="gen m.">m.</span>, Irish form of a Scottish word, probably of 
indigenous (? Pictish) origin, the title given to the <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">custodian</span> 
or <span class="def">defender</span> of the <span class="def">Scottish coast</span>
      </span>. Acc. to a note communicated by A. O. Anderson, the Scottish form seems to have 
been <span class="frn ">morbhar (-mhar)</span>, the first syll. being = <span class="frn ">mor</span> `<span class="ediltr">sea</span>'; <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=mod">mod</a></span>. Scotch Gaelic <span class="frn ">morair</span> `<span class="ediltr">lord</span>', and follg. exx.: <span class="frn ">ingen 
mhorbair Moireogh</span> `<span class="quote">of the Earl of Moray</span>', 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Rel.Celt." title="eDIL Bibliography">Rel. Celt.</strong></span> ii 164.13
      . 
<span class="frn ">an M[h]orbhairn is magh Muile, | ar morbhair-ne a bfer faire</span>, 
Elegy on Angus MacDonald, Lord MacDonnell and Aros 
(†1680), where <span class="frn ">Morbhairn</span> `<span class="ediltr">sea-gap</span>' = <span class="ediltr">Morvern</span>. In Irish 
lit. the first syll. mor- appears at first as short; the second 
part was no doubt equated with <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=maer">maer</a></span> (<span class="lbl">q.v.</span>) and later the word 
was perhaps regarded as = mórmaer `<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">great</span> <span class="def">steward</span>
      </span>' (the usual 
translation). 
        mormoér Búchan, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Goid." title="eDIL Bibliography">Goid.</strong></span> 108.19
        
      . 
        ba hé robo

        bennact . . . ar cec mormar
