<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="14270">Dáire</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. Dáire or <a class="reference" href="14270">dil.ie/14270</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2019</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                Daire, Daire, Dare, Dáire, Dáire, Daire, Dáre, Dáre, Dáre, Dáre, Dári, Dári, Dari, Daire, Dairi, Dairi, Dare, Dari, Dáire, Daire, Daire, Dairi, Dáire, Dáire, Dáre, Daire, Dairi, Dáire</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   


    <p class="result">
      <span class="itype io">io</span>, <span class="pos npr." style="color: #ff8000">npr.</span> <span class="gen m">m</span>, with long <i>ā</i> and slender <i>r</i>, not to be 
confused with Darae of different origin. It rhymes with 

        gáire, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CRR" title="eDIL Bibliography">CRR</strong></span> 6. 1
        
      ; with 
        báire, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 158 a 24
        
      : sáiset a mbáre 
dia brath: co tarb nDaire meic Fiachrach; with 
        áine, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> 
iii 41 st 1
        
      ; with 
        cnámi, 
          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 43 st 13
        
      ; with 
        náire, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> 
iii 134. 12
        
      ; with 
        sále, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Men." title="eDIL Bibliography">Men.</strong></span> 24. 22
        
      . There is no genuine 
<span class="pos npr." style="color: #ff8000">npr.</span> Daire with short <span class="ediltr">a</span>. Dáire went early out of use as a 
npr. In late MSS it is usually written Daire with un- 
accented a. Where Daire occurs in verse in connection with 
a npr. rhyming with a short syllable, it is the <span class="pos n.l." style="color: #ff8000">n.l.</span> Daire, 
as 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MacCarthy" title="eDIL Bibliography">MacCarthy</strong></span> 430 st 3
      : Domnall Daire (: bladaide). The writing 
Dare is sometimes ambiguous as it may render Darae as 
well as Dáire. Dáire is the right form for the following npr.</p>
