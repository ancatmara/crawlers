    <p class="result"> 
ORIGIN: <span class="case Gen.">Gen.</span> dechnada (never dechanta) points to a 
cpd.; <span class="lbl">cf.</span> the <span class="itype u">u</span>- stems cétnad, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=s&#xE9;tnad">sétnad</a></span> (occasionally eclipsing 
like dechnad: 
        cétnad nimrinn, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> iii 53 § 97 n. 1
        
      . 
        c. naisi, 
          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 
§ 98
        
      . 
        setnad mbecc, 
          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 20. 13
        
      ). The original meaning of 
dechnad being obscure, no certain etymology can yet be 
given. Possibly a cpd. of deach `<span class="quote">verse-foot</span>, <span class="lbl">&amp;c.</span>' with nath 
.i. ainm coitchenn dona huilib aistib, Corm. (<span class="itype u">u</span>- stem: 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 
386 a 8
      . 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BB" title="eDIL Bibliography">BB</strong></span> 302 b 4
      . 
        303 b 42
      , and probably <span class="gen masc.">masc.</span>). Dech- cpd. form of deäch as ern- of iarn.</p>
