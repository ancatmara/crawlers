<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="3240">an-</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. an- or <a class="reference" href="3240">dil.ie/3240</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2019</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                an-, am-, in-, im-, an-, in-, an-, é-, an-, am-, an-, am-, an-, am-, am-, am-, an-, an(n)-, an-, n-</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
  
  
    <p class="result">neg. <span class="pos pref." style="color: #ff8000">pref.</span> (IE vocalic *<span class="frn ">n-</span>), appearing as an-, am-, in-, 
im-(?), é- before different initials. See 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> § 869
ff.
      , Dillon, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Phil.Soc.Trans." title="eDIL Bibliography">Phil. Soc. Trans.</strong></span> 1944, 94 ff.
       Even in O. Ir. the 
original phonetic distribution has been disturbed by analogical formations, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> an- for in- before d, g (andach, 
anglan); an- for é- before c in nouns (aincél, aincride); 
both an- and am- before vowels (anecne, amulach). This 
becomes more widespread in later lang., where in addition 
to regular formations of the type éccóir, étrócar we find the 
unphonetic édaingen, édána, édlúith, édóchas, édóig, 
édomain, éiderb, éidrímtha (with extension of -d-), éigrinn, 
éscus (scís), étriad (anríad, <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>): énirt occurs in Wb. The 
form in-, regular before d-, g- (indles, with recomposition 
indíles, ingnad, also ingnáth) appears later in inḟebda, 
inḟoltach (= anfoltach), inísel, inmall, inmálla, innáire, 
intinne (teinne), inúath; note extension of -d- in ind(ḟ)úar, 
while imbil is a possible ex. of im- before b- (see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> p. 543
      ).</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
The forms an- and am- eventually become for all intents 
and purposes interchangeable in meaning (a), and many 
doublets arise (aindeóin, aimdeóin; anglan, amglan). But 
there are no exx. of an- before p- nor of am- before m-, s-, 
and t- (before i- only in amaires, before p- only in amprom, 
where prom is a loanword). Acc. to 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> p. 104
       
the neutral quality of the nasal seems to have been preserved 
before palatal vowels in the early language (note also 
amraid, anbal, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>). The later tendency is for m and n 
to be palatalized before front vowels and palatal consonants. 
<span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
        ainfírinne, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 2a17
        
      . The form am- presumably lenited 
l, r, n. In the case of initial b- the group -mb- later becomes 
-m(m)- (aimbrit aimrid). In its later extension am- lenites 
all other consonants before which it occurs. The original 
mutation after an- in the case of f, g(?), c was nasalization 
(anbal, anglan, aincride). In the later language an- lenites 
b c f g m (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainc(h)reitem">aincreitem</a></span> later ainchreitem, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>); t apparently 
remains unchanged (ainteist aintenn); an- before d gives 
an(n)-, this combination falling together with original an- 
before n- (aindeoin, -nn-; aindíaraid, -nn-; ainnert). In old 
compds. a long vowel in the second element is shortened 
(anbal, amraid with subsequent recomposition ainfíal, 
aimréid) (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> indles, ingnad above).</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
In frequent composition with nouns and adjj., and rarely 
with vbs. (ainlenaid, anadnaid, anaibsigid).</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
Though a few forms like aineóil <span class="ediltr">strange</span> occur fairly 
frequently these seem to be <span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> of the nouns rather than 
adjj. of the type sochineóil <span class="lbl">etc.</span> However, énirt is no doubt 
correctly given as adj. i-stem. The same may apply to the 
later aimnirt. See also <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aindeis">aindeis</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aind&#xED;les">aindíles</a></span> (see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aind&#xED;les">aindíles</a></span>), 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainf&#xE9;ich">ainféich</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ain&#xE9;itid">ainéitid</a></span>.</p>
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">not</span>, <span class="def">non-</span>, <span class="def">un-</span>
        </span> (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aimecna">aimecna</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aimles">aimles</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainbthen">ainbthen</a></span>, aincreitem, 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aindebaid">aindebaid</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainde&#xF3;in">aindeóin</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aind&#xED;bad">aindíbad</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aindliged">aindliged</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aine&#xF3;l">aineól</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainf&#xE9;ile">ainféile</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainfis">ainfis</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainteist">ainteist</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=am(a)ires">am(a)ires</a></span>; <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aimbil">aimbil</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aimdis">aimdis</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aimglicc">aimglicc</a></span>, 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainfesach">ainfesach</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=2 anglan">2 anglan</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=amraid">amraid</a></span> <span class="lbl">etc.</span> For exx. with é-, in- see 
Fasc. E, I).</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) The (later?) meaning <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">bad</span>, <span class="def">undesirable</span>, <span class="def">unsuitable</span>
        </span> (with 
nouns) is found <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> in <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainben">ainben</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainbreith">ainbreith</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aind&#xED;a">aindía</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aind&#xED;l">aindíl</a></span>, 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=anduine">anduine</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ain&#xE9;cht">ainécht</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 ainfine">1 ainfine</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=anflaith">anflaith</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=anfolad">anfolad</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aingn&#xED;m">aingním</a></span>, 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainimme">ainimme</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainm&#xED;an">ainmían</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 ainrecht">1 ainrecht</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainr&#xED;ad">ainríad</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainricht">ainricht</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainr&#xED;ge">ainríge</a></span>, 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aint&#xED;r">aintír</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=anchruth">anchruth</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=antocad">antocad</a></span>. Words with a(i)m- in this sense 
are less frequent (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aimb&#xE9;s">aimbés</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aimb&#xE9;scna">aimbéscna</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aimles">aimles</a></span>(?), <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aimricht">aimricht</a></span>; 
the adj. <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ain&#xED;armartach">ainíarmartach</a></span> is derived from a noun.</p>
    
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">c</span>) As intensive prefix <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">great</span>, <span class="def">very</span>
      </span> (see Dillon <span class="lbl">ad loc. cit.</span>). 
Though this meaning becomes common only in the later 
language there are some early formations; inglan is found in 
SR, ind(ḟ)úar, innáire in LL, ingarg in Todd Nenn., though 
inḟebda, iníchtar <span class="lbl">etc.</span>
       are late. The form. a(i)m- is not found 
with this meaning (aimlesc is for aidlesc), in which a(i)n- has 
the widest extension and is still current in the spoken language. 
The follg. are early exx.: anfann (usual from the mid. Ir. 
period on), <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=2 aingel">2 aingel</a></span> (Corm. Y), ainéitig (LL), ainecla (CCath.). 
The follg. are later: <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=anbrath">anbrath</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainching">ainching</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=and&#xE1;na">andána</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aind&#xED;chell">aindíchell</a></span>, 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aind&#xED;ummus">aindíummus</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=and&#xF3;chas">andóchas</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainfergach">ainfergach</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ainnert">ainnert</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aintreise">aintreise</a></span>. With late byform ana- cf. <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=anbroic">anbroic</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=anbroit">anbroit</a></span> etc. Some
