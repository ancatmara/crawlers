<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="15008">dedól</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. dedól or <a class="reference" href="15008">dil.ie/15008</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2013</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                dedól, degdóil, degail, deaghail</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   


    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype o">o</span> &amp; <span class="itype &#x101;">ā</span>. <span class="trans">The morning <span class="def">twilight</span>, the <span class="def">dawn</span>, <span class="def">daybreak</span>
      </span>. Phonet. deghoir, degdoil, <span class="lbl">&amp;c.</span> <span class="case d">d</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> 
        dedól, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 
136 b 1
        
      ; but already at the close of the 8th century 
also inflected as a stem in <span class="itype &#x101;">ā</span>, as shown by 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> 
July 26
      : hi paiss Iouiáni: cona chléir cáin glanóir 
tarmchruthud iar ndedóil (<span class="lbl">v.l.</span> ndeghoil): Issu i Sléib 
Thabóir; <span class="lbl">cf.</span> further the rhyme glédedóil: 
        Éremóin, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 
155 a 5
        
       (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> iii 262. 42
      ). 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BB" title="eDIL Bibliography">BB</strong></span> 36 b 48
      . 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AFM" title="eDIL Bibliography">AFM</strong></span> A.D. 940
      . 
degdóil: 
        senóir, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Add.30512" title="eDIL Bibliography">Add. 30. 512</strong></span>, 34 b 2
        
      . By native grammarians explained </p>
  
  
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) as a cpd. of dé- `two' + <span class="number dual">dual</span>: dedol 
(dedól YBL) .i. dédhual (dá dhual YBL) 
          .i. dual do aidche 
┐ dual do ló .i. soillse cumascda hó dhorchacht ┐ ó ṡoillsi, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm." title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm.</strong></span> <span class="lbl">s.v.</span>
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm.Y" title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm. Y</strong></span> 426
        ). deadhoil...ó dhédhual tig, 
O'Cl. (from Corm.). Lhuyd (from O'Cl.).</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">b</span>) as a cpd. 
of dé = dia + <span class="number dual">dual</span>: dedol .i. dia dhual .i. da ḟothadháil (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> 
fó-th.). 
          dá dagthodail do Dia. actáil ┐ teóir, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm." title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm.</strong></span> <span class="lbl">s.v.</span>
          
         </p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">c</span>) as identical with dedail: 
          deidoil .i. degail lái ┐ aidchi, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ACL" title="eDIL Bibliography">ACL</strong></span> iii 16 § 32
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span>, 523
        ). 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/P.OC." title="eDIL Bibliography">P. O'C.</strong></span>
         <span class="lbl">s.v.</span> Hence often 
so written: 
          degail, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celt.Rev." title="eDIL Bibliography">Celt. Rev.</strong></span> i 300
          
        . 
          deaghail, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MR" title="eDIL Bibliography">MR</strong></span> 112. 4
          
        
(B iv 1).—Wrong etymologies by Stokes <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm.Tr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm. Tr.</strong></span> vii
        , 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xxvii 88
        
        , see also <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> l 265
        , 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LEIA" title="eDIL Bibliography">LEIA</strong></span> D-36
        . 
          deid-oil, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ACL" title="eDIL Bibliography">ACL</strong></span> iii 16 § 32

 <span class="ediltr">at day-break</span> = issin d.: 
          dochuaid Loegaire ón deidoil dochum Temrach, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ACL" title="eDIL Bibliography">ACL</strong></span> <span class="lbl">l.c.</span>
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Trip." title="eDIL Bibliography">Trip.</strong></span> 52. 14
        ) 
          ised is dech do ṡenóir érge romoch ón degdóil, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Add.30512" title="eDIL Bibliography">Add. 30. 512</strong></span>, 34 b 2
          
        .</p>
