<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="18716">drinnrosc</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. drinnrosc or <a class="reference" href="18716">dil.ie/18716</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                drindrosc, drind, drindrosc</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   

    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> Also drindrosc <span class="itype o">o</span>, <span class="itype n.">n.</span> later <span class="gen m.">m.</span>? <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number p.">p.</span> usually -ruisc, 
-roisc, but 
        -rosci, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BDD" title="eDIL Bibliography">BDD</strong></span> § 8
        
       (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/YBL" title="eDIL Bibliography">YBL</strong></span> 91b
        
      ), 
        -roisci, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span>, 529
        
      . 
Synonymous with <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 indrosc">1 indrosc</a></span> and <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=rindrosc">rindrosc</a></span> (-ruisc?) (<span class="lbl">q.v.</span>) which 
sometimes appear as variant readings for it. <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">Wish</span>, <span class="def">request</span>, 
<span class="def">boon</span> demanded</span>, usually in return for quarter given; but in 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BDD" title="eDIL Bibliography">BDD</strong></span> § 8
       used of gifts demanded by a mother on the birth of 
a child from the father; in 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> iii 138.92
       (Expuls. of the 
Déisi) the terms rindruisc and indrosc apply to gifts claimed 
by a woman as her marriage-portion; <span class="lbl">cf.</span> also 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> viii 311.26
      , 
where an `indrosc' is asked for apparently without any 
equivalent boon. Almost always accompanied by numeral 
 trí (for an exception see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> viii 311
      ), though in some cases 
only one or two boons are subsequently specified. Acc. to 
Zimmer 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> i 94 n.
       lit. `rosc' <span class="ediltr">of contention</span>, drind being <span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> of 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 drenn">1 drenn</a></span> <span class="ediltr">quarrel</span>, rosc either <span class="ediltr">eye</span> or (more probably) <span class="ediltr">saying, 
utterance</span>; so also 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm." title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm.</strong></span> <span class="lbl">s.v.</span> indrosc
      . The form rindrosc (or 
rindruisc; only the <span class="number pl.">pl.</span> occurs), which is less freq. than the 
other two, may have arisen through association with rosc 
 <span class="ediltr">eye</span>, as though the meaning were apple of the eye. Possibly 
the orig. word was indrosc, drindrosc being due to assimilation of the numeral trí which nearly always preceded it (for 
an analogous case <span class="lbl">cf.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=drilind">drilind</a></span> above). Defined: 
        .i. itchi nó 
athchuingi, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span>, 649b
          
        
      ; .i. roga <span class="ediltr">choice</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 72.5
      . In all 
the follg. exx. except the last the boons are demanded in 
return for sparing life: anmain in anmain a Chuchulainn, or 
se. Tabar mo thrí drindrosc (drinnroisc, Eg.) 
        dam dano, ol C. 
Rotbiat, ol se feib dothaíset latt aináil, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/FB" title="eDIL Bibliography">FB</strong></span> § 87
        
       (three boons 
are then asked by C.; acc. to Henderson in his note <span class="lbl">ad loc.</span> 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ITS" title="eDIL Bibliography">ITS</strong></span> ii 109
       the last sentence of the quotation means that they 
were to be asked at one breath). 
        bās huassut, ol se. to trí 
drinnroisc (rindroisc, <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>) dait, al si, fep tissed liat anail (.i. 
do trí rogain ruisc feb conaitecht lat anail, <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> iii 252
§ 71
        
       (Tochm. Emire) (the wishes are not specified). 
        mo trí 
drinnruisc (indrosc, rindroisc, <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>) damsa, ol se. Rotbiad 
amoil notistais lat anail, or si, 
          254 § 76 
        
       (three boons are then 
asked). 
        co tartath a tri drinnroisc (indrosc, <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>) ndo, 
          251 § 70 
        
      
(three requests follow). 
        tabraid ar ndtri drinnrusc douinni, 
ol ind o[i]cc. Rob-bia, ol in triar ingen, acht nip innili, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBR" title="eDIL Bibliography">Táin Bó Reg.</strong></span> § 2
        
       Eg. (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> ii2 227
      ), where YBL reads: tabair mo tri 
lanḟocull dam-sa, ar M. Amal no naseca do thenga rodbia 
acht beatha, ar an ingen (no wishes specified in either text). 

        mo thrí drinnrosc (drinnruisc, Eg.) damsa, ol C. Rodbia, ol in 
ingen. Ar is ed rocinded dam inillius frim, ol C., .i. sídh ┐ córa 
do beth edraind ┐ do beith do aenmhnai ocum, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> vi 175.31
        
      . 

        mo trí drinnroisc (.i. roga), ol F. Rotbiah, ol int abac, acht 
[nach] ní bes ecmacht duind, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 72.5
        
       (only one boon is 
asked in sequel). batar he a tri drindrosci (drindruisc, St.) 

        forsin rig .i. altrom a maic eter [t]heora aicce, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BDD" title="eDIL Bibliography">BDD</strong></span> § 8
        
       (a
