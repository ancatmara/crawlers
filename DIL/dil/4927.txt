    <p class="result">(ad-tá) subst. <span class="pos vb." style="color: #ff8000">vb.</span> <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">is</span>
      </span>. The distinction betw. subst. vb. 
and copula is in the first place syntactical, and cases are 
found where there is no distinction of form. <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> the type, 
common in ACC: boe sab <span class="ediltr">he was a leader</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ACC" title="eDIL Bibliography">ACC</strong></span>
         26
      . See further 
below, and the discussion by Bergin, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xii 208
      -
        211
       (cia 
taísiu, cia bé, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>), who refers to cases like: 
        is uera pictura 
ro bai sin, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> ii 6.32
        
       as emphatic. See also 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xvi 46
      , 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> 475
      . 
        For nimtha laám, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 12a21
        
      , 
        nímptha 
fírion, 
          8d24
        
       (copula ?) see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> 487
      . Note also 
the form of the pron. in (later): 
        matait tall iat, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iv
        
       

        86.8 Comm.

        atá ar conair é, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ACL" title="eDIL Bibliography">ACL</strong></span> iii 216.z

cecinit, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xvii 175.14

    <p class="result"> 
Apart from the ordinary <span class="mood indic.">indic.</span> <span class="tns pres.">pres.</span> all forms of subst. 
vb. are from a root beginning with b-. In <span class="mood indic.">indic.</span> <span class="tns pres.">pres.</span> the 
stem tá (in absol. position attá) is used in <span style="color: #ff8000">non-rel.</span> sentences 
without conj. particle, after conj. particles with inf. pron. 
dat. and when the conj. particle consists of a preposition 
and <span style="color: #ff8000">rel.</span> (s)a n-. Absolute inflexion of tá- is found in oldaas, 
indaas, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> <span class="ediltr">than (is</span>), in meaning <span class="ediltr">is angry</span>, and with follg. 
suff. pron. The uninflected form fil (with accus.) is used 
after conj. particles where no dat. inf. pron. is present, 
in leniting <span style="color: #ff8000">rel.</span> clauses and often in answers to questions 
containing subst. vb., and in archaic texts in absol. position 
in meaning <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">there is</span>
      </span>. For fuller details see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> 
476
      -
        483
       and exx. below. See also 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> ii 419 ff.
      , Strachan, 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Phil.Soc.Trans." title="eDIL Bibliography">Phil. Soc. Trans.</strong></span> 1899, 1
      -
        82
      , Ó Máille, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> vi 1 ff.
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;tudesCelt." title="eDIL Bibliography">Études Celt.</strong></span> xxxiv 143-44
      . Forms 
of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=gaibid">gaibid</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=do-coissin">do-coissin</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=d&#xED;chsnigidir">díchsnigidir</a></span> and <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 ad-cumaing">1 ad-cumaing</a></span> also have 
the force of the subst. vb. in certain uses, see separate 
entries.</p>
