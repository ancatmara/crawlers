<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="19544">écht</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. écht or <a class="reference" href="19544">dil.ie/19544</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2013</span><br/></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   


    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype u">u</span>, <span class="gen n.">n.</span>? (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ToddLect." title="eDIL Bibliography">Todd Lect.</strong></span> iv 205
      ), later <span class="gen m.">m.</span> The original 
meaning seems to be <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">slaughter</span>, <span class="def">slaying</span>
      </span>, and this is 
generally implied, under all extensions of meaning.</p>
  
  
     


      <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">I</span> <span class="trans">A <span class="def">slaying</span>, <span class="def">slaughter</span>
        </span>; usually of slaying an enemy in 
battle, but also in the senses of <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">murder</span>, <span class="def">manslaughter</span>
        </span> (with 
gen. of agent or obj.): écht Maind <span class="ediltr">the slaying of Mand</span> 
(obj. gen.) 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-I1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-I¹</strong></span> 2181
        = 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> iv 278
        . 
          ferais ēcht 
ndochla ndobail | guin Fiachach, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-I1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-I¹</strong></span> 2068
          
        = 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 2784
        . 
naco ndernat echt fon innas(s)a nidat tualngi comram <span class="ediltr">until 
they accomplish such a killing as this</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 123b51
         (= 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Death-talesoftheUlsterheroes" title="eDIL Bibliography">Death-tales of the Ulster heroes</strong></span> 4
        ). 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Anecd." title="eDIL Bibliography">Anecd.</strong></span> i 13.5
        . do commaoidhimh an echda mhoir 
dorinde (<span class="lbl">viz.</span> the slaying of Fer Diad) 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> x 296 y
        . 
          is 
mór in t-écht doringni rí L., bás mo dam ingen do 
thuidecht trina cheilg, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 295a23
          
         = 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xiii 38.18
        . 
          saebécht doróni L. for mnai Conchobair, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> iv 32
          
        .

the Leinstermen</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 48b7
        . 
          dorigne P. echta ar na 
Tiauandaib,

        . nir bo echt cen aithi <span class="ediltr">not 
an unavenged slaying</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TTebe" title="eDIL Bibliography">TTebe</strong></span> 3523
        . mairg cathraig i 
ndernad in t-échtsa (<span class="lbl">viz.</span> the Crucifixion) 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 3306
        . lucht 
an echta <span class="ediltr">those who had wrought the murder</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/KMMisc." title="eDIL Bibliography">KMMisc.</strong></span> 
313.13
        . 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/D&#xE1;nD&#xE9;" title="eDIL Bibliography">Dán Dé</strong></span> xxvi 33
        . tír an echta `<span class="quote">the land on 
which the crime had been committed</span>' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BNn&#xC9;" title="eDIL Bibliography">BNnÉ</strong></span> 313.5
        . 
adchondairc A. na tigri...a haithli na n-echt sin do 
denam daib (after the tigers had killed a charioteer and his 
steeds), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TTebe" title="eDIL Bibliography">TTebe</strong></span> 2780
        . 
          trésna háraibh ┐ trésna héachtaibh 
do-bhéaradh ar a easgcáirdibh, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TSh." title="eDIL Bibliography">TSh.</strong></span> 2763
          
        . Here may 
belong: 
          ech cumal echta, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 414.21

          .i. 
go n-a duine, 
            416.1
          
        , possibly = gona duini <span class="ediltr">of slaying a 
man</span>; then ech c.e. might mean <span class="trans">a <span class="def">horse</span> worth a <span class="def">blood price</span>
        </span> (?). 

          aircet échta, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Acall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Acall.</strong></span> 789
          
         (`<span class="quote">silver taken as spoil from 
the slain</span>' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SG" title="eDIL Bibliography">SG</strong></span> ii 120
        ) <span class="lbl">cf.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 echta">1 echta</a></span>. Attrib. <span class="case g">g</span>
        <span class="number s.">s.</span>: aire échta 
<span class="ediltr">noble of death-deeds</span>, the designation of an officer entrusted 
with the duty of exacting blood-vengeance, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iv 322.
20
        . See 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PRIA" title="eDIL Bibliography">PRIA</strong></span> xxxvi C 16 § 109

          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 269.31
        ). 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xv 319

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iii 82.6

<span class="ediltr">clouds of (i.e. presaging) death</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ir.Monthly" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ir. Monthly</strong></span> 1920, 108

      <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">II</span> By extension <span class="trans">a <span class="def">slain</span> <span class="def">person</span>
        </span>, occasionally somewhat 
in the sense of Engl. <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">casualty</span>, <span class="def">loss</span> (<i>in battle</i>)</span> but some of the follg. exx. may belong to <span class="edilb">I</span> (see <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SGS" title="eDIL Bibliography">SGS</strong></span> iv 94-95
        ); <span class="lbl">cf.</span> eacht 
<span class="trans">
          <span class="def">pity</span>, <span class="def">grief</span>, <span class="def">sorrow</span>, <span class="def">mourning</span>
        </span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/P.OC." title="eDIL Bibliography">P. O'C.</strong></span>
        : ard cach n-écht dorochair and <span class="ediltr">every (warrior) slain there</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> iv 44
        . rob 
echt mór fer dibhsin gérbhó maith an dias n-aile (of three 
slain warriors) 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AFM" title="eDIL Bibliography">AFM</strong></span> v 1636.6
        . dorónadh écht mór annsin ō 
Ghallaibh .i. Pattraiccín C. <span class="ediltr">there was a serious loss on the 
English side, viz. P.C.</span> 
          1646 y
        . dorochrator échta móra ó 
Máguidhir <span class="ediltr">M. suffered some serious losses</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/HughRoe2" title="eDIL Bibliography">Hugh Roe²</strong></span> 30.
27 (17a)
        . 
          ni hinnister echt oirdearc diobh do thuitim, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AFM" title="eDIL Bibliography">AFM</strong></span> 
vi 2106.8
          
        . 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/L.Chl.S." title="eDIL Bibliography">L. Chl. S.</strong></span> § 54
        . echta a n-airech <span class="ediltr">the 
slain of their leaders</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Leb.Gab.i" title="eDIL Bibliography">Leb. Gab.(i)</strong></span> i 270
        . 
          nír bhó hionairimh

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AFM" title="eDIL Bibliography">AFM</strong></span> vi 1936.12
          
        . 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MarcoP." title="eDIL Bibliography">Marco P.</strong></span> 63
        .

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Miscell.Celt.Soc." title="eDIL Bibliography">Miscell. Celt. Soc.</strong></span> 352
          
        . 
          ba

bhfaoínlighibh, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AFM" title="eDIL Bibliography">AFM</strong></span> v 1278.11
          
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
The use of écht may be anticipatory: dús in tetarthad 
écht n-amra d'Ultaib do marbad <span class="ediltr">if he could succeed in 
slaying a famous champion of the U.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 124a8
         = 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Death-talesoftheUlsterheroes" title="eDIL Bibliography">Death-tales of the Ulster heroes</strong></span> 4
         (rendered: 
          i ndóigh éachta do dhéanamh ar 
Ulltachaibh, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Keat." title="eDIL Bibliography">Keat.</strong></span> ii 3106
          
        ). is amlaid forcoemnacair cach 
oen for erchomhair a échta <span class="ediltr">facing the man he was to slay</span> 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xi 61.17
        . 
          ni fiu lium echt anuasal nā gillanrach 
do marbadh, 
            vi 278.30
          
        . 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ML" title="eDIL Bibliography">ML</strong></span> 100.12
        . 
          gu nderna mé écht 
Ídhail, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span>, Decl. ex. 426
          
        . Probably of this type is: demin 
leis gia no thachrad écht dó nā lécfadh C. dó a dénam <span class="ediltr">even 
though he should get the chance of slaying (a champion</span>) 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 1180
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">III</span> <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">Exploit</span>, <span class="def">prowess</span>, <span class="def">deed</span> of <span class="def">valour</span>
        </span> (
          echt <span class="frn ">ab actu</span>
          , 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OMulc." title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Mulc.</strong></span> 365
