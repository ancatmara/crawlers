<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="30674">lór</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. lór or <a class="reference" href="30674">dil.ie/30674</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2013</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                loor, lour, lora, leór, lour</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos adj" style="color: #ff8000">adj</span> 
      <span class="itype o,-&#x101;">o, ā</span> (see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> 125
      ). The earlier non-dissimilated form: roar nadma .i. imbed nadma '<span class="quote">sufficiency of binding, i.e. abundance of binding</span>' 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CIH" title="eDIL Bibliography">CIH</strong></span> iii 776.8
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xxxvii 191
      . In O. Ir. Glosses always 
spelled loor, lour except <span class="number pl.">pl.</span> 
        lora, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 3a6
        
      . Later freq. leór. 
<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">enough</span>, <span class="def">sufficient</span>
      </span>; of people, objects etc. in a vague laudatory 
sense, perh. <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">great</span>, <span class="def">ample</span>; also <span class="def">adequate</span>, <span class="def">competent</span>
      </span>.</p>
