      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">d</span>) In Laws of the respite or period of stay during which a 
distrained article (see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=athgab&#xE1;il">athgabáil</a></span>) remained in the hands of the 
defendant (debtor), who in turn gave a `gell' as guarantee of 
later satisfaction of the claim. This seems to have been the 
procedure in the `athgabál iar fut' (<span class="ediltr">distress with time</span>). In 
the `athgabál tulla' (<span class="ediltr">immediate distress</span>) the article distrained 
was immediately transferred to the custody of the claimant 
instead of being left for its `anad' with the defendant 
(Plummer MS notes). See 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ODon.Suppl." title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Don. Suppl.</strong></span>,  
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 208
