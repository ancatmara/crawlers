    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos pron" style="color: #ff8000">pron</span> 
      -dos-, -dus-; -tas-, -tos-, -tus-; -as-, -os-, -us- (in 
connection with co n-, dia n-, <span class="lbl">&amp;c.</span>) MID.-IR. form of the 
infixed pronoun of the <span class="per 3">3</span> <span class="number sg.">sg.</span> and <span class="number pl.">pl.</span>; v. Strachan 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> i 
174
       <span class="lbl">sq.</span> The form entered the literature in the early 10th 
century. Earliest occurrences in Hy. Broc., SR, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AU" title="eDIL Bibliography">AU</strong></span> 
(A.D. 932). Where -da(s)- in early Mid.-Ir. instances is 
followed by a verb in s- it is uncertain whether the copyist 
meant -da- or -das-.</p>
