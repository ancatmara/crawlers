    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos pron" style="color: #ff8000">pron</span> Acc. to 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> 311
       orig. a Latinism in 
which cechtar de was treated as one word in order to translate 
<span class="ediltr">uterque</span> (the phr. cechtar de in ordinary use is somet. treated 
scribally as one word, see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 15d9
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 101c4
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 37b17
      ).
