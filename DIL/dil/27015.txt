    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype i&#x101;">iā</span>, <span class="gen f.">f.</span> <span class="trans">(lit. <span class="def">after-rising</span>) the name of one of the 
<span class="def">canonical</span> <span class="def">hours</span>
      </span>. See Best, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/KMMisc." title="eDIL Bibliography">KMMisc.</strong></span> 162 ff
      . 
        íarmérgi,

Decl. § 3.30
        
      .</p>

      <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">II</span> The time when this office was performed, <span class="lbl">i.e.</span> <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">midnight</span>, 
<span class="def">daybreak</span>
        </span> (the confusion here is due to the fact that Matins, 
though properly the midnight office, came in the late 
Middle Ages, to be celebrated immediately before lauds, 
the first of the day-hours of the Church. See Gougaud, 
<span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Gougaud" title="eDIL Bibliography">Christianity in Celtic Lands</strong></span>, p. 333
        ) : guth circe fráoich an 
tsléibhe | do cluinsin im iarmhéirghe `<span class="quote">to hear at dawn</span>,' 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BS" title="eDIL Bibliography">BS</strong></span> 152.12
        . o thá iarmeirge co muichdedoil `<span class="quote">from 
midnight to day-break</span>,' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AFM" title="eDIL Bibliography">AFM</strong></span> iii 10.2
        . 
          leasc leam éirghe san 
íarmhérghe | d'éis chogalta, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Decl. ex. 1190.
          
         
          ésca ní 
faicfidher, | ūathmar an īarmēirge, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> viii 212.18
