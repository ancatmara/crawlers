    <p class="result"> 
The presumed distinction between ar with accus. when 
motion is implied and with dat. when no motion is implied 
 
(
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Zeuss" title="eDIL Bibliography">Zeuss</strong></span> 622
      ; with reservations 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Vendryes" title="eDIL Bibliography">Vendryes Gramm.</strong></span> § 283
      ; see 
also 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> ii 73
      -
        74
      , Williams <span class="lbl">loc. cit.</span>) appears most clearly 
in the stereotyped phrases ar chenn <span class="ediltr">towards</span>, ar chiunn 
<span class="ediltr">ahead of, before</span> (see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 cenn">1 cenn</a></span>): ar beolu <span class="ediltr">towards</span>, ar bélaib <span class="ediltr">before, 
in front of</span> (see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=b&#xE9;l">bél</a></span>). The dat. is usual in ar cach láu <span class="ediltr">daily</span> 
(see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=l&#xE1;">lá</a></span>) and in ar thosuch (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 203a7
      , see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=tosach">tosach</a></span>). Usage 
is less clear outside fixed phrases, and that of the various 
collections of O. Ir. glosses differs (the available material 
is also much reduced by the frequent identity of form of 
<span class="case a">a</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> and <span class="case d">d</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> of nouns). The accus. is regular in Wb. in senses 
of taking away <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">from</span>
      </span> and counting <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">as</span>
      </span>, and also in glosses 
on Lat. <span class="frn ">ob</span>, the dat. where the meaning is <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">with regard to</span>, 
<span class="def">instead of</span>
      </span> and in glosses on Lat. <span class="frn ">super</span>. Both cases are 
found where the meaning is <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">for the sake of</span>, <span class="def">because of</span>
      </span>. In 
Ml. accus. is constant only in set phrases. The dat. is usual 
in meaning counts <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">as</span>
      </span>, do-beir ar (for?) <span class="ediltr">calls</span> and in glosses 
on Lat. dat. without preposition (ar gentib gl. gentibus, 

        78a12
      , 
        89c2
      ) and where a Lat. compd. noun is treated as 
two words (arintinmlegun gl. promulgatione, 
        71c18
      ). In 
general use the meanings saving <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">from</span>, <span class="def">instead of</span>, <span class="def">in meaning 
of</span>, <span class="def">in return for</span>, <span class="def">on account of</span>, <span class="def">because of</span>, <span class="def">for the sake of</span>, 
<span class="def">for the purpose of</span>
      </span> are all found both with accus. and dat. 
In Sg. the meanings <span class="ediltr">instead of</span> and <span class="ediltr">on account of</span> are found 
with both cases. See also 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Introd. § 130
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Bard.Synt.Tr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Bard. Synt. Tr.</strong></span> 2
      -
        4
      , 
        17
      , 
        20
       (where detailed rules are given for accus. and 
dat. both with simple ar and for compd. preps. such as ar 
aghaidh, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>).</p>
