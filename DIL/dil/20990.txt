    <p class="result">
      cadéin, céin, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> <span class="pos adj." style="color: #ff8000">adj.</span> and <span class="pos pron." style="color: #ff8000">pron.</span>; fad— (later 
fod—, bad—, bod—, bud—) is taken by 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span>(i 271
      , 
        ii 153
      ) 
to be an element of the verb to be. For cad— see below. 
The stress falls on the second syllable in the forms beginning 
with fad— (fod—, bod— <span class="lbl">etc.</span>), cad—; in fan—, can— the position 
of the stress is undetermined. In feis— it falls on the first 
syllable.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
The following forms are found in the Glosses. The absence 
of the mark of length may be accidental in some cases. 
The following rimes have been noted: 
        fadessin: sessir

    <p class="result"> 
The forms fadisin (<span class="per 3">3</span> <span class="number s.">s.</span> <span class="gen f.">f.</span> and <span class="per 2">2</span> <span class="number pl.">pl.</span>) and fanisin (<span class="per 1">1</span> <span class="number pl.">pl.</span>) 
are early lost; fadéin (fo—, ba—, bo—, bu—), fadesin and 
fes(s)in or fesne continue in use throughout Mid. Ir., but 
lose their specific characters, being employed indifferently 
in all persons and both numbers and genders. In 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Auraic." title="eDIL Bibliography">Auraic.</strong></span> 
650
       the form faden is given for <span class="number sing.">sing.</span>, fadesin for <span class="number pl.">pl.</span>, in 
combination with a pers. pron.: me faden, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>, sinni 
fadesin, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> During the Mid.-Ir. period féin comes more 
and more into use in all persons, genders and numbers and 
finally survives alone in the mod. language. In PH fadéin 
(—dén, fodéin, —dén) occurs 37 times and fadessin (fo—) 
14 times, besides corresponding forms in bo—, bu—; fes(s)in 
37 times; while féin (fén) is very frequent. LB favours 
the spelling fén instead of the more usual féin (LU, LL); 
dom péin
       occurs 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LB" title="eDIL Bibliography">LB</strong></span> 134b30
      . Other Mid. Ir. forms are 
fodéne (bo—, ba—, bu—) and féine.</p>
