<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="23233">fonaidm</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. fonaidm or <a class="reference" href="23233">dil.ie/23233</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                fosnaidm</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span>  <span class="itype n">n</span>, <span class="gen n.">n.</span> (<span class="pos vn." style="color: #ff8000">vn.</span> of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=fo-naisc">fo-naisc</a></span>) rare in Text of Laws, but 
frequent in Comm., often as gloss on <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=naidm(m)">naidm(m)</a></span> from which 
it does not seem to differ in meaning. Thurneysen ( 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/B&#xFC;rgschaft" title="eDIL Bibliography">Bürgschaft</strong></span> 
p. 83
      ) suggests that (despite the difference in prep.) it is 
an imitation of Lat. <span class="frn ">ob-ligatio</span>. Somet. written fosnaidm 
(<span class="lbl">cf.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=snaidm">snaidm</a></span>), see below.</p>
