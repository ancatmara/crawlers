                2013</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                air, ar, ár, úaire</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
  
  
    <p class="result">prep. <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 ar">1 ar</a></span> used as <span class="pos conjunction" style="color: #ff8000">conjunction</span> <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">for</span>, <span class="def">since</span>, seeing <i>that</i>, 
<i>for the</i> reason <i>that</i>
      </span>. In O. Ir. also air (see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> § 
168
      . This spelling predominates only in Ml., ar being 
commoner both in Wb. and Sg.). Also 
        ár, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 3255
        
      , 
        3257
       
(
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SCC" title="eDIL Bibliography">SCC</strong></span> 5
      ) (vowel perh. influenced by ór = úaire, MU2 Gloss., 
<span class="lbl">cf.</span> also ór from ó + ro). Occasionally leniting (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 42c8
      , 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 206a3
      ), see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> § 906
      . Perh. substituted for 
earlier ol, al (see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=2 ol">2 ol</a></span>). <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
        ar ni raibi, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> i 118.14
        
       = 
        ol ni 
rabi, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 10718
        
      . Also 
        ál asbert, 
          10721
        
       = 
        al, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xii 164.1
        
       
(TE2. ┐ isbert, <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>). Wind., 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> i Wtb
      . compares <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=olchena">olchena</a></span> 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=archena">archena</a></span>.</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
`Introduces principal causal clauses or periods', 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> § 906
       or dependent negative: ba samalta re Meidb na beth penning a selba lé. arna baí tarb a chomméit lé fora búaib '<span class="quote">it seemed to Medb as if she had not a penny of possessions since she had not a bull as great as that among her kine</span>' 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> ii 7624
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> ix 122
      . radis friss ara tísed uathad leis … ár nach crích ł ferand baí aice, '<span class="quote">he told him to come only with a small number … for he had neither land nor domain</span>' 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> ii 8382
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> ix 122
      . atraachtatar Ulaid co barbarda ara n-armaib. ár nar lam Sencha érgi eturru '<span class="quote">the Ulstermen rose furiously to seize their arms since Sencha did not dare to rise among them</span>' 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> v 34699
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> ix 122
      . The clause so introduced is usually 
an explanation or amplification of the circumstances attending a foregoing statement or question, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> 
        co farcbad leesi a 
liasom . . . Ar adrubairtseom is isind liic robui a anim a 
mathar, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Anecd." title="eDIL Bibliography">Anecd.</strong></span> i 10.13
        
       (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SCano" title="eDIL Bibliography">SCano</strong></span> 309
      ). 
        cid ane . . . meic na 
mbachlach . . . do chomram cucam ? Ar ba mese sacart oc 
baistiud ind anma sin fora athair, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sc.M2" title="eDIL Bibliography">Sc.M²</strong></span> 12
        
      . With absol. 
and deut. form of verbs: 
        airataat iltintudai leu cene,

        17c7
      .

danimmart greim á aite, 
          14b14
