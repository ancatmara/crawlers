<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="32895">-n-</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. -n- or <a class="reference" href="32895">dil.ie/32895</a></span><br/></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="per 1">1</span> <span class="number pl.">pl.</span> <span class="pos pron." style="color: #ff8000">pron.</span> inf., used in O.Ir. after the preverbs ar-, do- 
(&lt; to-, di-), di-, fo-, imb-, no, ro, and the negatives ní, nách, 
to denote the obj. (direct or indirect) of the vb. In O.Ir. occas. 
-nn- before a vowel. After ar-, imb- and nách a vowel (gen. 
<i>u</i> or <i>a</i>) is inserted before the pron. Continues in use throughout Mid.Ir. period; in earlier Mid.Ir. sometimes replaced by 
-ar-n- (
        nichar-fail, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 1560
        
      ; ro-rn-indarbais <span class="ediltr">thou hast banished 
us</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LB" title="eDIL Bibliography">LB</strong></span> 112b44
      ). Often strengthened by the particle -ni (-ne) 
follg. the vb.</p>
