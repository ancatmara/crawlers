<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="29367">laíd</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. laíd or <a class="reference" href="29367">dil.ie/29367</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2013</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                laí, laoi, laíde, laogha</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   

 
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="gen f.">f.</span> Later laí, laoi. <span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> laíde, also perh. 
        laogha, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xiii 
27.7
        
      . <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">poem</span>, <span class="def">lay</span>, <span class="def">metrical</span> <span class="def">composition</span>
      </span>.</p>
   
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) Orig. a particular 
type of poem, which like anair, anamain, emain and nath, 
was not of the `bairdne' or syllabic type. See 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MurphyMetrics" title="eDIL Bibliography">Murphy Metrics</strong></span> 3. 12
        . Mentioned as proper to the `dos': 
          in dos . . . 
laid a aisde, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 62.17 Comm
          
        . 
          bó i logh laoidhe cona 
toimsibh techta, muna ttoimhsither in aircheadol ni fḟil logh 
lais, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xiii 27.4
          
        . 
          cia halt aircetail . . . .i. i[n] nath, inn anair, 
inn anamain, in laidh, in sethrad, in sainemain, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Auraic." title="eDIL Bibliography">Auraic.</strong></span> 829 
(1578)
          
        . Reckoned as subdivision of anamain: 
          nath ┐ anair, 
l.<span class="oRef">¤</span>, ┐ emain. Itt e sein tra cetrí re na hanamna, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> iii 59 § 
114
          
        . Entitles poet to legal concession: 
          in laid ma tathar 
aga molad re ré trir geibidh greim rudartha, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 1059
          
         
(
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span>, 7a
          
        ) = 
          laig in filidh . . . gebid grem tuinide do, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OD." title="eDIL Bibliography">O'D.</strong></span> 
1581
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H5.15" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 5.15</strong></span>, 7a
          
        ). 
          fri hailche .i. do laídh no do odhum, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 1891
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/23P3" title="eDIL Bibliography">23 P 3</strong></span>, 22b
          
        ). Later of specific metres: 
          laidh 
frecnairc, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> iii 102 § 185
          
        . l.<span class="oRef">¤</span> lúascach, Murphy 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MurphyMetrics" title="eDIL Bibliography">Murphy Metrics</strong></span> no. 
77
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> iii 87 § 100
        . 
          88 § 102
        ). 
          laidh luibheancosach, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> 
iii 94 § 144
          
        . 
          laidh treabhraidh, 
            95 § 146
          
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) In tales <span class="lbl">etc.</span> freq. said to be recited or composed by 
hero, warrior, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>, (perh. with religio-legal implications): 

          cachain S. dho drecht a choimhgne ┐ laoidh, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xiii 26.23
          
        . 

          canaidsi l.<span class="oRef">¤</span> n-aili, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 3409
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SCC" title="eDIL Bibliography">SCC</strong></span> 18
        ). ro chan l.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
        : 
          Erig a gérait 
Ulad, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span>
             3591
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SCC" title="eDIL Bibliography">SCC</strong></span> 30
        ). 
          ro bāi ic dūscud a thighernai ┐ dorigni 
in lāid, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-I1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-I¹</strong></span> 2521
          
        . 
          dorigni in lāid mbicc and ac gresacht 
an arad, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-I1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-I¹</strong></span> 2447
          
        . 
          do gab fora adhmoladh ┐ itbert in 
laidh, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBCSt." title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC St.</strong></span> 1323
          
        . 
          adbeart an laoí ann, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 3623 n
          
        . 

          no canustar in laidh m-bicc ┐ rus freccair Ailill, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TE" title="eDIL Bibliography">TE</strong></span> 9
          
         Eg. 
(
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> i 123
        ). <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> laed .i. laiter no luaitir .i. `<span class="quote">it is sent or it is 
hastened</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Auraic." title="eDIL Bibliography">Auraic.</strong></span> 1581.
        
      </p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">c</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">poem</span>, <span class="def">lay</span>, <span class="def">song</span>
        </span> in general: 
          lúaidfidir láedib limmsa, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> ii 295.6
          
        . (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Hy." title="eDIL Bibliography">Hy.</strong></span> v
        ). 
          laidi ┐ roscada, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/YBL" title="eDIL Bibliography">YBL</strong></span> 126b41
          
        . luam 
laidi fo chaini cruth <span class="ediltr">leader of song</span> (of David), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 6057
        . laeid 
dó cach duine dlegair (of famous lake), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 20243
         (
          láid, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> iii 260.12
          
        ). 
          drechta .i. duana no laidhe ┐ roscada, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ODav." title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Dav.</strong></span> 589
          
        . binni laoidib a labrad `<span class="quote">sweeter than songs</span>' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> 
ii 16.5
        . 
          rosepaind ceōl mbind ┐ rogob lāid le, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> v 500.11
          
        . 

          tucc Flann mac Lonain laid ngloin / d'Ecnechan oigri 
Daloig, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IrishTexts" title="eDIL Bibliography">Irish Texts</strong></span> ii 1 § 3
          
        . 
          duais mo laoidheadh, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PBocht" title="eDIL Bibliography">PBocht</strong></span> 
8.37
          
        . a gcoimhlíon liom do laoidhibh (of three poems to 
cool chief's anger as the ardour of Cú Chulainn was cooled 
by the three vats of water), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/DD&#xE1;na" title="eDIL Bibliography">DDána</strong></span> 88.20
        . Of song of bird: 

          fomchain lóid loin, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> ii 290.7
          
        . See <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 portach">1 portach</a></span>.</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">d</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">poem</span>
        </span> as medium for legal, historical, religious and 
didactic lore: 
          ord fil ar loídib, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> Prol. 314

lérib, 
            334

        . 
          persa na laidhe, 

            4.13 Comm
          
         . See <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=fursundud">fursundud</a></span>.

mebrugud na laíde, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> iii 42.42

ibar Baile . . . / cia do bertar illaide (: daíne), 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 18181

        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">e</span>) In later language of pagan and christian lore: 
          cá 
bhfuighe i laoidhe nó i litir, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Aithd.D." title="eDIL Bibliography">Aithd. D.</strong></span> 32.11

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OR.Poems" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'R. Poems</strong></span> 1050

        . 
          neach don druingsi . . . / an bhfuair tú i laoi nó i litir, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/L.Cl.A.B." title="eDIL Bibliography">L. Cl. A. B.</strong></span> 269.26

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Eochairsg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Eochairsg.</strong></span> 21.39
