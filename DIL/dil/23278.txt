(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">a <span class="def">mound</span> or <span class="def">platform</span>
        </span>, prob. in most cases of earth, 
used as <span class="trans">a <span class="def">seat</span> or <span class="def">stand</span> for spectators</span>, but also as <span class="trans">a <span class="def">post</span> 
of outlook</span>; it varied in size and shape, being often large 
enough to accommodate a number of persons, but sometimes apparently intended for only one; it may in some 
cases have been circular; at the great interprovincial 
assemblies each king had a special f. and there seems to 
have been a f. set apart for women. There was sometimes 
a f. in or close to a chief's `dún.' There was a f. at Tara, 
identified by Macalister with a large circular mound still 
existing (see Temair Breg,
