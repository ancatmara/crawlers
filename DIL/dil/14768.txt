The old inflexion is represented by <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> Daui, Dau, Dái: 
 <span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> Duiach (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> ái <span class="ediltr">poetry</span>: <span class="case gen.">gen.</span> uath): <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number p.">p.</span> Dauich. In 
Mid.-Ir. MSS we meet with a double inflexion, one starting 
from the <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> Daui, Dau, forming the <span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> Daui, Dai; the 
other from the oblique cases (and <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number p.">p.</span>) forming the <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> 
Duach. For <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> Daui <span class="lbl">cf.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=fili">fili</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=o&#xED;gi">oígi</a></span>. Daui is a <span class="pos npr." style="color: #ff8000">npr.</span> in -vik 
like Ogh. <span class="frn ">DENA-VECA</span> (<span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span>, † <span class="frn ">Dianach</span>), Ogh. <span class="frn ">RITTA-VECAS</span> (<span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span>, 
Rethach). Its Ogh. equivalent would probably be † Ogh. <span class="frn ">DAVOVECCA</span> or † Ogh. <span class="frn ">DASU-VECCA</span> (= Dauoch), if, as is not unlikely, 
it is to be compared with Gaul. <span class="frn ">DAVOS</span> or Gaul. <span class="frn ">DASVVIOS</span>. Dau 
appears to have become obsolete as a <span class="pos npr." style="color: #ff8000">npr.</span> as early as the 
early 7th century; the <span class="case gen.">gen.</span> Duach, however, lingered on 
throughout the Mid.-Ir. period in connection with <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=s&#xED;l">síl</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 d&#xE1;l">1 dál</a></span>, 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=clann">clann</a></span>, Húi, and in place-names with Clúain, Ard (
        Ard 
nDuach,
