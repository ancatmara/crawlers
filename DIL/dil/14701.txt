 <span class="trans">A <span class="def">heifer</span> or a <span class="def">bull</span> <span class="def">calf</span>
      </span> growing into <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=b&#xF3;">bó</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=tarb">tarb</a></span> and <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=dam">dam</a></span>. See <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/EIF" title="eDIL Bibliography">EIF</strong></span> 60-61
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xlii 93, 100
      .

    <p class="result"> 
Frequent in the Laws, indicating the `sét' of the lowest 
value, the `sét gabla.' Its value was one-twelfth of a cow, 
 <span class="lbl">i.e.</span> two screpalls (not three as Atkinson says), or three 
`miach' (
        ii 258. 14
      ); the dairt (= three screpalls) ranks 
higher: 
        dígu sét somaíne .i. in sét is díthogaidi somaíne 
ann .i. dartaid dá screpall, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iv 28. 19

    <p class="result"> 
Dairt and dartaid both originate from the root of dairid, 
dáir, but their relation to each other is not clear; dartaid 
looks like a nom. ag., dairt like a vn. Both words became 
early obsolete, probably in early Mid.-Ir. The form dairtid 
(
        dairtida, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 386. 3
          <span class="lbl">f.b.</span>
        
      ) shows the influence of dairt. 
Dartada in the passage: 
        nocon ḟarrgaib acht teora dartada 
in Hérind .i. dairt i Cliu dairt i lLiniu dairt i Cuailṅgiu, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 
389 a 10
        
      , is either due to confusion of dairt and dartaid, or 
it is an early instance of the Mid.-Ir. <span class="number pl.">pl.</span> in -adha.—<span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=d&#xE1;ir">dáir</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=dairid">dairid</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 dairt">1 dairt</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=dart&#xE1;n">dartán</a></span>.</p>
