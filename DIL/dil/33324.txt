<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="33324">núachar</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. núachar or <a class="reference" href="33324">dil.ie/33324</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                nóchar, nuachar, nodhchar</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   




    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype o">o</span>, <span class="gen m.">m.</span> in late poetry also nóchar, <span class="trans">a <span class="def">mate</span>, <span class="def">spouse</span>
      </span>, 
poetic term, used of both sexes. nuachar `<span class="trans">a <span class="def">companion</span>, a 
<span class="def">wife</span>, a <span class="def">husband</span>
      </span>', O'R., who gives also the form nodhchar. 
        a 
Medb . . . nít chredb caíne nuachair, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 3093
        
       = 
        nit cerb 
caime n.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
        , 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-I1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-I¹</strong></span> 2282
        
      ; said by Fer Diad to Medb who tries to 
induce him to fight Cú Chulaind by offering him her daughter 
Findabair in marriage; <span class="ediltr">the plaint (? beauty) of a bridegroom 
(lover) does not touch thee?</span> ba hadba niad is núachair `<span class="quote">a home 
of hero and bride</span>', 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> iii 236.10
       (: Lúachair). ó ríg 
Crúachan clothchóir | fuair núachar co nert-maín `<span class="quote">he got a 
bride rich in substance</span>', 
        298.10
      . do dith Maisten, méit núachair `<span class="quote">for the loss of Maistin goodly bride</span>', 
        134.3
      . 
        ar bhithin 
leannain tsídhe ┐ ardnuachair . . . .i. Crédhe ingen Cairbri, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Acall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Acall.</strong></span> 748.
        
       ba roga nuachair cen ach | ingen Tuathail <span class="ediltr">a 
choice mate (bride</span>), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 298b42
       (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SG" title="eDIL Bibliography">SG</strong></span> 367.37
      ). is tú a rogha 
nuachair <span class="ediltr">her choice of husband</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> vi 31.20
      . ni túgha 
nuachuir lem-sa `<span class="quote">I have no choice of bridegroom</span>', 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Imr.Brain" title="eDIL Bibliography">Imr. Brain</strong></span> 69. 
15
      . deir co núachraib núa `<span class="quote">a fresh girl sought by suitors</span>', 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> iii 300.41
      . 
        ní heol dom an nóchar budh céile dhí, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Keat.Poems" title="eDIL Bibliography">Keat. Poems</strong></span> 121.
        
       do chéile nóchair <span class="ediltr">thy husband</span>, 
        212
      .</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
Used fig. <span class="trans">of a <span class="def">ruler</span> or <span class="def">champion</span> of <span class="def">Ireland</span>:</span> 
        raba missi a 
nuachur coir | d'ingin alaind hErimoin, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 149b46
        
      . 
        biaid 
cen buachail inis Fáil | aithli nuacha[i]r maith d'fhagbail, 
          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 39
        
      . ba he aonghlór na sochraite . . . gur vo hadhbhar nuachair d'Eirinn antí Conall (i.e. that C. was a fit successor to 
the high kingship), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xiv 223.12
      . dod nuachar (addressing 
Banba), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Keat.Poems" title="eDIL Bibliography">Keat. Poems</strong></span> 1352.

        na cethri chóicid la

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 297a50
