treated as <span class="itype &#x101;">ā</span>-st. (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> the vn. nóad which may belong to this 
vb.) and nói- (? noí-), <span class="lbl">cf.</span> the variant form nóithid (? noíthid) ; 
perh. both forms coexisted; <span class="lbl">cf.</span> the dual development of núae 
(&gt; núa and naoi) and of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=n&#xFA;aide">núaide</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=nu&#xED;de">nuíde</a></span>. O'Davoren's gl. 
noudh .i. athnugud suggests a connexion with <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=n&#xFA;ae">núae</a></span>.</p>
