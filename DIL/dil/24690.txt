<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="24690">fúaslaici, fúasailci</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. fúaslaici, fúasailci or <a class="reference" href="24690">dil.ie/24690</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2019</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                ronfuaslaice, fon-uasluice, do-fuissim, -forslaic</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   

    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos v" style="color: #ff8000">v</span>  Mid. Ir. formation prob. from O. Ir. 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=do-f&#xFA;asailci">do-fúasailci</a></span> through loss of the first preverb, according to 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> ii 
564
      , but see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AM" title="eDIL Bibliography">AM</strong></span> 48
      . Used side by side with <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=oslaicid">oslaici</a></span> (foslaici), <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> 
        fuaslaicthir ... oslaicther, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 1003
        
      , 
        1006
      . 
        co 
forsluicther ... ni fuaslaicfimne ... fonosailc-si, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xiii 
24.24
        
      , 
        25
       = 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/B&#xFC;rgschaft" title="eDIL Bibliography">Bürgschaft</strong></span> p. 32 § 2
      . <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> ronfuaslaice <span class="lbl">gl.</span>) don-forslaice), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Hy." title="eDIL Bibliography">Hy</strong></span> i 18
       (T). In deuterotonic form, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span>, 
        fa fuasailcci, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/B&#xFC;rgschaft" title="eDIL Bibliography">Bürgschaft</strong></span> p. 32 § 2
        
      . 
        fon-uasluice, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 214y
        
       (see 
other exx. from Laws text below), the f is inserted analogically. <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> such forms as <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=do-f&#xFA;arat">do-fúarat</a></span>, do-fuissim. A 
(probably spurious) compd. fuatuaslaic- is found, Laws 
Comm. In 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span>
         ii 362.12
      , 
        v 128.20
      ; 
        216.10
       it glosses fo-fuaslaice; 
in 
        iii 38.19
       arosloice. <span class="tns Perf.">Perf.</span> occasionally -forslaic (see below); 
<span class="lbl">cf.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=for-oslaici">for-oslaici</a></span>.</p>
  
  
     

      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">looses</span>, <span class="def">opens</span>, <span class="def">sets free</span> (in physical sense):</span> 
          ra fuaslaiced dind archoin a chonarach, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 1007
          
         = ro sgaoileadh 
St. ro fhuasluicc a claidhiub dia orcain <span class="ediltr">drew his sword to 
slay him</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Lism.L." title="eDIL Bibliography">Lism. L.</strong></span> 279
        . <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
          cach mac ... ima fuatuaslaicend 
in mathair a broind, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iii 38.19
          
         (gloss on: cach fermac 
arosloice broind a mathar). 
          triasa trascairther secht múir 
... ┐ tresa fuaslaicther int ochtmad múr, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 8017
          
        . </p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
Intrans. with FOR: fuaslaiced tra foirnn (= <span class="ediltr">let him open 
our grave</span>) 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 1502
        . dia fúaslaicthe tu uirri-seo <span class="ediltr">if you open 
this (box</span>) 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Mer.Uil." title="eDIL Bibliography">Mer. Uil.</strong></span> 167
         (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> ro ḟoslaicset furri <span class="ediltr">they opened it</span> 

          303
        ).</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">releases</span>, <span class="def">delivers</span>; <span class="def">redeems</span>:</span> dianfarslaic Dia ... do 
lámaib a derbráthar <span class="ediltr">when God released him</span> [<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> <span class="ediltr">Joseph</span>] 
 <span class="ediltr">from the hands of his brethren</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 7319
        . 
          dian forslaic angel 
Dé ... [Petar] ... a cumrigib, 
            7399
          
        . 
          fuaslaicit ... a anmain 
o na hocht cairib, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 4616
          
        . 
          isin sollamain-sa ro fuasligthe 
na doine, 
            5595
          
        . 
          is é ro fuaslaic fir Erenn ... ó daeire, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Cog." title="eDIL Bibliography">Cog.</strong></span> 
202y
          
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
 <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">Redeems</span> (a pledge):</span> dlegait flaithe fonuaslaicther a 
ngella `<span class="quote">the chiefs are entitled to the redemption of their pledges</span>' 
 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iii 24.12
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sench.M&#xF3;r" title="eDIL Bibliography">SM Facs.</strong></span> 48a
          
        ).</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">c</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">loosens</span> .i. e. <span class="def">dissolves</span>, <span class="def">annuls</span>:</span> fofuasluice cechtar 
da lina dochuru araili, ni fuaslaici a sochuru <span class="ediltr">either of the</span> 
(<span class="ediltr">contracting) parties can dissolve the bad contracts of the 
other; they cannot dissolve their good contracts</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> ii 362.6
        
(
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Stud.inE.Ir.Law" title="eDIL Bibliography">Stud. in E. Ir. Law</strong></span> 27
        ). secht n-anfolaid fofuaslaiced udhburta 
`<span class="quote">which invalidate the offerings</span>' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span>
           v 128.13
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">d</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">solves</span> (a problem) :</span> o rofúaslaic [Ioseph] fís Forainn 
 <span class="ediltr">explained Pharaoh's dream</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 7324
        . 
          in cheisd do chuirdís 
... ro ḟuaslaiced Find féin, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Acall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Acall.</strong></span> 2555
          
        . <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> a cor a tenid 
... conid amlaid sin ro fuaslaic ┐ romarb in natraigh <span class="ediltr">thus 
he solved [the difficulty] and killed the serpent</span> (? of Hercules 
and the Lernean hydra) 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BB" title="eDIL Bibliography">BB</strong></span> 417a18
        . See <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ar-osailci">ar-osailci</a></span> and 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=for-oslaici">for-oslaici</a></span>.</p>
