<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="28540">indsaigid, innaigid</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. indsaigid, innaigid or <a class="reference" href="28540">dil.ie/28540</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                innaigid, indsaigid, innsaige, n-innsaidhthe, n-innsaighi, hionnsaighthi, innaigid, innsaigid, indnaigid, innaigid, innaigidh</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype i">i</span>, <span class="gen f.">f.</span> <span class="pos vn." style="color: #ff8000">vn.</span> of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ind-saig">ind-saig</a></span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> § 805 (6)
      . 
According to Meyer (see refs. under <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ind-saig">ind-saig</a></span>, above) innaigid is <span class="pos vn." style="color: #ff8000">vn.</span> of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ind-saig">ind-saig</a></span>, indsaigid of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=in(d)-asaig">in(d)-asaig</a></span>. This would 
be philologically correct (the preverb ind- leniting in prototonic forms) but the two forms became confused and are 
therefore treated together. Later also innsaige. <span class="case g">g</span>
