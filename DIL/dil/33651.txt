    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="gen f.">f.</span> also oile, in phrase o.<span class="oRef">¤</span> i n-úir; acc. to 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Dec. § 14.4
      ,

oil i n-úir is the correct form, whereas oil a dég (oil dhég)

`eleventh' is incorrect; but in 
        § 3.12
       oili a n-úir (oile n-uir <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>)

is given beside oile dég; see also 
        exx. 673
      , 
        175
      , 
        229
      . The word

seems = Mid.Ir. ail `<span class="ediltr">rock</span>'; o.<span class="oRef">¤</span> i n-úir <span class="ediltr">a large stone or pillar

stone</span>(<span class="ediltr">fixed) in the ground</span>, either as a tombstone, or a memorial

or witness (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Bedel" title="eDIL Bibliography">Genesis</strong></span> xxviii 18
      , 
        xxxi 51
      , 
        52
      ) ? In 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iv

140.23 Comm.
      : cad iad caithge aile? A n-asdadh it sheilb

cene fola aile a ṅ-uir ┐ rathus a ime fort iarsuidiu (`<span class="quote">what are

the trespasses of stakes? To retain them in thy possession with-

out sticking them in the ground, after which thou art responsible

for the fence</span>', transl.), a passage evidently corrupt, aile appears

to be <span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> of ail `stone' depending on a noun governed by the

prep. cen, and the sense may be: `without . . . of a stone in

earth', <span class="lbl">i.e.</span> a witness-stone (or mere-stone?).</p>
  
  
    <p class="result">In follg. exx. used of a gravestone: imdha o chnuc Laithrigh

ille | fert is oil a n-uir uaibse `<span class="quote">many's the grave and tombstone(set) in the ground through thee</span>', Quiggin,<span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Prolegomena" title="eDIL Bibliography">Prolegomena</strong></span> p. 28 n. 1
      . 
        oil i n-úir do

dhéanamh ┐ leachtán cloch do dheanamh ós a cionn, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TSh." title="eDIL Bibliography">TSh.</strong></span>
10510
        
       (o.<span class="oRef">¤</span> anúir MSS.).</p>
