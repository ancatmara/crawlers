 <span class="edilb">I</span> Orig. <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">love</span> of <span class="def">God</span>
        </span>, <span class="lbl">i.e.</span> (<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">God's</span> <span class="def">love</span> to man</span> and 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">man's</span> <span class="def">love</span> of God (serc Dé) and his neighbour, commanded as the fulfilling of the Law</span> (
          car do chomnesam 
amal notchara fén, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 5837
          
        ).— Deercc represents <span class="frn ">caritas</span> 
of the Vulgate (Gk. <span class="frn ">ἀγάπη</span>). As the frequent addition of 
Dé (deercc Dé) shews, the sense of de- was no longer 
apparent.</p>
