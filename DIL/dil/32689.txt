<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="32689">muich, múich, muích</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. muich, múich, muích or <a class="reference" href="32689">dil.ie/32689</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                múich, múch, muich, múich, -úi-, -uí-, muich, múch, -ui-, -úi-, -uí-, muich, múch, múig, múch, múich</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   


    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> [<span class="gen f.">f.</span>] <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">gloom</span>, <span class="def">dejection</span>, <span class="def">sadness</span>
      </span>. Generally by edd. taken as múich, a secondary form of múch 
`smoke', but perh. a distinct word. In Mid.Ir. texts nearly 
always written muich without accent, in LB occas. múich (but 
the LB scribe is prone to superfluous accents and, moreover, 
-úi- oft. stands for -uí-). Prob. the word had a dual development: 
muich&gt; (1) múich through influence of múch, (2) muích 
through association with <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=maeth">maeth</a></span> = O.Ir. <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=mo&#xED;th">moíth</a></span>; <span class="lbl">cf.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=maeith">maeith, maoith</a></span> 
which may represent this word. The transition of -ui- to -úi- or 
-uí- is not unknown elsewhere (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> the occas. confusion between 
muin and maín).
