<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="41419">toracht, torachtu, torachtain</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. toracht, torachtu, torachtain or <a class="reference" href="41419">dil.ie/41419</a></span><br/></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos vn." style="color: #ff8000">vn.</span> of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=do-roich">do-roich</a></span>: confused 
with <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=t&#xE1;rrachtain">tárrachtain</a></span>, <span class="lbl">q.v.</span> It is difficult to separate them in the 
later language, <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Decl. § 24
      , 
        § 148
      , 
        Verbs § 19
      .</p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) In the glosses translates Latin <span class="frn ">successus, successio</span> 
and <span class="frn ">processus</span> in various senses: hón toracht .i. hond 
soinmichi són, gl. successu, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 48c16
        . ar in toracht .i. arint 
soinnigi gl. pro eventu, 
          49a1
        . hó thorachtib gl. successionibus, 
          34c3
        . inna toracht són gl. procesus, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 29a14
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) <span class="trans">act of <span class="def">arriving at</span>, <span class="def">coming up to</span>:</span> 
          iar torachtu in chori 
dó, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Trip." title="eDIL Bibliography">Trip.</strong></span> 472.13
          
        . 
          robatar oca torachtain (<span class="lbl">sic leg.</span> ?) in 
dunaid, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-I1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-I¹</strong></span> 3408
          
        . 
          sluaigedh ... do thorachtain Temrach, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xvii 353.20
          
        . 
          oc torachtain Temrach, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Anecd." title="eDIL Bibliography">Anecd.</strong></span> ii 4.17
          
        . 

          mochen fur torachtain cugum, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> vi 74.10
          
        . 
          roforcongradh ó Ṡenchan for filedaibh Ērenn torachtain chuige, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ACL" title="eDIL Bibliography">ACL</strong></span> iii 4.22
          
        . 
          im torachtain chucu laithe mbrátha co

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 2228

      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">c</span>) In various legal senses: a torachtain do ineich no 
slaintigend o bidbaid a toichedh `<span class="quote">to obtain for himself</span>,' 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 182.y
        . <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=t&#xE1;rrachtain">tárrachtain</a></span> I (d). is in nerrach co na 
torrachtain ina uide icce coir `<span class="quote">which is to be forthcoming</span>,' 

          ii 354.20 Comm.
         ar fomailt indligid na flatha for na ceilib 
fri torachtain uatha `<span class="quote">in exacting aid (?) from them</span>,' 
          v 188.2 
Comm.
         a torachtuin féin `<span class="quote">and return him (the stolen person)</span>,' 

          iii 64.18 Comm.
         incomalltar og ime dia mis .i. comlanaigther 
in íme comlan do toractain a forbu ín mís `<span class="quote">is brought to its 
completion</span>,' 
          iv 70.y. Comm.
         is na sétuibh cethardha cuna 
torachtuin fén `<span class="quote">when they themselves survive</span>,' 
          ii 68.x Comm.
