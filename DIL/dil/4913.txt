      <span class="pos v" style="color: #ff8000">v</span> (*
        ess-reg, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> ii 594
        
      ) with inf. pron. agreeing in 
number, person and gender with subject in meaning <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">rises</span> 
(often also <span class="def">rises</span> and <span class="def">goes</span>, <span class="def">goes</span>).</span> Perh. archaic use without pron. 
in: ineresomni gl. suscitare, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> i 713.26
       (`<span class="quote">in which we will 
raise</span>(?)', note). Note also: 
        atomrachtatar, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xii 365.17
        
       
(meaning not clear). Later with gradual obsolescence of inf. 
pron., beginning where verb is in dependent position, and 
ultimately as simple verb <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=&#xE9;irgid">éirgid</a></span>, <span class="lbl">q.v.</span> Some forms of the 
s-subj. and fut. may explain exx. given under dreisid. In mid. 
Irish a form a(t)fraig(id) arises with inorganic -f- prob. 
originating in cases where the inf. pron. ended in a vowel 
(<span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        ataregat, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 8191
        
      , 
        atafregat, 
          8200
        
      ). (Leniting forms a more likely starting-point 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LoingesMacnDu&#xED;lDermait" title="eDIL Bibliography">Loinges mac nDuíl Dermait</strong></span> 85
      ). The <span class="mood ipv.">ipv.</span> atré, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> 
is an orig. <span class="mood s-subj.">s-subj.</span> form (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> 375
      , Watkins, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celt.Verb" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celt. Verb</strong></span> 139
      , Zimmer, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/KZ" title="eDIL Bibliography">KZ</strong></span> xxx 118
      -
        119
      ). The s-subj. and fut. 
are distinguishable only by context. The form atraacht 
occurring occas. in mid. Ir. may be a dissimilation-form 
from the <span class="tns perf.">perf.</span> atraracht. <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> also atralacht cited below. For 
archaic forms permissible in classical verse see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Verbs
§ 17
      .</p>
