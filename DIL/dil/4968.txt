                antúaid, atúaidhean, -eamhain</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
  
  
    <p class="result">(túaid). In O. Ir. antúaid (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Gadelica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Gadelica</strong></span> 133
      ). <span class="pos Adv." style="color: #ff8000">Adv.</span> 
of place stressed on second syll. In later lang. also atúaidhean, 
-eamhain (see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Bard.Synt.Tr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Bard. Synt. Tr.</strong></span> p. 25
      ).</p>
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">from the <span class="def">north</span>
        </span> (freq. with verbs implying motion): 
antúaid cach ṅdiruch gl. flat rectus ab axe `<span class="quote">straight from the 
north</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BCr." title="eDIL Bibliography">BCr.</strong></span> 19c1
        . 
          rogabsat . . . la hairer nErend antúaid, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 6753
          
         (or to be referred to (c)?). 
          tic-si a.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
          , 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Fing.R." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fing. R.</strong></span> 16
          
        . 
Of north wind: 
          atuaid in dub gailbech, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 71
          
         = 
          anair in 
gaeth corcra . . . atuaith an dub, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 26.y Comm.
          
         
          corb 
taithne grian . . . i tuaidh do feruib betha, 
            22.18 Comm.
          
         

          tuc in míscél leo atuaid a tír Channan, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LB" title="eDIL Bibliography">LB</strong></span> 123b24
          
        . 
          a 
tuaidhean . . . / do smuaineadh leo a leanmhain, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Miscell.Celt.Soc." title="eDIL Bibliography">Miscell. Celt. Soc.</strong></span> 362.11
          
        . 
          teacht a dtuaidh, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Content." title="eDIL Bibliography">Content.</strong></span> xxviii 15

        . 
          táinig 
Féinius . . . adtuaidh ón Scitia go Maigh Seanáir, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Keat." title="eDIL Bibliography">Keat.</strong></span> ii 73
          
        .</p>
