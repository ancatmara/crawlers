      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> (eidre, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> also 
common) (AN loanword 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Risk" title="eDIL Bibliography">Risk</strong></span> 588
      ) <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">heir</span>, <span class="def">inheritor</span>, <span class="def">successor</span>, <span class="def">descendant</span>
      </span> (often 
equivalent to mac). The earliest dateable ex. recorded is 
eigher (:deiredh) in a 13th cent. poem 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/23N28" title="eDIL Bibliography">23 N 28</strong></span>, 35
       (see 
below), but eigre (oigre), the commoner form, may be 
older.</p>
