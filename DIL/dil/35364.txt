<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="35364">1 ro, ru</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 1 ro, ru or <a class="reference" href="35364">dil.ie/35364</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                ra, doràla, ri, dogní, dorigéni, dorigni, re, relic, reilced, níro, náro, coro, nír, nár, cor, ro, doro-r-ban, duforbad, do-r-airngert</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos preverb" style="color: #ff8000">preverb</span> used with various forms of a finite vb. whether 
simple or compound; lenites follg. consonant except, in Old 
Irish, when in pretonic position. (Its use as a fixed preverb in 
the composition of many compd. vbs. is not dealt with here). 
Under the accent it somet. assumes the form ra, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> doràla 
(= do-ro-la); and in O.Ir. becomes ri in the vb. dogní 
(<span class="tns pret.">pret.</span> <span class="per 3">3</span> <span class="number s.">s.</span> dorigéni, dorigni); with léicid, re (
        ní relic
        , 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 23b4
        
      , 

        nach reilced
        , 
          49a10
        
      ). It is often attached as enclitic to a 
conjunct particle: níro, náro, coro, later nír, nár, cor.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
In O.Ir. when used with a compd. vb. ro orig. followed all 
fixed preverbs: doro-r-ban (to-ro-ben-), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 62a20
      , duforbad 
(to-fo-ben-), 
        92b4
      ; but this rule becomes obsolete already 
in the O.Ir. period: 
        do-r-airngert, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 14c32
        
      . 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thurn." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thurn. Hdb.</strong></span> 
§§ 520
      -
        3
      . 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xii 286
      . </p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
In O.Ir. the vbs. ro-fitir (really a perf. tense, see (a) follg.), 
ro-cluinethar and ro-laimethar drop ro- only when preceded 
by another conjunct particle. For etymology and syntactical uses see Zimmer, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/KZ" title="eDIL Bibliography">KZ</strong></span> xxxvi 433 ff.
      ; Thurneysen, 
      
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/KZ" title="eDIL Bibliography">KZ</strong></span> xxxvii 
52 ff.
       and 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xii 286
      ; Strachan, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Phil.Soc.Trans." title="eDIL Bibliography">Phil. Soc. Trans.</strong></span> 1896, 
77 ff.
      ; 
        1897, 326 ff.
      ; 
        1899, 408 ff.
      ; 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xxiii 201
      ; M. L. 
Sjoestedt-Jonval, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;tudesCelt." title="eDIL Bibliography">Études Celt.</strong></span> iii 105 ff.
      , 
        219 ff.
       </p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
Used (<span class="edilb">a</span>) with the pret. to express a perfect. 
          ro comalnisid-si an ro pridchissem-ni dúib, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 26b6
          
        . In mod. Ir. it is 
displaced by do except after a neg. or a syntactical preverb: 
do ghabh, nír ghabh, gach ar ghabh sé.</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) with pres. ind. and subj. with perfective sense (generally 
to express an action which is completed before another takes 
place); also with subj. after résiu `<span class="quote">before</span>', acht `<span class="quote">provided 
that</span>', `<span class="quote">if only</span>', co `<span class="quote">until</span>': in nuall dongniat ho rumaith fora

        . 
          doberum . . . dilgud don fhoirind ronto-craidet, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 7941
