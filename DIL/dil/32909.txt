<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="32909">4 nach, nách</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 4 nach, nách or <a class="reference" href="32909">dil.ie/32909</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                nách, náich, nech</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos conj." style="color: #ff8000">conj.</span> introducing subord. neg. clause, <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">that . . . 
not</span>
      </span>. In O.Ir. used only with inf. pron. in place of ná, nád or 
as <span class="per 3">3</span> <span class="number s.">s.</span> <span class="tns pres.">pres.</span> <span class="mood ind.">ind.</span> of copula ; in Mid.Ir. more widely (but not 
with ro- forms of vb.). Occas. nách (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 12a6
      , 
        náich, 
          10a15
        
      ).</p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) after a n- (<span class="ediltr">when</span>), ol, huare (<span class="ediltr">because</span>), ar (in final clauses), 
co (in consecutive clauses), amal and the like ; with ar and co 
the word coalesces (arnach, connach) : a nnachat-toscelfat <span class="ediltr">when 
they will not experience thee</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 107c11
        . ol nacham-didna-sa <span class="ediltr">because it does not console me</span>, 
          86d3
        . huare nachan-soirai-nni <span class="ediltr">because thou dost not deliver us</span>, 
          93d10
        . arnachit-rindarpither <span class="ediltr">lest thou be cast away</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 5b33
        . arnacht-fordiucail in fer 
sommae <span class="ediltr">that . . . may not devour thee</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 36a32
         (= nachat-). 
arnach-ndermanadar Dia <span class="ediltr">that God should not forget him</span>, 
          32d5
        . 
arnacha-imrada <span class="ediltr">that he may not think them</span>, 
          51a1
        . 
          connacham-robae dliged, 
            90c16
          
        . connach-n-impad <span class="ediltr">so that he could not turn 
himself</span>, 
          122a14
        . connacha-dánaigfea <span class="ediltr">so that he will not bestow 
it</span> (<span class="lbl">sc.</span> trócaire), 
          96a7
        . 
          connách moidea nech
          , 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 2b4
          
         (<span class="per 3">3</span> <span class="number s.">s.</span> <span class="gen n.">n.</span> 
pron.).</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
Without inf. pron. : huare nach derni a adamrugud <span class="ediltr">because 
he cannot express admiration of him</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 128c3
         (exceptional 
construction in Glosses). do chin huair nach atamar <span class="ediltr">because 
thou didst not acknowledge thy guilt</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 1406.
         
          is i liss . . . ro alt 
conach acced fer hí, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> i 71.9
          
        . suail nach farroeblaṅgatar in 
bruiden <span class="ediltr">they almost leaped over</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CRR" title="eDIL Bibliography">CRR</strong></span> 15.
         cid damsa nach 
dígelaind ḟoraib? <span class="ediltr">why should I not punish you?</span> 
          46
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
Representing copula : amal nach forcend lipair nach 
maighin i mbí amén <span class="ediltr">as every place . . . is not the end of a book</span>, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Hib.Min." title="eDIL Bibliography">Hib. Min.</strong></span> 2.49
        . cid nach homo aspert? <span class="ediltr">why did he not say 
`<span class="frn ">homo</span>'?</span> 
          13.453
         (= cid arnach, Harl.). 
          suail nach ed domgni 
marb, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CRR" title="eDIL Bibliography">CRR</strong></span> 5.
          
         
          amal nách áil léind ar toccrád, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 5820.
          
         
          uair 
nachdat dee na híí chreti, 
            1141
          
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) in noun-sentences expanding a preceding pron. or noun 
or after vbs. of perceiving, thinking, saying, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> : is follus isindí 
nachim-rindarpai-se <span class="ediltr">in that he has not cast me away</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 5a18
        . 

          is ed ro chreti cách . . . nachab-ticfed, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 46a14
          
        . asberad som 
. . . nacha-soirbed <span class="ediltr">that he would not save them</span>, 
          53d6
        . dothuṅg 
. . . nacha-mbérat ar áis <span class="ediltr">that they shall not carry him off</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 
144.
         tiur-sa brethir . . . nachas-idnaidiub-sa <span class="ediltr">that I will not 
await them</span>, 
          4938
        . Without pron. : 
          isbertatar . . . nach ístais 
biad, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> i 75.15
          
        . 
          atbertsat nach tanic fáid amal Ísu, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 2796.
          
        
      </p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
Copula : 
          epert as nert Dáe . . . ┐ nach a nert fesin, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 63b12
          
        . 
ni ǽrburt nach armu peccad [d]ora[ta]d form <span class="ediltr">that it was not 
for my sin</span>, 
          44b19
        . in fhacca tusa nách Iúdaide mesi? <span class="ediltr">that I 
am not a Jew</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 2707.
         
          ro fhetamar-ni . . . conid o Dia ro

            4880
          
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result">

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 1845

          7644

nachat anmand uaisle . . . noemait nech, 
            5086
          
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">c</span>) in final clauses : álmí-ni . . . nachan-tecma drochrann <span class="ediltr">that an evil lot may not befall us</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> Jan. 10
         (arnan-tecma

          frithalid aslach diabuil . . . nach-for-ḟuca lais dia thig, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 1459.
          
        
      </p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">d</span>) in a few cases irregularly used at beginning of a main 
clause (see 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> ix 129-30
        ): ní ind fessin eirbthi ┐ nach dō duaisilbi nanní dogní 
 <span class="ediltr">and it is not to himself that he appropriates (ascribes</span>), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 51b12
         
(cop.).
           ocus noch [= nach] do cenel eile do hordaiged, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 
34.12 Comm.
          
         (cop.).</p>
    
  
  
    <p class="result">
