<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="32942">náire</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. náire or <a class="reference" href="32942">dil.ie/32942</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                náire</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype i&#x101;">iā</span>, <span class="gen f.">f.</span> (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 n&#xE1;r">1 nár</a></span>) náire <span class="gen m.">m.</span> and <span class="gen f.">f.</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Dec. § 1.
      
    </p>  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">shamefacedness</span>, <span class="def">bashfulness</span>, <span class="def">diffidence</span>, <span class="def">backwardness</span> or 
<span class="def">reluctance</span> (in undertaking someth.):</span> 
          nare . . . .i. in ruidiud tic 
isin gruaid ┐ is do sin is <span class="frn ">nomen</span> naire. Feile immorro ainim 
don einech bunaid, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm.Y" title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm. Y</strong></span> 983.
          
         
          ni hopair niad náre, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 
3275
          
         (i.e. distrust of his own strength, reluctance to fight). 

          Rúanaid atberthe co sse | frisseom ar met a náire, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 9580
          
         
(<span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> iii 204.12
         where Stokes reads náne `<span class="quote">splendour</span>' = 
áine?); the nickname Ruanaid (`<span class="quote">blusher</span>'?) was given to 
Diarmaid mac Aedha Sláine for his reluctance to expel 
Mochuda, <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> iii 465 § 35
         : 
          for in ruanaid (.i. hi ruidioth 
hoc gabail lama Mocutta), and, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BNn&#xC9;" title="eDIL Bibliography">BNnÉ</strong></span> 304 § 16
          
        : 
          dobēratt ind 
oicc aithis fort . . . .i. Diarmait Ruanaidh do radh riut. a náire 
fri maith, a nemnáire fri holc!, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 8228.
          
         
          araill dib ara 
n-uaisle saerthar iat, araill aile is a n-ecodnaigetu, araill aile 
is ara naire, araill aile is ara mire, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iii 112.14 Comm.
          
         (of 
certain hospitallers who are exempted from road-maintenance 
by reason of their nobility, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>; transld. `<span class="quote">some for the shame 
of it</span>'; for their diffidence or incompetence to undertake the 
task?). <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> also 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Keat.Poems" title="eDIL Bibliography">Keat. Poems</strong></span> 834
        : ní raibh stad ar theacht a 
thiodhlac | acht náire na dáimhe ré ndaoire (i.e. the only 
check on his bounty was the reluctance of his guests to put 
him to such expense).</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
<span class="trans">Feeling of <span class="def">shame</span> or <span class="def">humiliation</span>:</span> 
          do bhí náire mhór ar na 
fearaibh, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Bedel" title="eDIL Bibliography">1 Chron.</strong></span> xix 5.
          
         
          ag tabhairt náire dhon druing nach 
fuil ní aca, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/NT" title="eDIL Bibliography">1 Corinth.</strong></span> xi 22.
          
         <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">Shame</span>, <span class="def">disgrace</span> (in objective sense):</span> 

          fear táidhe . . . as é an náire mhór don mhnáoi, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Decl. ex. 
20
          
        . 
          náire ort, 
            19
          
        . athgabail aitire aslui feile .i. elas ar a nairi 
`<span class="quote">who absconds to his shame</span>' (?), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 214.25
        , 
          218.12
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) in concrete sense: tancatar immach in banmaccrad ┐ 
tuargbatar a nnochta ┐ a nnáre uile dó (i.e. their nakedness), 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 1361
         = 
          donnochtat [<span class="lbl">sic leg.</span>] a mbruindi fris, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-I1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-I¹</strong></span> 720.
          
        
      </p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">c</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">modesty</span>, sense of <span class="def">decorum</span>, <span class="def">nobility</span> of behaviour, <span class="def">generosity</span>
        </span> 
(approaching the sense of féile, from which it is distinguished 
by Cormac, see above; late and not common use) : do Gaidelaibh 
mo naire `<span class="quote">my honor</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BCC" title="eDIL Bibliography">BCC</strong></span> § 278
         (poem attrib. to Colum Cille) 
= 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MeasgraD." title="eDIL Bibliography">Measgra D.</strong></span> 46.30
        . tealach feile ┐ naire `<span class="quote">home of hospitality 
and generosity</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> ii 186.18
         = 
          t. feli ┐ garta, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> viii 102.18
          
        . 
<span class="lbl">Cf.</span> naire .i. glan, O'Cl.</p>
