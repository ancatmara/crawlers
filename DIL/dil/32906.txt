                nech, nacha, naich, na, nach, nacha, nacha, nach, nach mór</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">indef. pron. <span class="pos adj." style="color: #ff8000">adj.</span>, really a proclitic form of nech used 
before a noun (or adj. used substantivally) and before <span class="per 3">3</span> <span class="number s.">s.</span> and 
<span class="number pl.">pl.</span> <span class="case gen.">gen.</span> pron. <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 a&#xED;">1 aí</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=4 &#xE1;e">4 áe</a></span>. In <span class="number sing.">sing.</span> uninflected, except in <span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s">s</span>
      <span class="gen f.">f.</span> nacha 
(<span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s">s</span>
      <span class="gen m.">m.</span> naich occurs once in Glosses: 
        naich baill, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 5a5
        
      ; <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> 
Prol. 136
      ), inflexion in other cases being indicated by its effect 
on a follg. vowel.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
In <span class="case nom.">nom.</span> and <span class="case acc.">acc.</span> <span class="number sing.">sing.</span> <span class="gen neut.">neut.</span> O.Ir. uses the form na, geminating a follg. consonant (
      nach gl. quod, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 101a5
      , the <span class="gen neut.">neut.</span> being 
here a Latin word, is an emended reading); early in Mid.Ir. 
na gives way to nach.</p>
