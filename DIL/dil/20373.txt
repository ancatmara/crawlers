<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="20373">es(s)air</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. es(s)air or <a class="reference" href="20373">dil.ie/20373</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                easair, easair</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="gen f.">f.</span> (abstr. of as-ser-n-)</p>
   
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">1</span>)</p>
     
      
        <p class="result">
          <span class="edilb">(a)</span> <span class="trans">
            <span class="def">strewing</span>, <span class="def">litter</span>
          </span>; 
applied to <span class="trans">
            <span class="def">straw</span> or <span class="def">rushes</span> used for floor-covering, <span class="def">bedding</span>
          </span>, 
<span class="lbl">etc.</span>: easair `<span class="trans">
            <span class="def">litter</span> for <span class="def">cattle</span>, also a <span class="def">strawpallet</span>
          </span>' 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/P.OC." title="eDIL Bibliography">P. O'C.</strong></span>
           <span class="trans">`<i>the 
bed of corn-sheaves laid on the barn floor to be threshed</i> is also 
called easair'</span> J. Fleming (RIA slips). aín nua dia essair 
<span class="ediltr">new straw for covering it</span> (a wall) 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iv 314.7
          . dīrech 
n-airidi, noes nua dia esair <span class="ediltr">the stripping of a dais, new 
straw</span> (?) <span class="ediltr">to cover it</span> 
            314.3
           (wrongly punctuated in edn.). ro 
ésraít a tigi do bethi barrglas ┐ essair urárd úrluachra <span class="ediltr">litter 
of fresh rushes</span> 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TFerbe" title="eDIL Bibliography">TFerbe</strong></span> 66
          . 
            conaca M....in dairthach fo 
lán-essair glain, 
              <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 286a42
            
          . The ex.: 
            in tan do síned in 
beist a bragit cosin n-esair, 
              <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/FB" title="eDIL Bibliography">FB</strong></span> 57
            
           may belong to (<span class="edilb">2</span>), but 
the meaning <span class="trans">
            <span class="def">bedding</span>
          </span> would not be out of place. Of <span class="trans">
            <span class="def">lining</span>
for a <span class="def">grave</span> (?):</span> 
            ro hadnacht cen esair, 
              <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> iii 114.
71
            
           (see note 
            p. 499
           <span class="lbl">ib.</span>).</p>
      
     
      
        <p class="result">
          <span class="edilb">(b)</span> of <span class="trans">things strewn about in 
<span class="def">disorder</span>, an <span class="def">untidy</span> <span class="def">litter</span>, a <span class="def">mess</span>:</span> 
            ro athchuiredar a n-airm 
...┐ a cathberti...gurba hesair uathmar urscailti...cach laemluirech ┐ láighnead ┐ lebar-sciath ro ḟágsat...ar cert-lar 
na cath-laithrech sin, 
              <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MR" title="eDIL Bibliography">MR</strong></span> 314 y
            
          . 
            re himad na n-easrach 
n-uathmar n-urscailti, 
              316.18
            
          . <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
            o thictís a hesair áin, 

              <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> ii 78.14
            
          , where reading áig (with another 
copy) we might render (<span class="ediltr">slaughter</span>-)<span class="ediltr">heaps of battle</span>.</p>
      
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">2</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">food</span>, <span class="def">meat</span>
        </span> (the origin of this sense is obscure; some 
exx. suggest <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">dismembered</span> <span class="def">carcase</span>
        </span> which is on the whole the

          <span class="def">dish</span>
        </span> would usually suit, but unless 
the word is here a distinct one this cannot be an early 
meaning, but may be a later development): esair bidh do 
roind do cách (one of the labours permitted on a Sunday), 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OD." title="eDIL Bibliography">O'D.</strong></span> 1950
        . aighe do tuit don easair itir da tech <span class="ediltr">a joint

234.7 Comm.
         (<span class="quote">Unterlage</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xiv 346 x
        ). 
          ro fiafraig in 
ba herrlamh in esair,

         (referring to a

          narb 
ullamh cuid na meithli, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BCC" title="eDIL Bibliography">BCC</strong></span> § 212

          bai in esair aici

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Acall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Acall.</strong></span> 1118

            1115
          
        ). Here 
also, perhaps: 
          coin...ag gleic fán easair fhalaimh, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/D&#xE1;nfhocail" title="eDIL Bibliography">Dánfhocail</strong></span> 158

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OGr.Cat." title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Gr. Cat.</strong></span> 617

        </span> makes better
