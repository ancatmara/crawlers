        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> ii 
566
       comparing `deróil.' But Meyer brings the latter &lt; 
        de-ro-fóil, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wortk." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wortk.</strong></span> 176
        
      . In Mid. Ir. foróil gives way to foráil, 
furáil, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> possibly through influence of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=er&#xE1;il">eráil</a></span> (<span class="lbl">q.v.</span>) which

          fuláir foláir faláir furáil foráil faráil 
(guthaidhe leis orra),
