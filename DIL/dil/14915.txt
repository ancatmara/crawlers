    <p class="result">
      <span class="itype u">u</span>, <span class="gen m.">m.</span> <span class="pos vn." style="color: #ff8000">vn.</span> of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 dechraid">1 dechraid</a></span>.</p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
 <span class="edilb">I</span> <span class="trans">The state of being possessed by ungovernable <i>rage</i>, a 
being beside one's self, a <span class="def">raging</span>
        </span>. 
          conattech Cúchulainn blegon 
furri iarna dechrad díttaid, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 2415
          
         (<span class="lbl">v.l.</span> 
          dhéchradh, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/C&#xF3;irAnm." title="eDIL Bibliography">Cóir Anm.</strong></span> § 149
          
        ). for d.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
         <span class="ediltr">raging, raving</span>: 
          ┐ a tenga ar foluamain 
re mét in imgabáil...a Birgad atá do thenga ar dechrad ná 
habair acht fír, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Fianaig." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fianaig.</strong></span> 82. 28
          
        .</p>

 <span class="edilb">II</span> <span class="trans">Wild, ungovernable <span class="def">anger</span>, warlike <span class="def">rage</span>, vehement 
<span class="def">fury</span>
        </span>. Often in a good sense = <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">bravery</span>
        </span>. Only of persons.

joined to such words as borrfad, búrach, dásacht, déine, 
ferc, lonnas, luinne, lúthbás, tairpthige, and the like.—ní 
fúaircifesa and fer rosásad...a déni ┐ a thairpthigi ┐ a dechrad 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 359
