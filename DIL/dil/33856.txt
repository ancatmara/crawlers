<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="33856">1 ón</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 1 ón or <a class="reference" href="33856">dil.ie/33856</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                ón</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="itype indecl.">indecl.</span> <span class="gen neut.">neut.</span> <span class="number sing.">sing.</span> demonstr. <span class="pos pron." style="color: #ff8000">pron.</span>, lenited form of són

(&gt; 
        sodin, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> i 316
        
      ), and used in nearly the same way. <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">This</span>,

<span class="def">that</span>
      </span>, referring to someth. preceding, either a neuter noun or

(more often) a statement.</p>
   
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) generally with copula: commarí

far ṅdígal huaimse is coru dúib ón <span class="frn ">quam uindictam reddere</span>
        

<span class="ediltr">that your vengeance should proceed from me, that is meeter for

you</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 5d37
        . amal as do maccaib .i. iar forcitul ón <span class="ediltr">that is,

after teaching</span>, 
          16a14
         (gl. tanquam filiis). de iudeib ón, gl.

seductores qui de circumcisione sunt, 
          31b15
        . in testimni donuc

sum riam is do imthrenugud ón . . . as n-inunn tosach pre-

ceptae Iohain ┐ Íesu ( = his citation of the previous texts is

to prove that, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> i 486.37 
        (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Tur." title="eDIL Bibliography">Tur.</strong></span> 39
        ). tibit . . . ┐ do-

berat gáir dó. Bá do chuitbiud Loegairi ón <span class="ediltr">this was to deride

L.</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/FB" title="eDIL Bibliography">FB</strong></span> 64. 
        
        `Ni ba mor' ar F. `.i. mo breith latt do Emain

Macha . . .' Ba écen ón (i.e. it was necessary to comply with

the demand), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> i 106.17
        . 
          `Rop sén maith dūn' ol iat. . . .

`Ni pu sén maith itir ón', 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MR" title="eDIL Bibliography">MR</strong></span> 18.1
          
        . With npr. or pers. pron.

as pred. (= Fr. <span class="frn ">c'est</span>, Germ. <span class="frn ">das ist</span>): 
          `Inn é Cormac sút?' ar

cách. `Is é ón ém' ar Medb, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 185. 
          
        
        `Táet ferthigis fri

Máel Dúin' ol G. `Máel Dúin ón ém' ol sisi <span class="ediltr">so that is M.!</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span>
          1798
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> ix 488.9
        ).</p>
    
		
      <p class="result">Esp. common in phrases like: is dethbir, fír, inonn, refer-

ring to a foregoing statement or word: 
          ar ilib . . . ┐ ni ar na

huilib doínib. Deithbir ón, uair ní dona hulib doínib ro

tharmnaig césad Crist, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 5228. 
          
        
        `Fír dam-sa ón' bar C.

<span class="ediltr">I spoke the truth in that</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 1110. 
        
        
          ni bat ecra ind ḟir sin (is

inund ón ┐ ni deceltar dam), 
            445
          
         (a gloss incorporated in the

text). 
          `Elẏ Elẏ . . .' is inann on ┐ `a mo Dé', 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 3349.
          
        
      </p>
    
		
      <p class="result">Regularly used in connexion with the <span class="gen neut.">neut.</span> <span class="pos pron." style="color: #ff8000">pron.</span> ed: 
          is

hed ón as fír, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 13b23
          
        , <span class="lbl">cf.</span>

M.'s practice</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Mon.Tall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Mon. Tall.</strong></span> § 33. 
        
        mad ed ón <span class="ediltr">if that be so</span>, 
          § 56.
        

coica macc . . . ed ón ruc Eua do clainn [<span class="lbl">sic leg.</span>], 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MacCarthy" title="eDIL Bibliography">MacCarthy</strong></span>
          26.14
        . Is ed ón <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">that is so</span> becomes later a formula of assent = <span class="def">yes</span>
        </span>
(Mod.Ir. seadh): `Gaoth druadh sonn' ol D. `Asseadh on' ol A.


          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Leb.Gab.i" title="eDIL Bibliography">Leb. Gab.(i)</strong></span> i 256.5
        . 
          `Is alaind an n-inghen úd' ar A. `Ass edh

ón' ar C., 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BCC" title="eDIL Bibliography">BCC</strong></span> § 241. 
          
        
        Ed ón = Lat. <span class="frn ">id est</span>, see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 ed">1 ed</a></span>, and Thurneysen,

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 3009.
        
      </p>
    
		
      <p class="result">After the interrogatives cid, cindus: `Ced ón, a meic bic'

for C. `nad fetar . . .?' <span class="ediltr">what is that?</span> ( = what do you mean ?),


          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 930.

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TTebe" title="eDIL Bibliography">TTebe</strong></span> 3408
          
        .

that so ?</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Hib.Min." title="eDIL Bibliography">Hib. Min.</strong></span> 3.82
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">b</span>) with other verbs, as subj.: `Airg-siu dia guidi . . .'

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> i 74.15

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Mon.Tall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Mon. Tall.</strong></span> 34.

worse for me</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 1415.
        
      </p>
    
		
      <p class="result">As obj.: 
          nicon laimemmar-ni ón,

      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">c</span>) rarely referring to someth. following: nicon géb-sa ón

      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">d</span>) in Mid.Ir. the pronominal character of ón tends to dis-
