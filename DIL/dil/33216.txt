<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="33216">1 noch</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 1 noch or <a class="reference" href="33216">dil.ie/33216</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2013</span><br/></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">co-ordinating <span class="pos conj." style="color: #ff8000">conj.</span>, folld. by absol. form of vb., confined to O.Ir. and early Mid.Ir. periods. Some of the nuances below may not be justified, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> v 86
      . </p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) in the Glosses (Wb. and Sg., somet. in Ml.) used to introduce an admitted fact which is in contrast with or opposition 
to something preceding, <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">but</span>, <span class="def">however</span>, and <span class="def">yet</span>
        </span>; oft. strengthened 
by a particle like ém, immurgu: dodcad do chách leo-som 
noch is doib-som a dodced sidi <span class="ediltr">they deem (their wrong-doing) 
 a misfortune to all and yet the misfortune of it is their own 
(recoils on themselves)</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 2b3
        . 
          is follus isindí nachim-rindarpai-se noch ǽm am ísrahélde, 
            5a18
          
         (gl. numquid repulit 
Deus populum suum? absit; nam et ego Israhelita sum). 
          cid dia 
n-epir-som aní-siu? noch ni rabatar ind ḟir so riam fo recht 
fetarlicce, 
            19d11
          
         (gl. quomodo convertemini iterum ad infirma? 
a question addressed to the Galatians, implying that they had 
been formerly under the Law, though they really had never 
been). <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
          19c6
        . amal no bed noch ní fail <span class="ediltr">as though it were; 
but it is not</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 32a1
        . 
          noch ní cóir son acht corop . . . issed as 
chóir, 
            40b7
          
        . 
          amal bid tar ǽsi n-uilc huaim-se [<span class="lbl">sic leg.</span>] friu-som noch immurgu ní robae huaim-se a n-olc n-isin, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 74a2
          
         
(gl. quasi non inferrent mala sed redderent). <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
          38b2
        . 
          oent[ṡ]ēt coel tairiss, noch ba si a chonair, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xi 446.58
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TEm." title="eDIL Bibliography">Toch. Emire</strong></span> § 65
        ) = 
          ┐ ba si, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> iii 249.
          
         co ticceth ind ian beoss, 
noch ba trait donicceth, ba coirm aní no bith isin lothur <span class="ediltr">by the 
time the pitcher returned [from distributing ale], though it returned quickly, what was in the trough was brewed</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IrishTexts" title="eDIL Bibliography">Irish Texts</strong></span> 
i 7 § 23
        . adgladadar B. i[n] noidin . . . noch ni ragab labrad in 
tan sin <span class="ediltr">though at that time it had not begun to speak</span>, 
          14.28
        . 
dotet indala epscop do guidi dond rígh. `Noch ní tiber do 
neuch' ar ind rí <span class="ediltr">but I will grant [that boon] to none</span>, 
          11.1
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) in Ml. used generally with follg. cop. to introduce a 
synonym or explanation, <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">that is</span> to say</span> ( = Lat. <span class="frn ">id est</span>): noch is 
conaccertus-sa ón (gl. psalterium . . . emendaveram), 
          2a1
        . noch 
is ind foircthi són (gl. studiosi), 
          16b4
        . noch is asindet-som 
buith doib i ndoiri <span class="ediltr">that is, he declares them to be in captivity</span> 
(explaining the vb. Lat. <span class="frn ">inducit</span>), 
          108b6
        . lase no silaigid noch is 
asn-indid[id] a adamrae <span class="ediltr">when ye sow, that is, when ye declare 
his wonder</span>, 
          115b11
         (gl. adserendo, analysed as compd. of 
Lat. <span class="frn ">serere</span> `<span class="ediltr">to sow</span>'). <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> also 
          19a14
        , 
          23a20
        , 
          35b6
        , and the similar 
use of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=3 sech">sech is</a></span>.</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
In Laws (text) often seems to stand for an intensified <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">and</span>
        </span>
         <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Stud.inE.Ir.Law" title="eDIL Bibliography">Stud. in E. Ir. Law</strong></span> 54
        , 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> v 86
        : tobert a chin forsin fer batair 
bech, noch is i breth inso bretha la Ultu `<span class="quote">and this is the judgement</span>', 
        
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iv 178.15
        . ro suidiged . . . la log a tomalta . . . noch is ed 
log tomalta ro suidiged dō, set beisid fiu `<span class="quote">now the price of waste 
which has been fixed for it is a "<span class="frn ">sed</span>" of the same value</span>', 
           v 388 y
        . 
 <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
          376.3
        , 
          412.2
        . atait secht mna la Feine fris nā comalat fir 
noch ite ailde a m berda <span class="ediltr">with whom men do not co-nurse, but it is 
they who rear their own offspring</span>, 
          202.2
        . 
          ind noc[h] is rind, 
            12 y 
Comm.
          
         (the word `ind' = <span class="ediltr">point</span> is explained by the more 
familiar `rind'). ma scarid ┐ bid imtocad leo, noch bid commaithi a folaid <span class="ediltr">if [the married couple] separate and they have 
property and if their property is equally good</span>, 
          ii 388.13
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
Folld. by conjunct form of vb.: cuic seoit . . . ro midir 
Morand; noch fil tri seota cacha tratha ro follaigther <span class="ediltr">but there 
are three `seds' for every day that is neglected</span>, 
          i 102.7
        .</p>
