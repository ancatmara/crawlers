      <span class="itype &#x101;">ā</span>, <span class="gen f.">f.</span> (Lat. <span class="frn ">rēgula</span>) O.Ir. ríagol, -ul; later <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> riagail. <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> 

        riagol, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 32a2
        
      ,

      , 
        178a2

      <span class="number s.">s.</span> 
        riagoil, 
          9c22

      <span class="number s.">s.</span> <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number p.">p.</span> 
riaghla). In later Ir. somet. with <span class="itype guttural">guttural</span> st. in <span class="number pl.">pl.</span>
    </p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">A <span class="def">rule</span> (whether <span class="def">prescription</span>, law, tradition or custom):</span> 
used in a less definite and legal sense than dliged. In Sg. 
commonly of rules of grammar, orthography, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>: iar 
riagoil chenelaig aiccend <span class="trans">
          <span class="ediltr">the general rule of accents</span>
        </span>, 
          220a5
        . cia 
forcomam-ni riagoil sen-grec hi scríbunt (= <span class="frn ">scripturam antiquam servamus</span>), 
          9a22
        . 
          ní thabrat-som smacht no recht no 
ríagail ar denam a peccaid, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 2378
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> iv 250 § 16
        ). 
          is hí 
ind riaguil chert . . . fognad cách Dé mog, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> 4.15
          
        . 
          it é do 
theoir riagla . . . ainme ocus umaldóit, serc in C[h]oimded, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> i 194.7
          
        . riagail soscéli cluined ocus ríagail mín manach 
<span class="trans">
          <span class="ediltr">let him hear the rule of the Gospel</span>
        </span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> iii 100 § 25a
          
        . 
          is mairg 
ailter cen ríagail, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ACL" title="eDIL Bibliography">ACL</strong></span> iii 215 § 3
          
        . riagla naemh nEirenn 
d'ḟogluim (= <span class="frn ">regulas</span>), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Lat.Lives" title="eDIL Bibliography">Lat. Lives</strong></span> 105.10
        . 
          iar riagail na 
sruthi, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 5555
          
        . riagla na n-aínted <span class="trans">
          <span class="ediltr">rules for fasts</span>
        </span>, 
          5553
        . 

          riagail na Casc, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MacCarthy" title="eDIL Bibliography">MacCarthy</strong></span> 308.7
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BB" title="eDIL Bibliography">BB</strong></span> 13b
          
        ). 
          co riaglaib ic 
clerchib, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Cog." title="eDIL Bibliography">Cog.</strong></span> 140.12

          <span class="ediltr">the rules of 
war</span>
        </span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Content." title="eDIL Bibliography">Content.</strong></span> xvi 70
        . 
          do réir riaghlach an réasúin, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Eochairsg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Eochairsg.</strong></span> 
53.22
          
        . díogha a reachta is riaghla a ré (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> rejecting the laws 
and standards of their time), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Hackett" title="eDIL Bibliography">Hackett</strong></span> xlii 80
        . </p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
In Laws Comm. oft. used to gloss cáin, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> 
        
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iv 166.2
        , 
          v 
236.20
        , 
          292.9
        , 
          480.24
        . 
          do reir riaghla . . . in lanamnais, 
            ii 
342.29
          
        . As <span class="pos vn." style="color: #ff8000">vn.</span>: 
          anadh ┐ dithim do riagail uirri a haithle a 
dethbiriusa, 
            i 302.7
          
        . <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=r&#xED;agailt">ríagailt</a></span>.</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
Esp. of a monastic Rule laid down by the founder or head 
of a monastery: nach oen, ol Maol Rúoin . . . forcomedar mo 
riaguil <span class="trans">
          <span class="ediltr">who observes my Rule</span>
        </span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Mon.Tall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Mon. Tall.</strong></span> § 6
        . aon troscut i 
riagail Comgeild <span class="trans">
          <span class="ediltr">there is one fast in Comgall's Rule</span>
        </span>, 
          § 68
        . luid 
. . . co Roim do acallaim Grigair for cend uird ┐ riagla (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> 
regarding the rule of a monastery), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 3095
        . ? 
          Eri ogh, inis 
na naomh | co n-imad riaghal [<span class="lbl">sic leg.</span>] ro-caemh, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MacCarthy" title="eDIL Bibliography">MacCarthy</strong></span> 
408.12
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BB" title="eDIL Bibliography">BB</strong></span> 49b41
        ). Hence a techn. term for the metrical rules 
incorporating the precepts of a religious founder: 
          
            <span class="frn ">ut est</span> a 
Riaguil Ailbe, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ODav." title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Dav.</strong></span> 1003
          
        ; <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> iii 92 fg
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
Passing into sense of <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">rule</span>, <span class="def">authority</span>
        </span>: 
          cóic cét déc rigḟer atát 
fót ríagail, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 297a41
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SG" title="eDIL Bibliography">SG</strong></span> 364.34
        ). 
          fo riaguil ríg nime, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> 
Ep. 96
          
        . 
          co rāith cech recht asa riaghuil, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> viii 196.8
          
         
(prophecy of degenerate times: <span class="trans">
          <span class="ediltr">has lost its authority</span>
        </span>? or <span class="trans">
          <span class="ediltr">has

        </span>?). gébhuidh Banbha arís riaghuil <span class="trans">
          <span class="ediltr">will 
submit to rule or law</span>
        </span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Dec. ex. 697
        . iomchoruidh lámh 
an . . . dhúthrachtaigh ríaghail <span class="trans">
          <span class="ediltr">shall bear rule</span>
        </span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Bedel" title="eDIL Bibliography">Proverbs</strong></span> xii 24
        . 
<span class="lbl">Cf.</span> mac Riagaill ráin, ba riagail `<span class="quote">who was a ruler</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span>

        .</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
Of a rule of life, line of conduct, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>: ba hí seo riagol a 
chrabuid .i. no gebed in uli ṡalmu, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> (= <span class="frn ">quotidiana eius vita</span>), 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Lat.Lives" title="eDIL Bibliography">Lat. Lives</strong></span> 62.13
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LB" title="eDIL Bibliography">LB</strong></span> 29a66
        ). ar cruas a riagla ┐ a mbethad 
(of the Irish saints), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BCC" title="eDIL Bibliography">BCC</strong></span> § 155
        . 
          San Clára fa cruaidhe 
riaghail, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PBocht" title="eDIL Bibliography">PBocht</strong></span> 1 § 12

          <span class="ediltr">whiter its

        </span> (of a sheep changing colour),

         (<span class="lbl">leg.</span>

      <p class="result"> 
In loose sense: tri chét bliadan . . . tri riagla rímim (= I

          § 12
        .

      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) in concrete sense, <span class="trans">a <span class="def">rule</span>, <span class="def">measure</span>, <span class="def">line</span>

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CCath." title="eDIL Bibliography">CCath.</strong></span> 5847
