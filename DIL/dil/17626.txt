      <span class="pos v" style="color: #ff8000">v</span> According to Baumgarten, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xxxiv 190
      , the citations here belong to two separate verbs: 1 do-feid (&lt; <span class="frn ">di-fed-</span>) <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">leads</span>, <span class="def">escorts</span> (from one point to another); <span class="def">leads to</span>; <span class="def">brings about</span>
      </span>, for which see the examples under (<span class="edilb">a</span>), (<span class="edilb">c</span>) and (<span class="edilb">d</span>), and 2 do-feid (&lt; <span class="frn ">to-fed-</span>) <span class="trans">takes precedence over</span>, for which see the examples under (<span class="edilb">b</span>). Also do-feid <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">brings</span>, <span class="def">leads</span>
      </span>. In pret. and perf. this vb. 
is easily confused with <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=do-fo&#xED;di">do-foídi</a></span> <span class="ediltr">sends</span> and <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=do-feth">do-feth</a></span> (*to-feth-, 
*to-fo-eth); the <span class="per 3">3</span> <span class="number s.">s.</span> <span class="tns pret.">pret.</span> 
        dofaith, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Hy." title="eDIL Bibliography">Hy.</strong></span> ii 39.47
        
       is by 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> ii 514
      
referred to *to-fo-eth-, but by editors of Thes. treated as a 
trans. <span class="pos vb." style="color: #ff8000">vb.</span> (reading dafáith
      , dafáid).</p>
