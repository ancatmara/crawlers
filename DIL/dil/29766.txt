<span class="pos Vn." style="color: #ff8000">Vn.</span> léiciud.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
The principal meanings are (a) <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">lets</span> <span class="def">go</span>, <span class="def">releases</span>
      </span>, (b) <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">lets</span>, 
<span class="def">allows</span>, <span class="def">permits</span>
      </span>, (c) <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">leaves</span> (<span class="def">behind</span>), <span class="def">allows</span> to <span class="def">remain</span>.</span> These, 
together with a number of more specific meanings, are found 
independently (I), and also recur in a large variety of 
constructions in which prepositions and, to a lesser extent, 
prepositional phrases and adverbs either modify the meaning 
or define more closely the relation between the verb and its 
logical subject or object (II, III). Under II (as well as I) a 
number of constructions will be found in which the meaning 
is <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">goes</span>, <span class="def">betakes</span> <span class="def">oneself</span>, <span class="def">enters</span> <span class="def">upon</span>, <span class="def">begins</span>, <span class="def">proceeds</span>
      </span>, with or 
without reflexive pronoun, and also a number in which it is 
<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">throws</span>, <span class="def">hurls</span>
      </span> (do-léici).</p>
