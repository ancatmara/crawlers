    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos v" style="color: #ff8000">v</span> On the forms see Bergin, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Jn.Celt.Stud." title="eDIL Bibliography">Jn. Celt. Stud.</strong></span> i 183 ff
      . 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span>
Verbs § 35
      . <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> also 
        § 18
       and also <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Peritia" title="eDIL Bibliography">Peritia</strong></span> ix 122
      .</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
In O.Ir. the general meaning is</p>
   
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">is <span class="def">entitled</span> to, has a <span class="def">right</span> 
to, is <span class="def">owed</span> (as a <span class="def">debt</span>)</span>, passing into the kindred sense</p>
     
  
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">b</span>) 
 <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">deserves</span>, <span class="def">merits</span>
        </span>.</p>
     
  
    <p class="result">
      The construction is: dligim x do y <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">I am 
entitled to x</span> (thing) <i>from y</i> (person); <span class="def">y owes me x</span>; <span class="def">I deserve x 
from y</span>
      </span>; the corresponding pass. being: dlegair x do y <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">x is due 
from y</span>.</span> With Lat. dative (hence Ir. DO): 
        dlegair discipulis 
orare, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 26a32
        
      . In Mid. Ir. DO was confused with and 
ultimately supplanted by DE, the prep. being felt rather as = 
Lat. <span class="frn ">de</span>, <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> ii 347.41
      : amal rodlecht de misero <span class="ediltr">as (much 
as) was due from a poor fellow</span>.</p>

      <p class="result"> 
 <span class="edilb">II</span> <span class="voice Pass.">Pass.</span> 
          dlegair x do y
      </p>
     
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">x is <span class="def">due from</span> y; y <span class="def">owes</span> x</span>, (<span class="edilb">b</span>) <span class="trans">x is 
<span class="def">required of</span>, is <span class="def">incumbent on</span> y</span> (where x generally a vn. or 
clause). In O.Ir. the construction is felt to be personal, as is 
shown by the use of pl. form; later it comes to be impers., 
esp. in cases of type (b).</p>
