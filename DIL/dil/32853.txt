<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="32853">múraid</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. múraid or <a class="reference" href="32853">dil.ie/32853</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                múirid, múrad</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos v" style="color: #ff8000">v</span> 
      <span class="itype &#x101;">ā</span>, also múirid, <span class="itype i">i</span>.</p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) the orig. sense may be <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">levels</span>, <span class="def">makes level</span> (soil, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>), 
hence <span class="def">razes</span>, <span class="def">demolishes</span> (esp. of buildings)</span>. 
          cach tailach dara 
tictis na múrtis co fáctais ina foenglenntaib, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 263b48
          
         = 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MU" title="eDIL Bibliography">MU</strong></span> 14.27
         (`<span class="quote">levelled</span>'). 
          ro mursam a tulcha . . . co bfuilit ina 
bfanglentaibh, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> p. 825 n. 7
          
         (St.), <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          p. 711 n. 7
        . 
          múirfidhear cnuic na cruinne, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ir.Monthly" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ir. Monthly</strong></span> 1927, 261.15
          
        . muchum 
┐ murum in cathraigh <span class="ediltr">let us raze</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Aen." title="eDIL Bibliography">Aen.</strong></span> 3083.
         
          murait in 
dai[n]gin ┐ brisid in rigdae, 
            565
          
        . 
          múirfemait do tṡid ort, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Acall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Acall.</strong></span> 5078
          
         (`<span class="quote">we will wall up</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SG" title="eDIL Bibliography">SG</strong></span> ii 224
        , but <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          do thochailt 
ar sída oraind, 
            5136
          
        ). 
          múrfither bar múr ri miledaib Grec, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 239a16
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TTr." title="eDIL Bibliography">TTr.</strong></span> 1648
        ). 
          cor loisc ┐ cor mur Boroma, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xviii 
37.2
          
         (Tig.). 
          co ro loisc[s]et in indsi ┐ co rus-múrsat, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AU" title="eDIL Bibliography">AU</strong></span> ii 
148.19
          
        . 
          ro múrsetar Báirc ṁBresail, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xiii 40 § 10.
          
         
          cathair 
A. do múiretur ┐ d'airgetur Tuircínigh, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Maund." title="eDIL Bibliography">Maund.</strong></span> 132.
          
         cid . . . 
do muirthea in raith <span class="ediltr">though the fort were to be destroyed</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/YBL" title="eDIL Bibliography">YBL</strong></span> 
315b54
        . 
          gur muradh ┐ gur mughaighedh . . . in baile, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AU" title="eDIL Bibliography">AU</strong></span> iii 
586.1
          
        . In looser sense: nā muirter do mhuir an cill . . . co 
brath `<span class="quote">that the church should never be overwhelmed by the sea</span>', 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Misc.Hag.Hib." title="eDIL Bibliography">Misc. Hag. Hib.</strong></span> 17 § 16.
        
      </p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) in wider sense <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">destroys</span>, <span class="def">ruins</span> (of persons):</span> 
          a betha 
daenna, . . . sochaide ro múris!, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 4661.
          
         
          in carracc . . . do 
múiredh triar no ceathrar roimpi, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CCath." title="eDIL Bibliography">CCath.</strong></span> 1859.
          
         
          ro mursad 
cona cloind ┐ cona muintir uile he, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/YBL" title="eDIL Bibliography">YBL</strong></span> 179b4
          
         (`<span class="quote">walled in</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> 
xxiv 285.2
        ).</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">c</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">piles up</span>, <span class="def">builds up</span>, <span class="def">constructs</span>, only of constructing a 
<span class="def">mound</span> over a <span class="def">grave</span>:</span> 
          is ann ro foilged a corp; | fuirri ro múrad 
mór-chnocc, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> iii 40.4
          
        . 
          do múiredh in tuluch so air, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Acall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Acall.</strong></span> 7572
          
        , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          1073
        , 
          1085
        . 
          ro múiredh in tulach so thairrsi, 

            1257
          
        . 
          ro muired in fert, 
            2082
          
        . ? (fig.) gabais i n-allmuirib, 
mūras mōr ṅdēserce (of Colum Cille), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ACL" title="eDIL Bibliography">ACL</strong></span> iii 217 § 3
        ; <span class="ediltr">he 
heaped up much charity</span>? but text may be corrupt, perhaps 
= mórais.</p>
    
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
<span class="pos vn." style="color: #ff8000">vn.</span> múrad (múired) <span class="gen m.">m.</span>
    </p>
   
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) in strict sense `<span class="trans">act of <span class="def">levelling</span> 
the <span class="def">ground</span>
        </span>' (Laws, Gloss.): masa maidm talman na cumangar 
do lesugad trena muired no trena linadh `<span class="quote">by levelling it</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> 
iii 254.22 Comm.
         ar múrad talman ar Conán <span class="ediltr">after levelling</span> 
(? <span class="ediltr">heaping) the earth over C.</span> (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> <span class="ediltr">burying</span>), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Acall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Acall.</strong></span> 3985.
         Hence 
loosely with obj. gen. of person <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">burying</span>:</span> Tephi . . . mairg 
forsmélad a múrad `<span class="quote">her entombment</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> i 8.42
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) generally <span class="trans">the act of <span class="def">razing</span>, <span class="def">demolishing</span>:</span> 
          ro sailset muradh 
in daingin forna Troianaib ┐ a dilgend iar sin, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Aen." title="eDIL Bibliography">Aen.</strong></span> 2352
          
         
(where muradh seems = <span class="ediltr">levelling the walls</span>, oppd. to dílgend 
<span class="ediltr">utterly destroying</span>). <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
          2045
        . 
          do thogail ┐ loscud ┐ múrud 
cathrach Lamidóin, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 224a21
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TTr." title="eDIL Bibliography">TTr.</strong></span> 506
        ). 
          di milliud ┐ di 
múriud . . . na Troe, 
            227b15
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TTr." title="eDIL Bibliography">TTr.</strong></span> 765
        ).</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">c</span>) <span class="trans">exceptionally of <span class="def">building</span> (<span class="def">walls</span>), <span class="def">fortifying</span>:</span> 
          a catracha 
do murad ┐ do daingnedud, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BB" title="eDIL Bibliography">BB</strong></span> 434a27
          
        .</p>
