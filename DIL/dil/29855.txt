                2019</span><br/></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   


    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> (leth + scél) </p>
  

    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">a <span class="def">one-sided</span> or <span class="def">biased</span> <span class="def">story</span>; one <span class="def">side</span> of a <span class="def">dispute</span>
        </span>: 
          gan bhreith idir truagh is tréan | do bhreith ar luagh ná ar leithsgéal '<span class="quote">no judgement between the wretched and the powerful is tainted by bribe or bias</span>', 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> lviii 142 § 8
          
        . 
          go bhfeasadh luighe ar leithsgéal '<span class="quote">until he can distinguish between an oath and an accusation</span>', 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> lxiv 162 § 1.4
          
        . na beir luaithbreith ar leithscél <span class="ediltr">do not be quick to judge one side of the story</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Acall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Acall.</strong></span> 1414
        . 
          gach agra dá bhfuil againn ┐ gach leithscéal dá bhfuil agaibhsi, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MaguiresofF." title="eDIL Bibliography">Maguires of F.</strong></span> 74
          
        .</p>
    
  

    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">b</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">excuse</span>
        </span>: ag déanamh locht gan leithsgéal `<span class="quote">inexcusably</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/D&#xE1;nD&#xE9;" title="eDIL Bibliography">Dán Dé</strong></span> xxi 4
        . leithsgéul sonn re sesamh t'fhiach <span class="ediltr">an excuse for not paying you what I 
owe you</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/L.Cl.A.B." title="eDIL Bibliography">L. Cl. A. B.</strong></span> 163.45
        . lethsgéul . . . fa ghnóaighe nach édir do chor ar cáirde <span class="ediltr">make pressing affairs an excuse</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RSCl&#xE1;ra" title="eDIL Bibliography">RSClára</strong></span> 77a
          
        . In various constructions with gaibid: dá ngabhthar leo mo leithsgéal 
<span class="ediltr">if they accept my excuse</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/DD&#xE1;na" title="eDIL Bibliography">DDána</strong></span> 21 a. 23
        . 
          aighne géar nach géabha ar leithsgéal, 
            59.3
          
        . 
          gabhadh an lēaghthó[i]r leithsgéal an sgrīobhneóra, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/P&#xE1;rl.namB." title="eDIL Bibliography">Párl. na mB.</strong></span> 3218

        . níor ghabhais acht leith-sgéal lag `<span class="quote">you gave but a lame story about</span> . . .', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Content." title="eDIL Bibliography">Content.</strong></span> iv 57
