    <p class="result">(*
        to-cuir-, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> ii 501
        
      ), in O.Ir. a deponent <span class="pos vb." style="color: #ff8000">vb.</span> 
used in the <span class="trans">senses <i>(1)</i> <span class="def">puts</span> and <i>(2)</i> <span class="def">invites</span>
      </span>. For the development of meaning (2) see Gaidoz, <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xxiii 91
      . In O.Ir. (1) occurs chiefly in 3 s. , often in impers. construction; in subj. and pret. the root la- is used side by side with cuir- and the 
 fut. is supplied by *to-(fo)cherr- (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 cuirid">1 cuirid</a></span>); in (2) all forms are supplied by *to-cuir-. In later Mid.Ir. *to-cuir- comes to be represented by tochraid (tachraid), treated as a simple vb. and often forming an ē-fut. (see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 5707
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Cog." title="eDIL Bibliography">Cog.</strong></span> 225.18
      = 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 309b3
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Acall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Acall.</strong></span> 1137
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Aen." title="eDIL Bibliography">Aen.</strong></span> 290
      ), while dorala or tarla (the 
former of which survives as late as the 17th cent.) exists 
independently in the sense <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">met</span>, <span class="def">happened</span>
      </span>. The form tachraid 
may be regarded as a lineal descendant of do-cuirethar, 
dochuir[id], -tochraid rather than as a distinct denom. vb. 
formed from the vn. tochur, tachar, the syncopated form 
(common already in Mid. Ir.) being probably due to the need 
for distinguishing it from tochuirid <span class="ediltr">invites</span>, in which syncope 
does not as a rule occur. See 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> § 762
      . 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span>
Verbs § 95
      . </p>
