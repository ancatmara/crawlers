        deach .i. siolla,

      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">b</span>) as deg-ḟuach: 
          deach... 
degfuach nó defuach dano a hinni, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BB" title="eDIL Bibliography">BB</strong></span> 321 b 32
          
        .—Deach 
possibly an old derivative of dá (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> dé-de and dé- in cpds.) 
and originally applied only to a verse-foot of two syllables 
(
          comrac dá sillab, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span> <span class="lbl">l.c.</span>
          
        ). Deach <span class="lbl">i.e.</span> disyllabic 
dĕ-äch with shortening of dē- in hiatus. Mir. déch.</p>
