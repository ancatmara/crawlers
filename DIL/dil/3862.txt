<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="3862">apaid</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. apaid or <a class="reference" href="3862">dil.ie/3862</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2019</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                aptha</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
  
  
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> (? ad + buith, see Greene, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> ii 339
      ). Also aptha. 
<span class="trans">
        <i>act of</i> <span class="def">harbouring</span> <i>an escaped criminal or allowing him to 
remain on one's land</i>:</span> 
        mesir leptha ar leth, mesir aptha ar 
trian, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xiii 41.31
        
       `<span class="quote">It seems that <span class="frn ">leptha</span> and <span class="frn ">aptha</span> denote 
degrees of responsibility for sheltering or otherwise aiding 
and abetting a tribeless man who has committed some offence, 
and that <span class="frn ">leptha</span> denotes the graver and <span class="frn ">aptha</span> the lighter 
responsibility</span>', 
        230
      ; <span class="lbl">cf.</span> Comm. in 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> ii 178.z
       (on the words 
of the text o leaptha no aptha): 
        cret is lepuid ar leith no 
is aptha ar trian do na deor[ad]uib ┐ do na murchuirthi[b], 
uair nocha nfuil fine aca budein? Isedh am is aptha ar 
trian doib, fine i nurruidh ar ataitt griun. Issedh is leptha 
ar leith imurro cach fine otha sin amach. mac huar . . . co 
n-oponar a lepaith ┐ a apuith, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xiii 23.13
        
       = 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 57
       
(H 3.18, 20b), <span class="lbl">i.e.</span> a son in respect of whom his father has 
given notice that he is not to be sheltered, Plummer MS 
notes (`<span class="quote">so dass untersagt wird, ihm Lager oder Wohnung zu 
gewähren</span>', 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/B&#xFC;rgschaft" title="eDIL Bibliography">Bürgschaft</strong></span> 11
      ). 
        ma ainfine robe ina gnais is 
lepa ┐ ma fine is apa; ar is apa la fine ┐ leba la hainfine. 
meiser lebtha ar leth, meiser aptha ar trian, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 2548
        
       (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Eg.88" title="eDIL Bibliography">Eg. 88</strong></span>, 46(47)a
        
      ). 
        cisne lesa dona ibh sen tascnat treise? . . . 
focra naptha, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 230.z
        
      . 
        .i. smacht biata do mic bithbinigh tar apadh, 
          234.2 Comm.
        
       <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 808
       (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span>, 
373b
        
      ): cumal focrai naptha .i. foccrai leptha la apuid. 
cisne tri biada na tuillead leaptha na hapta cidbe ro caithe? 
<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> three kinds of refection which do not incur the fines 
for these two kinds of harbouring, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 438.14
      . apa 
sceo leaba faonan comlaithe coir <span class="ediltr">these two kinds of harbouring 
are liable to similar adjustments</span>(?), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 2531
       (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Eg.88" title="eDIL Bibliography">Eg. 88</strong></span> , 
44(45)b
        
      ), where Plummer MS notes suggests reading comlaithre and translates `apa' and `leba' <span class="ediltr">alike constitute conspiracy</span>. 
        direnar .i. nach duine direnar cin biid accradh inna 
apuidh no a lepaidh, 
          483
        
       (H 3.18, 251). See also <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=b&#xE1;napaid">bánapaid</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=dergapaid">dergapaid</a></span>.</p>
