<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="41872">trí</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. trí or <a class="reference" href="41872">dil.ie/41872</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2019</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                tri, trí, tre, tri, tri, trí, trí, tri, tri, trib, teoir, teora, teora, teoraib, teoraib, tri, trí, teora, teora, teora, trí, trib, i trí, teóra, trí</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos num." style="color: #ff8000">num.</span> <span class="pos adj." style="color: #ff8000">adj.</span> <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">three</span>
      </span>. Usually spelt tri in the O. Ir. Glosses, 
but trí when absolute (see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> § 386
      ). Archaic 
tre (Camb., <span class="lbl">etc.</span>). Like <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=d&#xE1;">dá</a></span> does not prevent nasalisation of 
a following noun after pl. poss.: a tri nglantae <span class="ediltr">their three 
cleanings</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm.Bodl." title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm. Bodl.</strong></span> 38.13
      . See 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xi 148.9
      .</p>
