<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="32400">1 mo</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 1 mo or <a class="reference" href="32400">dil.ie/32400</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                mu, -sa, -su, -sea, -siu, mu, mú, Mo</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   


    <p class="result">
      <span class="case gen.">gen.</span> of <span class="per 1">1</span> <span class="number s.">s.</span> pers. <span class="pos pron." style="color: #ff8000">pron.</span>, used only before subst. as proclitic, 
both as poss. and as obj. gen. (<span class="lbl">e.g.</span> iar mu soirad <span class="ediltr">after delivering me</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 104d2
      ); often emphasized by the enclitic -sa, -su 
or (after palatal vowel or const.) -se, -sea, -siu follg. the subst. 
The form mu is common in Ml.: 
        mú bás, 
      
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 80a9
      ; 
        ar mu sémi-se, 

          22d1
        
      ; 
        mu menma, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SP" title="eDIL Bibliography">SP</strong></span> ii § 1
        
      ; 
        mu lebran, 
          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> § 2
        
      ; 
        for mu mud, 

          § 8
        
      . Lenites a follg. consonant; the lenition is generally expressed in the case of c, t: 
        mu charat, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 73d1
        
      ; 
        tar mu chenn, 

          76d9
        
       (but: 
        mu cenn, 
          80b2
        
      ); 
        ar mu chnamaib-se, 
          58a11
        
      ; 
        mu 
thire, 
          62a7
        
      ; 
        mu th[u]aithe-si, 
          34b6
        
      ; in the case of other consonants occasionally in Mid.Ir. texts (not in O.Ir.): 
        dom 
ghalar, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> i 124.8
        
      ; mo ṡerc, <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 
        10
      ; 
        mo phopa, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CRR" title="eDIL Bibliography">CRR</strong></span> 3
        
      ; 
        mo 
pheccaid, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 7359
        
      ; 
        mo dherbrathair, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xii 393.36
        
      ; 
        mo 
Dhúilem, 
          394.1
        
      ; 
        mo tsethracha, 
          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 2
        
       (ts = ṡ). Before lenited 
f the usage varies: moisitiu (= m'ḟoísitiu), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 46b12
      ; 
        m[ḟ]eulae, 

          47c4
        
      ; 
        m[ḟ]ithisi, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> 76.9
        
      ; but: 
        do ól mo flede, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/FB" title="eDIL Bibliography">FB</strong></span> 6.
        
      
    </p>

lenited f, but occas. persists:

    <p class="result"> 
Affixed to a preceding prep., the vowel is dropped before an 
initial vowel or lenited f, commonly before consonants also; 
the letter m is freq. doubled, showing that it never suffered 
lenition:

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Hy." title="eDIL Bibliography">Hy.</strong></span> vi 8

    <p class="result"> 
After the particle a folld. by a voc. case, mo remains unlenited: 
        ammo phopa, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 1106

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> ii 
122 § 62
        
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ACL" title="eDIL Bibliography">ACL</strong></span> iii 293 § 1.
      
    </p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
In late language m' is occas. lenited before an initial vowel: 

        mh'occlach . . . mh'oircinneach . . . m'umhla nó mh'urraim, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BNn&#xC9;" title="eDIL Bibliography">BNnÉ</strong></span> 266 § 234.
        
      
    </p>
  
  
    <p class="result">

(usually a hypocoristic form), esp. with names of saints, such 
forms coming in time to be treated as a single word and often 
replacing the orig. name of a saint in popular usage. See
