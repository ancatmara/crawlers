<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="33147">3 ní</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 3 ní or <a class="reference" href="33147">dil.ie/33147</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                ní-a-, ní</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos adv." style="color: #ff8000">adv.</span> of negation <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">not</span>
      </span>, used with vbs. For use as copula see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 is">is</a></span>.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
Folld. by conjunct form of vb. In O.Ir. sometimes geminates initial consonant of vb.: 
        ni-rrobe, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 14c31
        
      . 
        ni-ssluinde, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 66b18
        
      . <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> ni ténat (= ní ddénat), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 24a25
      , 
and the cop. forms nítam, níta, nítat, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>, where t = unlenited 
d (see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> i 4
      ).</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
Infixes obj. pron.; with <span class="per 3">3</span> <span class="number s.">s.</span> <span class="gen m.">m.</span> and <span class="gen n.">n.</span> pron. ní-a- &gt; ní, 
the gender of the pron. being indicated by treatment of 
follg. initial of the vb., <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> ní beir <span class="ediltr">does not carry</span>; ní-mbeir 
(= ní-an-beir) <span class="ediltr">does not carry him</span>; ní bheir (= ní-a-bheir) <span class="ediltr">does 
not carry it</span>. 
        ni-mbaí suidi, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 6399
        
       (<span class="case d">d</span>
      <span class="number s">s</span>
      <span class="gen m.">m.</span>), <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        6384
      . ni-n-anais <span class="ediltr">thou wilt not save him</span>, 
        5999
      . 
        nis-gluais, 
          5107
        
       (<span class="case a">a</span>
      <span class="number s">s</span>
      <span class="gen f.">f.</span>). ni 
chēl <span class="ediltr">I will not hide it</span>, 
        2886
      . 
        ní cheil, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 5b5
        
       (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        ní ceil, 
          4d16
        
      , 
without pron.). ní thabur dit ón <span class="ediltr">I give not this to thee</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 179a2
      . 
nir-tora diliu <span class="ediltr">will not reach us</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 2747.
      
    </p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
In Mid.Ir. ní seems to lenite the initial cons. of follg. 
active vb., in MSS. generally expressed in case of tenues: 
        ni 
chēlat a márgnima, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 8953
        
       (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/FB" title="eDIL Bibliography">FB</strong></span> 71
      ). 
        ni choemstais, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 932.
        
       

        ni chuingem flaith, 
          1219
        
      . 
        ni ḟarggaib múr, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CRR" title="eDIL Bibliography">CRR</strong></span> 4.
        
       
        ni ḟil, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 59.
        
       
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 1766.
       
        ni thanic, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 2556.
        
       
        ni tharda fornn digail, 

          2727
        
      . 
        ni therna, 
          5070
        
      . 
        ní thairmescad, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 8566
        
       (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/FB" title="eDIL Bibliography">FB</strong></span> 42
      ). So 
with pass. vb., contrary to later usage: 
        ni thardad, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 9169
        
       
(
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/FB" title="eDIL Bibliography">FB</strong></span> 90
      ), <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        ní trebtar, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> Prol. 176
        
       (threbthair <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>).</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
Occas. prefixes h- to a follg. initial vowel: 
        ni haithgēn, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 
1689.
        
       
        ni himmēr, 
          1541
        
      . 
        ní himmertha, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 2874.
        
      
    </p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
Folld. by ro- form of vb., níro often becomes nír: 
        nir 
leicsetar, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 6421.
        
       
        nir fhétsat, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 2079
        
      , <span class="lbl">etc.</span>
    </p>
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
Used (<span class="edilb">a</span>) to negate a vb. in an independent sent. with all 
forms of indic. mood.</p>
     
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) with the subj. in an independent sent. to express desire 
or command (in place of the imper. with ná): ní intamlae, gl. 
ne aemuleris, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 56c13
        . ní dene (ne facias), 
          56c15
        . 
          ni taibre 
grád for nech, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 29a22
          
        . ni dérsid for soíri (ne libertatem . . . 
detis), 
          20b10
        . ním-thomoldid <span class="ediltr">do not goad me</span>, 
          20d4
        . 
          ni imthesid 
cen imdéicsin, 
            22c4
          
        . 
          ní dechais . . . co ríg nIsrahél, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 5951
          
        , 
 <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 3739.
         
          niro . . . oslaice dorus na huamu-sa ┐ niro urchoite 
do nech, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 352.
          
         
          niro techta occut feirg, 
            4094
          
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">c</span>) exceptionally in some other cases. Introducing a dependent noun-clause, instead of ná (nach): 
          is follus . . . ni 
dligthi comardad fris, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/FB" title="eDIL Bibliography">FB</strong></span> 41
          
        , where, however, the second sent. 
may be regarded as in apposition to follus. 
          asbertatar . . . ní 
thardad in cauradmir, 
            90
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 9169
        ). ispert sí ni targad leó 
 <span class="ediltr">that she would not go with them</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Fianaig." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fianaig.</strong></span> 6.16
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
In a few cases in early poetry ní folld. by inf. dat. pron. 
seems to take the place of the subst. vb.: nim-huain <span class="ediltr">I have 
no leisure</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 1185.
         ar nin-fissid frisbered omnu huain `<span class="quote">for 
not to us is the Knower</span>' (= <span class="ediltr">we have no longer the Knower)</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ACC" title="eDIL Bibliography">ACC</strong></span> 
16
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xx 164
        ).</p>
