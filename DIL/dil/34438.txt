<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="34438">poind, poinn</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. poind, poinn or <a class="reference" href="34438">dil.ie/34438</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2013</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                puinn, poing</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="gen f.">f.</span> (&lt; Lat. <span class="frn ">pondus</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Risk" title="eDIL Bibliography">Risk</strong></span> 595
      ), mod. puinn.</p>
   
    
      <p class="result">Lit. `<span class="trans">
          <span class="def">a point</span>
        </span>', hence (<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">a <span class="def">point</span> (of <span class="def">advantage</span>, <span class="def">importance</span>, 
<span class="def">interest</span>), <span class="def">something</span> <span class="def">important</span>:</span> ardfíugra ┐ foiscthe . . . iat-sum uli, is hí a hoenur in phóind ┐ in fhírinde (i.e. the substance, 
reality, of the Mass as opposed to Jewish sacrifices), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LB" title="eDIL Bibliography">LB</strong></span> 
257a66
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result">Hence <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">profit</span>, <span class="def">avail</span>
        </span> (freq. in neg. sent.): ni bid . . . poind don 
teiched sin <span class="ediltr">such flight was of no avail</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LB" title="eDIL Bibliography">LB</strong></span> 156b34
        . 
          cathaigset 
. . . ┐ ni boi poind doib ann, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 7223
          
        . ica fhiafraigid de cidh 
poind boi dhó dia loingius <span class="ediltr">what he had gained by his voyage</span>, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Lism.L." title="eDIL Bibliography">Lism. L.</strong></span> 3720
        . <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BNn&#xC9;" title="eDIL Bibliography">BNnÉ</strong></span> 64.8
        . 
          d'fhaicsin a mbláith—beag 
an phoinn!, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MeasgraD." title="eDIL Bibliography">Measgra D.</strong></span> 61.18
          
         (Gilla Brighde Mac Conmidhe, 13th cent.). créd do poinn? <span class="ediltr">what avails it?</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IrishTexts" title="eDIL Bibliography">Irish Texts</strong></span> ii 
p. 13.5
        . 
          beag do phoinn, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/D&#xE1;nD&#xE9;" title="eDIL Bibliography">Dán Dé</strong></span> xxv 40
          
        . </p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">b</span>) folld. by part. gen. or DE, <span class="trans">a <span class="def">particle</span>, <span class="def">small</span> <span class="def">amount</span>, <span class="def">minimum</span>
        </span>, nearly always in neg. sent. or after prep. cen: puinn 
éisg ni gabhthar ar eass <span class="ediltr">hardly any fish is caught</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> v 142.20
        . 
cen phóind tarba dō ann <span class="ediltr">without any gain to himself from it</span>, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LB" title="eDIL Bibliography">LB</strong></span> 145b58
        . cor soidhsettar na heachraidh isin mhuir . . . gan 
poinn dia ttarba ga n-erradhaibh <span class="ediltr">without their owners getting 
any profit of them</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BNn&#xC9;" title="eDIL Bibliography">BNnÉ</strong></span> 325 § 43
        . fonn . . . gin puinn toirthe 
<span class="ediltr">without any fruits</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MarcoP." title="eDIL Bibliography">Marco P.</strong></span> 11
        . 
          síth do denam etarra . . . gan 
poind do mhilliudh 'sa tír, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AU" title="eDIL Bibliography">AU</strong></span> iii 552.27
          
        . Absol. adubairt . . . 
nach raibe poind air <span class="ediltr">that there was nothing wrong with him</span>, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Acall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Acall.</strong></span> 7934
         (`<span class="quote">that he felt no whit ashamed</span>', O'Grady). Hence 
used after ní as an <span class="pos adv." style="color: #ff8000">adv.</span> (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> Fr. <span class="frn ">ne . . . point</span>): cóig glanchroinn 
ris nach gráin poing
         `'<span class="quote">neath which I need not fear much</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/D&#xE1;nD&#xE9;" title="eDIL Bibliography">Dán Dé</strong></span> xxv 59
        . ni teit gaoth puint (nó pint) tar gach madhg <span class="ediltr">no 
wind crosses the seas</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> v 487.2
         (B. na f.). ar stát don nuadh 
ní bhuaidhfeadh pearsa poinn <span class="ediltr">no one could surpass the pomp 
of the upstart</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xD3;Bruad." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ó Bruad.</strong></span> ii 20 y
        . 
          do thál air bhasdard nách feadair cé dīobh poīnn ē, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> iv 107 § 1
          
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result">
        <span class="trans">Rarely positive, an <span class="def">amount</span>
        </span>: ith do phuinn gu hailghen 
`<span class="quote">eat your portion cheerfully</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> v 134.73
         (<span class="lbl">leg.</span> phruinn = 
phroinn?). do chrú na gceap . . . d'fhéadadh poinn <span class="ediltr">of the stock 
that had much power</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xD3;Bruad." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ó Bruad.</strong></span> ii 202.8
        . poinn `<span class="quote">much, a great 
deal</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/P.OC." title="eDIL Bibliography">P. O'C.</strong></span>
        
      </p>
