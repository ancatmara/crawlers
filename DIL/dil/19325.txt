    <p class="result"> 
The O.Ir. quantity of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 &#xE9;">1 é</a></span> before the suffix is attested 
by the rime 
        glēsom : hēsom, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> iii 44 § 60
        
      . Thurneysen 
remarks (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thurn." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thurn. Hdb.</strong></span> § 447
      ) that the length of the vowel in 
ésom may be due to the influence of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=2 &#xE9;">2 é</a></span> which, to judge 
from Welsh <span class="frn ">wy</span>, is long by nature. In late Mid.-Ir. however the vowel is regularly short before the strengthening 
or anaphoric suffixes. The shortening may be explained 
by the analogy of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=3 mes(s)e">3 mes(s)e</a></span>, tussu s.v. <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=t&#xFA;">tú</a></span>.</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
A detailed examination of the use of the independent 
pron. in Old and Mid. Ir. belongs to syntax and would be 
out of place here ; we give however some illustrations of 
the typical uses for these periods. Some special uses are 
noticed in IX.</p>
