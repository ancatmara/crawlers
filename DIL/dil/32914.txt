<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="32914">1 nád</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 1 nád or <a class="reference" href="32914">dil.ie/32914</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                nach, nád, nár, nad, nach</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span style="color: #ff8000">rel.</span> <span class="pos particle" style="color: #ff8000">particle</span> used in neg. sentences, also written nát, nad, 
nat ; used in O.Ir. and earlier Mid.Ir. for <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=2 n&#xE1;">2 ná</a></span> in certain position (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thurn." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thurn. Hdb.</strong></span> § 779
      , 
        § 853
      , 
        § 854
      ), later superseded by nach. 
With ro- forms of vb. both 
        nád ro and nár are used.</p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) in <span class="case nom.">nom.</span> relation (leniting follg. initial) : 
          dund óis nad 
chaithi cach tuari, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 6c11
          
        . 
          is dreecht díib nad ro chreit, 
            5c2
          
        . 
is crann crín nad déni thoil ind ríg <span class="ediltr">he who doth not the will of 
the king</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> ii 294.28
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SP" title="eDIL Bibliography">SP</strong></span> iv 1
        ). 
          féil fir nád char corplén, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> 
June 22
          
        . 
          nach ní . . . nad rabi i firinne na nEbraide, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Hib.Min." title="eDIL Bibliography">Hib. Min.</strong></span> 
10.337
          
        . 
          in firion atcobra na huile beatusa . . . ┐ nad n-accombra 
ind-ulcc, 
            13.431
          
         (eclipsis is unusual in sentences of this type). 

          in fhairend nat carut in tsualaig-se, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 5929
          
         (elsewhere ná and 
nach are used in PH).</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
With copula : ní nad dír <span class="ediltr">what is not fitting</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 25a6
        . indí 
nád ní libsi <span class="ediltr">that which ye deem nothing</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 8a18
        . ro selgatar 
rótu | nád soréid la báethu [<span class="ediltr">a thing] which fools deem not easy</span>, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> Prol. 30.
         
          naímdemail nad charddemail sain, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 6010.
          
         
ate donadmand . . . nadad cora do nadmaim (<span class="lbl">sic leg.</span>) `<span class="quote">which 
are not rightfully bound</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iv 60.15
        . 
          tri deirg-mireanda 
naidet usa a mbelaib cacha hecalsa, 
            v 430.22
          
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
Exceptionally with inf. pron. (instead of nach) : la nech 
nadid-chreti <span class="ediltr">that does not believe it</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 15b14
        . <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> a dó . . . 
nada-scribthar <span class="ediltr">which are not written</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Hib.Min." title="eDIL Bibliography">Hib. Min.</strong></span> 8.247
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) in acc. relation : duthluchetar ní nad tardatis <span class="ediltr">something 
they had not given</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 85d5
        . in ní nad cumcu-sa ? <span class="ediltr">is it a thing

          18b5
        . a nat denat ar magistir <span class="ediltr">what our masters do 
not</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 15d6
        . 
          ní nad roichet Ulaid uli rosoichi-siu th'óenur, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/FB" title="eDIL Bibliography">FB</strong></span> 11.
          
         mor seol suilig, mor sreth séis | nad róig tuirim <span class="ediltr">which 
reckoning reaches not</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 512

          mor cet ceol nat roich tuirem,

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> Feb. 6.

from any one else</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 3742.
        
      </p>
