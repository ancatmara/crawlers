<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="41529">trá</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. trá or <a class="reference" href="41529">dil.ie/41529</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                .t., thrá, tra, thra, tráth, t., trá, tráth</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos adv." style="color: #ff8000">adv.</span> and <span class="pos conj." style="color: #ff8000">conj.</span> (See 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> p. 558
      , 
        p. 673

Written <span class="frn ">plene</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BCr." title="eDIL Bibliography">BCr.</strong></span> 3d
        
      : anísiu t.<span class="oRef">¤</span> is écen remfuar bissext ┐ 
emblesim `<span class="quote">this, then, is necessary, to prepare bisext and 
embolism</span>.' Usually contracted to .t. in O.Ir. Glosses. 
Later when written fully the forms thrá, tra, thra occur. 
Also occasionally tráth. That t.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
       is a fully stressed wd. is 
shown by the rhymes: 
        tra: chombartsa, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> ii 292.1

        trá: lá, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/DD&#xE1;na" title="eDIL Bibliography">DDána</strong></span> 2.13

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TD" title="eDIL Bibliography">TD</strong></span> 1.9
      .</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
With regard to its position in the sentence, the use of 
t.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
       corresponds to that of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=dano">dano</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=didiu">didiu</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=immurgu">immurgu</a></span> <span class="lbl">etc.</span> (see these 
words), and these are commonly interchanged with it in 
Mid. Ir. MSS., <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> marbthair dōib dano in mucc (tra, Hl.), 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sc.M2" title="eDIL Bibliography">Sc.M²</strong></span> 5

principal stressed word in the sentence. The commonest

    <p class="result"> 
In addition to emphasising the word it follows trá usually 
has a conjunctive or adversative sense with reference to 
what has gone before.</p>
   
    
      <p class="result">
