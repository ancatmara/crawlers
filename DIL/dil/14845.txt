    <p class="result"> 
The rhymes with menman and celebrad (<span class="lbl">v. supra</span>) do not 
necessarily prove original short -e-. Débroth <span class="trans">the <span class="def">doom</span> of 
<span class="def">God</span>
      </span> would be an ordinary cpd. of dia and bráth (u- stem), 
the latter passing into -broth in enclisis as scáth &gt; scud 
(foscud, <span class="case d">d</span>
      <span class="number sg.">sg.</span>), gním &gt; gnom. Mo d.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
      , dar mo d.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
       <span class="ediltr">by the doom 
that God will pass on me on the day of Judgment</span>. It seems 
the Irish equivalent of W. <span class="frn ">`min doiu braut,'</span> not an adaptation of it. In `mo d.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
      ,' debroth is probably the <span class="case dative">dative</span>.</p>
