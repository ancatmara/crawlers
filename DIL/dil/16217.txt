    <p class="result"> <span class="itype &#x101;">ā</span> <span class="gen f.">f.</span> (<span class="pos vn." style="color: #ff8000">vn.</span> of <span class="frn ">do-feid (&lt; di-fed-)</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xxxiv 190-92
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/UR" title="eDIL Bibliography">UR</strong></span> 118
      ) <span class="trans">act of <span class="def">leading (to)</span>, <span class="def">bringing (about)</span>
      </span>: 
        mescetlach .i. in marcach duaine, ut est ar ding (? leg. didin) a fir mescetlaig .i. bis a cumas a chetuil, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CIH" title="eDIL Bibliography">CIH</strong></span> iv 1515.10
        
      . cía fili ar secht cumala -dlig ara dínsium, ara díguin, ar thothlu a ṡét i téol ┐ táidiu, ar thlenamain dia chiunn a chétmuintire, ar dídin a ḟir meschétlaig muintire for drochbésu? '<span class="quote">who is the poet who is entitled to seven <span class="frn ">cumal'</span>s for his being insulted, for violation of his protection, for the stealing of his chattels openly or secretly, for abducting his wife in disregard of him, for leading the member of his household who is competent to recite into bad practices?</span>' 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/UR" title="eDIL Bibliography">UR</strong></span> 102 § 3
