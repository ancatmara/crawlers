      <span class="itype u">u</span> and later <span class="itype o">o</span>, <span class="gen m.">m.</span> (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=2 othar">2 othar</a></span>) <span class="case g">g</span>

      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">the state of being <span class="def">ill</span>, <span class="def">illness</span>, passing into sense being

nursed, treated, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> during <span class="def">illness</span>
        </span>; distinguished from `<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=galar">galar</a></span>':

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 296.27
        , 
          28 Comm.
         (strictly `galar' is used rather of the

malady, `othrus' of the resulting confinement). ra crechtnaigit

. . . co mbátar i n-othrus cach óen ná erbailt díb fochetóir,


          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 242b37
