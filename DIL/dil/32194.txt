      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype &#x101;">ā</span>, <span class="gen f.">f.</span>, and <span class="itype o">o</span>, <span class="gen m.">m.</span> <span class="case v">v</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> 
        midlach, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/FB" title="eDIL Bibliography">FB</strong></span> 67
        
       (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 8878
      ). 
<span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> midlaige; 
        midlaig, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 6157
        
       = 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-I1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-I¹</strong></span> 1672.
       The form 
midlaech (due to association with <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=l&#xE1;ech">láech</a></span>) appears to be always 
treated as <span class="itype o">o</span>, <span class="gen m.">m.</span> stem. The orig. sense may be <span class="trans">one <span class="def">incapable of</span> 
<span class="def">bearing</span> <span class="def">arms</span>, <span class="def">below</span> the <span class="def">status</span> of a <span class="def">warrior</span>, perhaps a <span class="def">weak-witted</span> person</span>. In 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iv 344.6
      . the term midhlach is applied 
to a definite class of persons at the lower end of the social 
scale, placed after oínmit, and is defined: 
        fer nara gaib sealb 
na horba na treba na trebthair do, 
          352.30
        
      . <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> also 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> iv 
124 § 1
      : ni théiged rí cen rígain . . . ná midlach cen drúithsig. 
It seems sometimes to be equated with `<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=2 dr&#xFA;th">2 drúth</a></span>': 
        Find . . . is dó 
ropad druth Lomna Midlag, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm.Y" title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm. Y</strong></span> 1018
        
       (orc treith) = 

        Lomna druth (.i. mídlach), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm." title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm.</strong></span> p. 34.
        
       
        mol Midend midlaige, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> viii 308.1
        
      , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        Midend druth Eochath maic m.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
        , 
          307.32
        
      . 
The general sense in lit. appears to be <span class="trans">a <span class="def">coward</span>, <span class="def">weakling</span>:</span> 
        m.<span class="oRef">¤</span> 
.i. medón-lax .i. leth-lax, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm.Y" title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm. Y</strong></span> 946.
        
       
        nim m.<span class="oRef">¤</span>-sa re teacht 
a comhruc, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Aen." title="eDIL Bibliography">Aen.</strong></span> 2916.
        
       
        darat bruth ┐ bríg in cach midlaig díb, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 243a18
        
       (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TTr." title="eDIL Bibliography">TTr.</strong></span> 1956
      ). 
        ni gnim cubaid . . . serc do thabairt do 
midlaich (: imdaid), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xiii 202.13
        
      . 
        nir bo thutim midlaige, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 121b44
        
      . 
        ni hatach mbille .i. ni hatach bocht no midlaige, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thr.Ir.Gl." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thr. Ir. Gl.</strong></span> 133
        
       (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> July 3
      ) = 
        ni hatach boicht no mídlaig,

      . 
        ní ba haisdear midhlaighe,
