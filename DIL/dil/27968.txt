    <p class="result"> 
Immurgu (? 
        imm-ro-gáu, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> § 907
        
      ) never 
occurs at the beginning of a sentence and was stressed on 
the second syllable as its use in poetry shows: 
        amorra ┐ 
cheana ┐ idir ... focail nach berar ara cheill iad ┐ as c. comardadh riu adhon ... ni bia mo mheithealsa amorra | gan leith-shearsa leanna, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CI3" title="eDIL Bibliography">C I 3</strong></span>, 45b1
        
      . 
        aniú iomorra gan mearbhaill, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Content." title="eDIL Bibliography">Content.</strong></span> xxii 10.
        
      
    </p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
Used as <span class="pos conj." style="color: #ff8000">conj.</span> and <span class="pos adv." style="color: #ff8000">adv.</span> with adversative force or 
emphasizing the previous word or phrace, <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">however</span>, <span class="def">moreover</span>, 
<span class="def">well</span>, <span class="def">then</span>, <span class="def">now</span>, <span class="def">indeed</span>, <span class="def">in truth</span>, <span class="def">but</span>
      </span>, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> :</p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">I</span> With adversative force serves to contradict or modify 
a previous statement or something implied in it, or to 
express contrast with what has just been said = <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">however</span>
        </span> 
(<span class="frn ">autem</span>) : ní thucci mo menme immurgu gl. nam si orem 
lingua spiritus meus orat mens autem mea sine fructu est, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 
12d11
        . ithésidi immurgu beta hícthi `<span class="quote">it is they, however</span>, 
(<span class="ediltr">and not the aforementioned) who shall be saved</span>,' 
          3d29
        . 
          is 
beo ind anim tri sodin is marb in corp immurgu trisna 
senpecthu, 
            4a6
          
        . 
          dosnicfa cobir cid mall bith maith immurgu intain dondiccfa, 
            5c5
          
        . rubu fersom muintere ... 
nirbu choimdiu imurgu `<span class="quote">he had been a man of (the) household ... he had not been a lord however</span>,' 
          33a5
        . ní boi immurgu 
hi cridiu gl. humanitatem quidem uerbis, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 34a16
        . 
          ní ar 
indí asndarobartis immurgu inna briathra hisin, 
            31b22
          
        . 
ba cunda[il] sem immurgu `<span class="quote">he was sage however</span>,' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> ii 
291.6
        . denúas dotiagar hisatech ... sis iarsuidiu ... dotiagar 
ass immurgu `<span class="quote">from above is the coming into the house ... 
beneath, afterwards, is the coming out, however</span>,' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> ii 
292.20
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> Poems II
        ).</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
In O.Ir. combined as strengthening <span class="pos particle" style="color: #ff8000">particle</span> with 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=cammaiph">cammaiph</a></span> and <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 noch">1 noch</a></span> and <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=2 ocus">2 ocus</a></span> : camaip 
immurgu oen cháractar f, `<span class="quote">we have, however, formed one 
character f</span>,' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 9a22
        . noch immurgu ní rolsat som cumachtae ṅdé diib `<span class="quote">however they notwithstanding did not cast the 
power of God from them</span>,' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 16d2
        . guth sechmadachti ┐ 
todochaidi immurgu `<span class="quote">a word of the preterite and at the same 
time of the future</span>,' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 147b7
        . 
          issinn aithrech a ndoriṅgénsam 
risin fer ind ingen immorro atbélat a bbéoilside, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBFr." title="eDIL Bibliography">TBFr.</strong></span> 
246.
          
         
          <span class="frn ">vir</span> immorru <span class="frn ">a virtute anima in tribulationeis</span>
         ` <span class="quote">`<span class="frn ">vir</span>,' 
however, (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> in contradistinction to `<span class="frn ">homo</span>')</span>,' ... 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Hib.Min." title="eDIL Bibliography">Hib. Min.</strong></span> 
457.
         persa na laidhe imorro Dubhthach `<span class="quote">but the author 
of the poem (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> as opposed to the authors of the Senchas) 
was D.</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 4.13
        . muna fidir imurro ... [is] slan dí 
`<span class="quote">on the other hand if she does not know she is guiltless</span>,' 
          iv 
60.y
        . uball <span class="frn ">quasi</span> aball, aball immorro ō burg Etāile dianit 
ainm <span class="frn ">Abellanium</span>
         <span class="ediltr">A., however, comes from</span> ..., 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm.Y" title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm. Y</strong></span> 
1272
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Anecd." title="eDIL Bibliography">Anecd.</strong></span> iv 111
        ). 
          ni tarat immurgu do Chonnachtaib 
acht dā c[h]ois na-mmucce, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sc.M2" title="eDIL Bibliography">Sc.M²</strong></span> § 18.
          
         his taithbingith 
immargu nachiddeni ar oen in cethardi-se `<span class="quote">on the other 
hand anyone who does not do it for one of these four reasons 
is a fault finder</span>,' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> vii 162.27
        . lecsiu immurgo ildeth 
(<span class="lbl">sic</span>) nde mess for a menmainsom `<span class="quote">leave it, however, to God 
to judge the mind of such</span>' (<span class="lbl">viz.</span> <span class="ediltr">and do not refuse them the 
sacraments</span>), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Mon.Tall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Mon. Tall.</strong></span> 148.11
        . bes biaid immurgo láad 
muindtirisiu ní nodglanai tene brathai `<span class="quote">thy monks, however</span>, (<span class="lbl">viz.</span> <span class="ediltr">in contradistinction to mine) shall perchance have 
sometimg for the fire of doom to cleanse</span>,' 
          130.2
        . achd ni 
Maolrúoin imargu asrubart frissim ón `<span class="quote">it was not, however,

          129.10
        . éisem ro ba

was her confessor but she was soliciting him</span>,' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> i 68.2
        . 
inghen immorro tusa ┐ nī rochinnis t'óighe co se `<span class="quote">you, 
however, are an unmarried girl and have not dedicated your 
virginity so far</span>,' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ACL" title="eDIL Bibliography">ACL</strong></span> iii 309.12
        . `cid on, a maic ... nach 
fetur-sa' `nat fetur-sa immurgu' `<span class="quote">I do not know it however</span>' (=even if I should know), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BColm." title="eDIL Bibliography">BColm.</strong></span> 76.5
        . ba dímicin 
mór leo Fergus dia tabairt hi tindscra ... roptar buidig 
immorro do Chonchobor ar a degthindnacul dóib <span class="ediltr">all the 
same they were thankful to C</span>., 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> iv 24.13
        . 
          do bhríogh 
go mbí onóir ar an seabhac ré linn a bheatha ... an chearc 
iomorra, léigthear san laithigh dhi ré linn a beatha, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TSh." title="eDIL Bibliography">TSh.</strong></span> 
923.
          
         
          atá an eaglas Romhánach na héineaglais ... na heiricidh 
iomoro as beag dhíobh nách bí contrárdha dá chéile, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xD3;H&#xE9;odhusa" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ó Héodhusa</strong></span> 34.7.
          
        
      </p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">II</span> With emphasising force, <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">moreover</span>, <span class="def">besides</span>, <span class="def">now</span>, <span class="def">then</span>, 
<span class="def">verily</span>, <span class="def">indeed</span>:</span>
      </p>
     
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) to add an additional statement : 
            nísnaiccilled san immorru acht dusnéced nammá, 
              <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Imr.Brain" title="eDIL Bibliography">Imr. Brain</strong></span> § 61.
            
           
dorigne Dia imorro in rigthech uachtarach do shlog amra 
airchaingel `<span class="quote">moreover, God made for the</span> ...,' 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ToddLect." title="eDIL Bibliography">Todd Lect.</strong></span> 
iii 38.1
          .</p>
      
     
      
        <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) to continue a narrative <span class="trans">
            <span class="def">now</span>, <span class="def">then</span>:</span> is e imorro a 
n-airem na slog sin `<span class="quote">now this is the reckoning of those hosts</span>,' 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ToddLect." title="eDIL Bibliography">Todd Lect.</strong></span> iii 42.26
          . ro-érig immorro Conchobor mochtrath ar n-a barach `<span class="quote">now C. arose early on the morrow</span>,' 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CRR" title="eDIL Bibliography">CRR</strong></span> § 14.
           
            dála iomorro Cairbre Niaidh Fear, 
              <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CRR2" title="eDIL Bibliography">CRR²</strong></span> § 28.
            
           
ó thainigc immurgu cusna dēdenchu (<span class="lbl">sic leg.</span>) `<span class="quote">now when 
his end was approaching</span>,' 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BColm." title="eDIL Bibliography">BColm.</strong></span> 106.1
          . is e adbar, imorro, 
fa tainic an diabul `<span class="quote">now this is the cause why the Devil 
came</span>,' 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BCC" title="eDIL Bibliography">BCC</strong></span> 378.25
          . a n-inbaidh na huaire ... sin immorro 
`<span class="quote">at that particular time then</span>,' 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Fl.Earls" title="eDIL Bibliography">Fl. Earls</strong></span> 114.3
          .</p>
      
     
      
        <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">c</span>) <span class="trans">as strengthening <span class="pos particle" style="color: #ff8000">particle</span> <span class="def">indeed</span>, <span class="def">verily</span>
          </span> (sometimes 
with slightly implied opposition to a previous statement) : 

            `is anim dún nad-fil is taig' `atá immurgu' ol Conall oc 
tabairt chinn Ánlúain assa chriss, 
              <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sc.M2" title="eDIL Bibliography">Sc.M²</strong></span> § 16.
            
           "úair 
ní th'ari-siu téit ria" "téit immorro" ar in gilla `<span class="quote">for it is 
no business of yours' `it is indeed,' said the servant</span>,' 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> 
i 134.5
          . 
            cade didiu bar fisse? or Fiachnai. nī ansa immurgu, 

              viii 157.20
            
          . is hí immurco foropir cáich lindi indsin `<span class="quote">this, 
indeed, is what we consider to be the additional labour of each 
man</span>,' 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Mon.Tall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Mon. Tall.</strong></span> 133.11
          . 
            "in ba i tossaig no fain deriud?" 
"i tossaig imorro" or sisi, 
              <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/M&#xF3;rM." title="eDIL Bibliography">Mór M.</strong></span> § 1.
            
           
            ni ar meth gascid 
... dorochrabair-si acht forlond do imbirt foraib ┐ ummoro 
tra, ar si, darochair far comlund lib-si, 
              <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> iii 504.587
