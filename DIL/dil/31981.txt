<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="31981">mer</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. mer or <a class="reference" href="31981">dil.ie/31981</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2019</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:

    <p class="result">
      <span class="itype o,-&#x101;">o, ā</span>. <span class="pos adj." style="color: #ff8000">adj.</span> used chiefly of persons, apparently <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">off one's 
head</span>, <span class="def">demented</span>, <span class="def">crazy</span>
      </span>, both of temporary condition due to 
excitement, intoxication, anger, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>, and of permanent 
quality; in heroic lit. applied to warriors (often in unfavourable sense <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">foolhardy</span>, <span class="def">rash</span>
      </span>); in Laws to the insane or half-witted. In late lit. often used in a complimentary sense 
<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">spirited</span>, <span class="def">lively</span>, <span class="def">agile</span>
      </span>; see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Dec. § 65
       (
        mear . . . ó chéill in 
mholta).</p>
