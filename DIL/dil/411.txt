<span class="pos Vn." style="color: #ff8000">Vn.</span> ecmong.</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
<span class="trans">There are two meanings, (a) <span class="def">strikes</span> (trans.), (b) <span class="def">happens</span>
      </span>. As 
prot. -ecmaic -ecmaing and vn. ecmong are found only in 
sense (b), and the <span class="tns s-pret.">s-pret.</span> (found in O. Ir. glosses) only in 
sense (a) there may have been two distinct compound verbs. See, however, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MU2" title="eDIL Bibliography">MU²</strong></span> 1049
       and 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> v 242.153
       cited below under (a). 
But sense (b) could have arisen from sense (a), where, on the 
other hand, a compd. of icc could have been influenced by or 
conflated with boingid or a compd. thereof. Treated as one 
vb. by 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> (ii 555)
       and Thurn. (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> 354
      ).</p>
