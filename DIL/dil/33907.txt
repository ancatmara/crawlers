<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="33907">opar, (opair)</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. opar, (opair) or <a class="reference" href="33907">dil.ie/33907</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2013</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                opar, obar, opair, oiprib, obair, oibre, obra, obar</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   


    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype &#x101;">ā</span>, <span class="gen f.">f.</span> (Lat. <span class="frn ">opera</span>) <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> opar (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 4351
      ); obar con-

tinues in use beside opair (obair) throughout Mid.Ir. period.

<span class="case d">d</span>
      <span class="number p.">p.</span> 
        oiprib, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 9d19 
        
      (<span class="lbl">a prima manu</span>). obair (<span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> oibre, obra),


        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Dec. § 42.16
      . 
        obar, 
          § 56
        
      . In Mid.Ir. generally treated as

<span class="itype guttural">guttural</span> stem in pl.</p>
  
  
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">work</span>, <span class="def">activity</span>, <span class="def">employment</span>, <span class="def">occupation</span>
        </span> in wide sense:


          opar <span class="frn ">ab operacione</span>
          , 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm.Y" title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm. Y</strong></span> 1012 
          
        = 
          opair, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm." title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm.</strong></span> p. 33.
          
        

arna epíltis oc opair <span class="frn ">semper</span>
         <span class="ediltr">working</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 121d16
        . ni hopair

niad náre <span class="ediltr">diffidence is no business of a champion</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 3275.
        

togaes denma opre <span class="ediltr">proper folk for work</span> (i.e. fighting), 
          5238
         =

degh-aos denma oibre, St. ro bai a n-obair ┐ a feidm ic a

fulang (= they were sustained by their activity, of soldiers in

battle), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CCath." title="eDIL Bibliography">CCath.</strong></span> 5911. 
        
        oc admilliud na hoipre <span class="ediltr">frustrating the

work</span> (a siege), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> ii 4.28
        . an tí ler obair bhar

mbasuccadh <span class="ediltr">whose endeavour it was to kill you</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BNn&#xC9;" title="eDIL Bibliography">BNnÉ</strong></span> 73
§ 130
        . deilbh oige ní hobair dhuit `<span class="quote">the making of poetry is not

a thing for you!</span>' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Content." title="eDIL Bibliography">Content.</strong></span> xxii 29. 
        
        obair fhaoilte `<span class="quote">an act of

thanksgiving</span>' (?), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/A.&#xD3;D&#xE1;laigh" title="eDIL Bibliography">A. Ó Dálaigh</strong></span> xlix 18.
         Esp. <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">menial</span> <span class="def">work</span>, <span class="def">toil</span>,

<span class="def">labour</span>
        </span>: 
          mugsaine ┐ daer-opair, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 1760. 
          
        
        fer oibre <span class="ediltr">a work-

man</span>,

          lucht na hoibre,

        . obair don daoirseach (= <span class="frn ">opus servo</span>), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TSh." title="eDIL Bibliography">TSh.</strong></span> 1874. 
        
        
          an

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Bedel" title="eDIL Bibliography">Exodus</strong></span>

        .</p>
    
		
      <p class="result">
        <span class="trans">Of a <span class="def">laborious</span> or <span class="def">difficult</span> <span class="def">undertaking</span>:</span> in ochtar is calma . . . a n-opar 'com tharraing

esti `<span class="quote">the eight strongest men . . . had hard work to pull me out</span>',


          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MacCongl." title="eDIL Bibliography">MacCongl.</strong></span> 93.4
        . In the phrase (is) opair <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">hardly</span>, <span class="def">scarcely</span>
        </span>: ferais snechta mór dóibh gurbh obair doibh a n-airm do chongbáil '<span class="quote">so that they could scarcely hold their weapons</span>' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/C&#xF3;irAnm." title="eDIL Bibliography">Cóir Anm.</strong></span> ii 21
        , 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> xiii 122
        . 
          obar . . . | breith na bpobal i bparrthus, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/D&#xE1;nD&#xE9;" title="eDIL Bibliography">Dán Dé</strong></span> xviii 30
          
        ; '<span class="quote">His people can scarcely gain entrance to Paradise</span>' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> xiii 122

fhás, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/A.&#xD3;D&#xE1;laigh" title="eDIL Bibliography">A. Ó Dálaigh</strong></span> xii 5
          
        ; '<span class="quote">scarcely one like Mary arose from among those who descended from Adam </span>' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> xiii 122
        .</p>
    
		
      <p class="result">In loose sense of <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">doings</span>, <span class="def">goings</span> on</span>, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>: is ait an obair-se
