<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="26064">glére</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. glére or <a class="reference" href="26064">dil.ie/26064</a></span><br/></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   

    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> (glér, gléir). An abstract, the precise ṁeaning of which 
in any given context is not always easy to decide. It seems 
to have the two primary senses : (1) <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">abundance</span>
      </span> ; (2) <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">choice</span>, 
<span class="def">élite</span> (passing into that of <span class="def">excellence</span> in general)</span>. The former 
meaning seems to be that of the numerous occurrences of the 
word in SR ; the latter meaning prevails in the more recent 
exx. The sense `<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">purity</span>, <span class="def">brightness</span>
      </span>' given by some translators 
has no support, and is probably due to a confusion with 
gleór.
