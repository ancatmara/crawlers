<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="20071">enechrucce, ainechrucce</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. enechrucce, ainechrucce or <a class="reference" href="20071">dil.ie/20071</a></span><br/></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype io">io</span>, <span class="gen m.">m.</span> (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 enech">1 enech</a></span> + <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ruccae">ruccae</a></span>)</p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">A</span>
      </p>
     
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) In technical legal usage <span class="trans">a <span class="def">dishonour</span> to a person consisting in <span class="def">injury</span> to a <span class="def">relative</span> of a certain degree, or to his 
<span class="def">tenant</span> of a certain status:</span> 
            enechruice .i. enech rú cian .i. 
cian o inchaib atcíther a rú .i. a imdergad. enech din .i. 
agad crodergad [agait croderg] amail rogab mac do máthar, 
mac do shethar céli foissmad, céli taistil ni na bita [main 
anb ita] diles sectmad lógi a enig [loige enech] ind, 
              <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thr.Ir.Gl." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thr. Ir. Gl.</strong></span> 19
            
           (the varr. in square brackets are added from 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span>, 
120
           = 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 224
          ) = 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm.Y" title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm. Y</strong></span> 532
           (where enech...croderg 
is omitted and the penultimate sentence reads: ni nad bitái 
dilis. <span class="lbl">Leg.</span> ní nadbi taí diles <span class="ediltr">what is not thine own 
possession</span>. See 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xv 247
          ). cach gres, cach enechruice, 
is ḟor cintaib treisi ata `<span class="quote">all attacks, all insults are among 
the offences of three days' stay</span>' (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> with regard to these, 
distraint may be stayed for three days) 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 162.27
           
(with gl.: 
            cach gres .i. do neoch i ndlegar lan, cach enechruice .i. o ta lan amach, .i. leth ┐ sechtmaid, 
              166.18
            
          ). 

            oenchumail i n-enechruice rig frisi forfuacht...ba mathair 
.uii. rig frisi foruacht (.i....oenchumail .uii. mbo doib a 
morfessiur inna enechgris), 
              <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xv 348
            
          .</p>
      
     
      
        <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) <span class="trans">the <span class="def">compensation</span> due for such <span class="def">dishonour</span>; a half 
or a <span class="def">seventh</span> of the `eneclann' of the person so <span class="def">dishonoured</span>:</span> 

            teora fodla fuil for inchaib duine .i. enicland ┐ enechruice 
┐ enechgriss (enechruice .i. i leith ┐ i sectmad, 
              <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> 
v 96.6
            
          . 
            airer enechruice (.i. cumal enechgrisi no enechruici 
nama), 
              ii 204.22
            
          ; 
            206.4
           (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
            126 n.1
          ). <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 

            lanamnas fir tathigthe cen targud, cen urgnam; cuiced 
a lamthorad cuit in fir .i. in chele fri himscarad doib, arus 
enachruice dosum furesi insain, cia forruastar fria, ised 
asrirtar do de, 
              396 x
            
          . na teora eirce seo... .i. eneclann ┐ 
dire ┐ aithgin .i. eneclann ┐ enechruice ┐ enechgris 

            iii 26.27 Comm.
          
        </p>
      
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">B</span> in wider sense <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">shame</span>, <span class="def">disgrace</span>, <span class="def">insult</span>:</span> ma rogata 
nech do torc... ┐ ma dotī dām caem, bes enechruice duit 
(<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> you will be shamed for want of provender), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 
122.7
        . fer tri sruba; sruib tuirc fochlaid scoltas ainechruicce cach aimsir <span class="ediltr">snout of a rooting hog which cleaves 
shamefacedness (from lack of provender) at any time</span> 
          iv 
310.18
        . Similarly: ainm cuile no scrine bis iar cul colbu, 
is indi bit tarsanna coema fri enechruice <span class="ediltr">in which are fair 
savouries (as provision) against shamefacedness</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span>, 
368b
          
        = 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 790

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> ii 292
        ). 
ind athis ┐ ind enechrucce ra immir Lamidóin formsa 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 224a27
         = 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TTr." title="eDIL Bibliography">TTr.</strong></span> 512
        . 
          ba hathis...leis...Frigía do bith

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TTr.2" title="eDIL Bibliography">TTr.²</strong></span> 181
