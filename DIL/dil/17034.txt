Charles-Edwards, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> xxiv 75-78
        , proposes the following ordering for the O.Ir. senses: <span class="edilb">I (a)</span> <span class="trans">an individual’s <span class="def">entitlement</span>, <span class="def">right</span>.</span> <span class="edilb">(i)</span> <span class="trans">a <span class="def">right</span> in respect of fair contract as opposed to <span class="frn ">naidm</span> ‘binding surety’.</span> <span class="edilb">(ii)</span> <span class="trans">name of the second of the five paths to judgement chosen by a plaintiff who is the creditor in a contract, whereas the third path, <span class="frn ">cert</span>, is chosen by someone arguing that the terms of a contract were unfair,</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/C&#xF3;icCon." title="eDIL Bibliography">Cóic Con.</strong></span> § 8-9 (R/E Recension)
        . <span class="edilb">(iii)</span> <span class="trans">Of the judicial settlement of a claim which follows upon the defendant’s decision to accept legal process, the right as upheld by a judicial verdict. Hence </span>
        <span class="edilb">(iv)</span> <span class="trans">rightful state or condition.</span> <span class="edilb">(b)</span> <span class="trans">(collectively) body of rights.</span> <span class="edilb">(c)</span> <span class="trans">thing or action to which someone is entitled; <span class="def">debt</span>, <span class="def">obligation</span>.</span> <span class="edilb">(d)</span> <span class="case g">g</span>
        <span class="number s.">s.</span> <span class="trans">as attrib.: <span class="def">rightful</span>.</span> <span class="edilb">(e)</span> <span class="trans">As adverb: <span class="def">rightfully</span>.</span> <span class="edilb">II</span> <span class="trans">(Latin usage)</span> <span class="edilb">(a)</span> = <span class="trans">
          <span class="frn ">ratio</span>:</span> <span class="edilb">(i)</span> <span class="trans">
