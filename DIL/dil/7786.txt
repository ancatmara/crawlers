                2013</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:

    <p class="result">

      <span class="itype i">i</span>, <span class="gen f.">f.</span> Later also with dental and velar inflection. Note 
also <span class="case g">g</span>

    <p class="result"> 
<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">Law</span>, <span class="def">regulation</span>, <span class="def">rule</span>; <span class="def">fine</span>, <span class="def">tax</span>, <span class="def">tribute</span>.</span> Precise legal 
significance difficult to define. Appar. regarded as statute 
law (a law imposed by a king, perh. by an over-king) or by 
the church (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xii 69
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ir.Hist.Stud." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ir. Hist. Stud.</strong></span> xiii 101
      -
        3
      ) in contrast 
to aurradus <span class="ediltr">customary law.</span> It is always more severe than 
the latter, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xiv 339
      . See 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/C&#xF3;icCon." title="eDIL Bibliography">Cóic Con.</strong></span> pp. 65
      -
        66
      . Frequ. 
glossed by ríagail ( 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> ii 222.3 Comm.
       <span class="lbl">etc.</span>). The wider 
meaning <span class="ediltr">written law</span> undoubtedly derived from the numerous 
monastic ordinances issued in the 8th cent., Thurn. 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> 
xviii 396
      . By annalists appar. equated with <span class="frn ">lex</span> (see 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AU" title="eDIL Bibliography">AU</strong></span> 733
      , 
        736
      , 
        766
       and Index <span class="lbl">s.v.</span> Law). <span class="trans">In legal commentary, denotes the law as recorded in old books in contradistinction to <span class="frn ">aurradus which denotes current law</span>
      </span> (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IrishJurist" title="eDIL Bibliography">The Irish Jurist</strong></span> xxxiii 359
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LiteratureandPolitics" title="eDIL Bibliography">Literature and Politics in the Celtic World</strong></span> 199-200
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> lii 136 n. 39
      ).</p>
