<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="33761">6 ol</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 6 ol or <a class="reference" href="33761">dil.ie/33761</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2013</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                al, all, oll, oul, oldat, olt</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">defective <span class="pos vb." style="color: #ff8000">vb.</span> <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">says</span>, <span class="def">said</span>, <span class="def">quoth</span>.</span> When folld. by a pron. as

subj. the forms ol-sé, -sí, -siat (O.Ir. -seat) are used (in Mid.Ir.

occas. é, íat); Quin argues that the second element is the stressed demonstrative pronoun <span class="frn ">sé</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> v 98
      . For length of the vowel see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> liii 138
      . In

common use throughout the O.Ir. and a great part of the

Mid.Ir. period. In Ml. Glosses the rival form ar appears

(
      
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 44b10
      , 
        
          c20
      ); in Mid.Ir. ar (or, for, bar) are used side by side

with ol (which is fairly common in CCath., rare in Acall.); in

Mod.Ir. ar alone survives. There are a few traces of <span class="frn ">olsí</span> with a pronominal subject in Mid.Ir. texts: 
        olsi Cūchulaind, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 779
        
      .</p>

      ).</p>
   
    <p class="result">Used like Lat. <span class="frn ">inquit</span> in direct oration and always paren-

thetical, generally after the opening words of the speech

reported, in 3d pers. sing. or pl. (exceptionally with 1 s. pron.)

and with force of present or past. In Glosses often used to

refer to the speaker of words reported in the Lat. text. Often

tautological, preceded by a vb. of saying introducing the

quotation; occas. folld. by FRI. An analogical <span class="number pl.">pl.</span> formation

oldat (oldait, olt) occurs in Mid.Ir.</p>
