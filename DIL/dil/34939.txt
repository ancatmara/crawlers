<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="34939">reiclés</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. reiclés or <a class="reference" href="34939">dil.ie/34939</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2013</span><br/></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   


    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="gen m.">m.</span> <span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number p.">p.</span> -a. Not found in O.Ir. Etymology doubtful; 
Stokes in preface to 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ThreeHom." title="eDIL Bibliography">Three Hom.</strong></span>
       suggests a formation from 
Lat. <span class="frn ">reclusum</span>, <span class="lbl">i.e.</span> a recluse's cell; later in Lism. Lives Index, 
from ro-eccles (&lt; <span class="frn ">ecclesia</span>), <span class="lbl">i.e.</span> a large church. Also written 
reglés: 
        rigles, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BColm." title="eDIL Bibliography">BColm.</strong></span> 18.21
        
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xii 393.33
      ; 
        reidhglés, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> v 
84.16
        
      .</p>
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) orig. <span class="trans">an <span class="def">oratory</span> or <span class="def">small</span> <span class="def">church</span>
        </span> built by an Irish saint 
for his own use; sometimes used to include the plot or enclosure about it. ó tharnic in recles do chumtach ┐ ro fhás a fér, rucc 
gilla Dáre a ech isin recles (= <span class="frn ">ecclesiam</span>), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Lat.Lives" title="eDIL Bibliography">Lat. Lives</strong></span> 61.12
         
(
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LB" title="eDIL Bibliography">LB</strong></span> 28a57
        ), <span class="lbl">cf.</span> tuctha dá ech Dare inna recles for fér, ar rob 
férach ind relec (= <span class="frn ">in oraculum</span>), 
          15.2
        . 
          do bhúain crand do 
cumhdach reclesa, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Lism.L." title="eDIL Bibliography">Lism. L.</strong></span> 2585
          
         (of an oratory built by St. 
Findian). 
          teora bliadna boí cen lés | Colum ina dubreclés, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 652
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ACC" title="eDIL Bibliography">ACC</strong></span> 24 Comm.
        ). 
          Cainneach a nDoire do grēs | is 
Brenaind 'sa duibriglēs, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xii 393.33
          
        . <span class="number pl.">pl.</span> 
          mo roilge, mo 
roiglēs-[s]a, 
            <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 34
          
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
Hence <span class="trans">a <span class="def">monastic</span> <span class="def">cell</span>
        </span>, the hut occupied by an Irish monk 
in a coenobitic settlement, and in general an anchorite's cell: 
is uathad nech díb téged as a recles acht mad don eclais 
(= <span class="frn ">extra cellulam suam</span>), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Lat.Lives" title="eDIL Bibliography">Lat. Lives</strong></span> 92.16
        , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          96.13 
        , 
          97.3
        . 

          issé [Oengus] no chanad a thṡalmu . . . .l. isind abhaind . . . 
.l. dano fón ṁbile feissin ┐ .l. ina reicles, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> 10 z
          
        . 
          reccles 
diamhuir derrit . . . oen-mac [ec]ulsa . . . ic cantain a 
urnuighthe innti, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xiv 58 § 72
          
        . 
          it' recles (reiclis <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>) oc 
ernaigdi, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> iii 108 § 49
          
         (Rule of Ailbe). 
          téid an inghen la 
Mochuda ┐ dogní reclés di hi cCluain Dalláin, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BNn&#xC9;" title="eDIL Bibliography">BNnÉ</strong></span> 296 § 29
          
         
(= <span class="frn ">construxit cellam</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/V.SS.Hib." title="eDIL Bibliography">V. SS. Hib.</strong></span> i 185.33
        ). 
          dochuadar fo hErind 
. . . ┐ ro gabsatt cellae ┐ reclesae, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BColm." title="eDIL Bibliography">BColm.</strong></span> 8.23
          
        . Loosely used 
to include buildings and ground of a community: ni tharla bó 
isin recleis ind inbhaidh sin (of Brigid's community), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Lism.L." title="eDIL Bibliography">Lism. L.</strong></span> 
1369
        . In fig. sense: reclés Hérenn Glenn Dá Locha `<span class="quote">Abbey-church</span>' (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> monastic centre), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Triads" title="eDIL Bibliography">Triads</strong></span> 11
        . </p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
<span class="trans">Later used generally of a church connected with a monastery or of the monastery or abbey itself</span>. <span class="trans">? In annals, a small <span class="def">conventual reliquary church</span>
        </span> (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Peritia" title="eDIL Bibliography">Peritia</strong></span> xiii 259-75
        ): 
          gur bennaigh 
reiccles riaghalta, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Anecd." title="eDIL Bibliography">Anecd.</strong></span> iii 3.3
          
        . gan leabhair náid mionna i 
reighléis ná i mainistir `<span class="quote">abbey-church</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Keat." title="eDIL Bibliography">Keat.</strong></span> iii 2768
        . 
          don 
recles re nabarthar Senboth Átha, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BNn&#xC9;" title="eDIL Bibliography">BNnÉ</strong></span> 216.1
          
         = <span class="frn ">ad monasterium</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/V.SS.Hib." title="eDIL Bibliography">V. SS. Hib.</strong></span> ii 149 x
        . 
          Dunadhach in reiclesa Coluim Cille i 
nArd Macha, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AU" title="eDIL Bibliography">AU</strong></span> 1010
          
        . 
          la aba[i]d reiclesa Poil ┐ Petair i nArd 
Macha, 
            ii 242.5
          
        . celloir mor-reiclesa Daire `<span class="quote">manciple of the 
monastery of D.</span>', 
          256.6
        . 
          Ard Macha do losccadh . . . idon na 
huile reiclesa ┐ in[n]a huile tempaill . . . cenmotha reicles 
Brighti, 
            194.18
          
         (`<span class="quote">the houses of Canons Regular</span>', MacCarthy); 
<span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AFM" title="eDIL Bibliography">AFM</strong></span> iii 48.1
        . <span class="trans">Ecclesiastical <span class="def">enclosure</span>, subsiduary enclosure within a larger settlement</span>: 
          in reclés .i. in n-eclais mbicc, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Trip." title="eDIL Bibliography">Trip.</strong></span> 970
          
        . 
          conaitigir Pátraic ined a reclésa fair, 
            2700
          
        . 
          dair mor Reglesa Fingin, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CS" title="eDIL Bibliography">CS</strong></span> 256.2 (s.a. 1013)
          
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
<span class="trans">In wider sense of a church or chapel in general</span>: 
          reigless 
onorach, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Fl.Earls" title="eDIL Bibliography">Fl. Earls</strong></span> 40.11
          
         (of a chapel in a castle of the king of 
Spain). 
          reccles, 
            34.20
          
         = 
          sepel, 
            <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 6
          
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) in follg. exx. used of <span class="trans">a <span class="def">tomb</span>
        </span> (perh. through influence of 
reilec): 
          isin ecluis tucad inud a reclésa do . . . ┐ is and ro 
hadnuiced é, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 204.15
          
        . 
          bid tosach deg-[g]rēsa dam | 
reclēsa na rig-apstal, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xii 397.12
          
         (poem on the graves of 
the Apostles, called `reilgi' elsewhere in it).</p>
