    <p class="result"> 
In Mid.Ir. MSS forru (forro, forthu, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>) and foraib are 
often found without distinction of case. In the Laud-Lismore text of Acall. pretonic for is very rare; it is 
regularly replaced by ar. But with suff. pron. <span class="per 3">3</span> <span class="number s.">s.</span> <span class="gen m.">m.</span> <span class="gen n.">n.</span> 
fair is predominant; there are very few instances of air. 
In the other pronominal forms those with and without 
f- seem to share the field equally.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
In classical verse the pretonic form is AR, but the pronominal forms are regularly those of FOR without f. an 
exception is orat, orad (beside ort). <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> O. Ir. airiut, erut 
from AR. See 24 P 8 (McKenna, Tracts).</p>
