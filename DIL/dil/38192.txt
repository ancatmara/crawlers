<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="38192">2 so, su</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 2 so, su or <a class="reference" href="38192">dil.ie/38192</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2013</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                su-, so-, su-, sonairt, soibheart, soifer, soicheneol, sogh-, soth-, soghthengaid, soighidirdhealbhtha, sothbiadach, sothchroidheach, suacubuir, sochenēuil, sochoisc, soacallaim, soāig, Sodelbi</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos prefix" style="color: #ff8000">prefix</span> leniting the follg. initial. In hiatus usually 
su-; in other positions so- and su- alternate without regard to 
the quality of the vowel in the next syll., 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> 
§ 365
      . In old compds. with follg. neutral consonance: sonairt 
(nert), solus (lés), <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 
        § 166
      . Later felt as an autonomous element of the compd. and adopts the quality of the vowel of 
follg. syll., <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> 
        soibheart, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/D&#xE1;ntaGr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Dánta Gr.</strong></span> 41.27
        
      . 
        soifer, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Leb.Gab.i" title="eDIL Bibliography">Leb. Gab.(i)</strong></span> 
156 n. 4
        
      . 
        soicheneol, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MR" title="eDIL Bibliography">MR</strong></span> 96.6
        
      . Occasionally spelt sogh-, 
soth-: 
        soghthengaid, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 444
        
      . 
        soighidirdhealbhtha, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Luc.Fid." title="eDIL Bibliography">Luc. Fid.</strong></span> 
375.1
        
      . 
        sothbiadach, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 42.4. Comm
        
      . 
        sothchroidheach, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Donl." title="eDIL Bibliography">Donlevy</strong></span> 62.24
        
      . Used in composition with nouns, adjectives, 
participles, verbals of necessity, and rarely with verbs. When 
prefixed to nouns to form adjj. the resulting formations, 
when from o- and ā- stems, generally assume i-inflexion: 

        suacubuir, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 67c8
        
       (accobor). 
        sochenēuil, 
          101d12
        
       (cenél). 

        sochoisc, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 30b28
        
       (cosc). (Note, however, 
        sásad sognass, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Anecd." title="eDIL Bibliography">Anecd.</strong></span> i 60 § 84
        
      . 
        a slesaib saerclann socineol, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/StairErc." title="eDIL Bibliography">Stair Erc.</strong></span> 894
        
      ). 
The usage in the case of u- stems varies: 
        bad sochrud, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 
6a30
        
      . 
        fer sochraid, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 5751
        
      . 
        mad . . . socomais (socomas, <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>), 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xiv 369 § 30
        
       (commus). On the model of compds. of o- 
and ā- stems compds. are freely formed in the later language 
by prefixing so- to the gs. of any noun, the resulting formations being treated as attrib. genitives and left uninflected 
(see Bergin, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xii 229
      ): 
        ben . . . soacallma, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MacCongl." title="eDIL Bibliography">MacCongl.</strong></span> 97.3
        
      . 

        lind . . . soóla, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 3025
        
      . 
        saerṡloinnti soicheneoil, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MR" title="eDIL Bibliography">MR</strong></span> 96.9
        
      . 
Nouns formed by prefixing so to o- and ā- stems usually retain the declension of the simplex. Note, however, soacallaim 
(soacallma, soacallmacht, <span class="lbl">v.ll.</span>) `<span class="quote">affability</span>', 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Tec.Corm." title="eDIL Bibliography">Tec. Corm.</strong></span> § 1.5
      . 
soāig (ág), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> i 118 § 8
      . </p>
