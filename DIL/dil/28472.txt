<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="28472">indles, indíles</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. indles, indíles or <a class="reference" href="28472">dil.ie/28472</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                indles</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   


    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos adj" style="color: #ff8000">adj</span> 
      <span class="itype o,-&#x101;">o, ā</span> (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=4 in-">4 in-</a></span> + díles). See <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 d&#xED;les">1 díles</a></span>, Binchy, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Cr&#xED;thG." title="eDIL Bibliography">Críth G.</strong></span> p. 83.
       <span class="trans">The primary meaning is <span class="def">not belonging</span>, not 
one's <span class="def">absolute</span> <span class="def">property</span>:</span> 
        inles .i. in- fo diultad, co nāch 
les ē, acht la nech aile, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm.Y" title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm. Y</strong></span> 784.
        
       
        innles .i. ni diles 
[acht] fri nech ele, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OMulc." title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Mulc.</strong></span> 770.
        
       
        ni forbrister nach n-innles 
.i. nocho foirbrisi do neoch ani is innles do do breith uadha, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OD." title="eDIL Bibliography">O'D.</strong></span> 547
        
       (&lt;
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.17" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.17</strong></span>, col. 436
      ).</p>
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">not forfeit</span>, <span class="def">not due</span>, <span class="def">not lawfully</span> <span class="def">to be paid</span>, therefore 
<span class="def">unlawful</span>:</span> indiles cach iarraid tar ainme indeithbire `<span class="quote">unlawful is every fosterage-fee beyond the avoidable blemish</span>,' 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> ii 180.4
        , glossed : 
          ... .i. is indles in iarraidh don aite 
o ferfuither ainm-cneidhe ar in dalta tre indethbirius, 

            180.7 Comm.
          
         nach indles cor cid itir da eccond `<span class="quote">such a contract is not unlawful even between two idiots</span>,' 
          iii 4.13 Comm.
         
cia nascar a dilsi, is indles cach ngait `<span class="quote">though its perpetuity 
be secured on bond, no gift of a stolen object is valid</span>,' 
          v 216.9 
Comm.
         <span style="color: #ff8000">Superl.</span> 
          secht tobarta ata inndilsem la feine ce 
ro nastar for macud ┐ raith, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 348
          
         (&lt; 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span>, p. 192b
          
        ). 
Used <span class="pos substantival" style="color: #ff8000">substantival</span>ly : cach indbass bid indles n-ūag `<span class="quote">jeder 
Reichtum wird gänzliche Herrenlosigkeit sein</span>,' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC4;lt.Ir.Dicht." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ält. Ir. Dicht.</strong></span> 
ii 24 n. 5
         = 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> v 211.70
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 125b14
        ). ni bi eric tar on, 
tar ainim, tar einech n-indtlem .i. dia nderna indles don 
ni dobera tar cend a enigh `<span class="quote">if he cause a non-forfeiture of 
that which he may give on behalf of his honour</span>,' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ODav." title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Dav.</strong></span> 
1087.
        
      </p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">entailing</span> <span class="def">liability</span>, entailing <span class="def">culpability</span>:</span> díles ┐ indles 
di brúud for lár thige : díles cach rochóem, indles cach 
ndíchóem ; díles ór ┐ argat ┐ uma[e], indles cach (n)ombur 
┐ cach sreth as chórus for lár <span class="ediltr">that which is immune and that 
which entails culpability as a result of breaking on the floor 
of a house : immune is every excessively valuable thing, every 
ordinary thing entails liability ...</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Cr&#xED;thG." title="eDIL Bibliography">Críth G.</strong></span> 221
        -
          223
        . 
díles ┐ indles i n-immid : díles suide ┐ ḟreslige [n-]indi ┐ cia 
bronntar indi co comardda[i] cinn i suidiu ; indles aní bes 
arddu ci[u]nn <span class="ediltr">that which is immune and that which entails 
liability as regards a bed : sitting and reclining in it is immune 
though anything be broken in it as high as the head when sitting, 
anything higher than the head entails liability</span>, 
          233
        -
          235
        . 
indlis do neoch no muille <span class="ediltr">he who incites him is culpable</span> 
(of a dog that has been set on to do an injury), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 
492.7 Comm.
         ma rob indiles cach dib a cheile, is lan fiach 
o cechtar de ind <span class="ediltr">if to attack either of them entailed culpability 
for the other, it is full fine from each</span>, 
          iii 238.x Comm.
         is slan 
don fir fris' nā dalann, co foeigem, ┐ indles iar foeighem `<span class="quote">the 
man with whom she has made no assignation is safe till she 
screams, but it is illegal after screaming</span>,' 
          v 274.18 Comm.
         

          cislir cairdi do chuisin ... cairdi feine ┐ cairdi n-airdbidhe 
┐ cairdi n-indlis ... Cairde nindlis, nechtar da lína adrorastar 
a crich a cheili is diles, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 804
          
         (&lt; 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span>, p. 372b
          
        ) = 

          cairde n-indlius criche, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 22
          
         (&lt; 
        
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span>, 10a
          
        ).</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">c</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">inappropriate</span>, <span class="def">improper</span> (of the use of a word) :</span> caide 
ruidhles ┐ dileas ┐ coitchind ┐ indles in focail is guta ... 
indles di immorro guth fotha, uair ni fota hi inti fein `<span class="quote">what 
are the peculiar, proper, common, and improper of the word 
vowel</span> ? ... <span class="ediltr">Improper to it, however, is voice foundation, when 
it is not a foundation in itself</span>,' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Auraic." title="eDIL Bibliography">Auraic.</strong></span> 380
        -
          384
        . caite 
ruidhles ┐ diles ┐ coitchend, indles alta ? ... indles 
immorro do dialt, ar ni bi alt and `<span class="quote">inappropriate, however, 
to a monosyllable for no</span> alt <span class="ediltr">joint, exists there</span>,' 
          832
        . innleas 
a tabairt for airimh n-aili acht for a seacht `<span class="quote">improper, its 
application to a number other than seven</span>' (of the word seachta, 
<span class="ediltr">heptad</span>), 
          749
        . indles immorro do forbaid dib dul i llocc 
araili `<span class="quote">inappropriate, however, for any accent of them to go 
in place of another</span>,' 
          821
        . coitchend indles don insci saerda 
.i. coitchend ara gnathugud, indles immorra ara aindilsi 
<span class="ediltr">common owing to its being used, but a solecism owing to its 
inappropriateness</span> (of artificial gender, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> to speak of crand 
as masc.), 
          843
        . nī fordiuchtror fordūain indlis `<span class="quote">nicht 
erwache ich durch ein ungehöriges Lied</span>,' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC4;lt.Ir.Dicht." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ält. Ir. Dicht.</strong></span> 
ii 24 § 1.
        
      </p>
     
      <p class="result">

          <span class="def">non-owned</span> <span class="def">woman</span>
        </span> (i.e. another 
man's wife who lives with a given man by her husband's 
permission),' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xii 74.18
        -
          19
         (see also 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Stud.inE.Ir.Law" title="eDIL Bibliography">Stud. in E. Ir. Law</strong></span> 65.2
        ). a atitin a prim mna ocus a fine ata cach ben 
dib sain aicce cenmotha in be nindlis; ocus a aititin a 
fir ata saide `<span class="quote">by the cognizance of his chief woman and of 
his tribe he has all these women except the</span> `indlis' <span class="ediltr">woman, 
and he has her by the consent of her husband</span>,' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> ii 400.26

         lan do 'na prim-mnai ... i[n] ae[n]mud rand fichet 
do i n-a be n-indlis `<span class="quote">full honour-price is paid to him for his

      </p>
