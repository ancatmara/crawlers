<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="31158">M</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. M or <a class="reference" href="31158">dil.ie/31158</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                m, sm, -mm, mb, m, mb</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
  
  
    <p class="result">the tenth letter of the Irish alphabet, was in the Ogham 
alphabet called by the name muin `<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">vine</span>
      </span>' and was the first 
letter of the third aicme or letter-group, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Auraic." title="eDIL Bibliography">Auraic.</strong></span> 993
      , 
        1186
      .</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">1</span> in early Irish orthography the letter m represents two 
sounds:</p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">i</span> In the following cases it was a voiced nasal labial:</p>
      
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) 
initial, folld. by a vowel, <i>l</i> or <i>r</i>;</p>
        
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">b</span>) in the initial combination 
sm;</p>
        
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">c</span>) before <i>b</i> and <i>p</i>;</p>
        
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">d</span>) medial and final after a vowel 
where it represents early -mm (&lt; <i>mb</i>, <i>mp</i>, <i>sm</i>, <i>dm</i>): in these 
cases it was by O.Ir. scribes generally doubled in order to 
distinguish it from lenited <i>m</i>, a practice which continued to 
be common during the entire Mid.Ir. period. <span class="lbl">E.g.</span> am(m) 
&lt; Indog. *<span class="frn ">esmi</span>, <span class="lbl">cf.</span> Lat. <span class="frn ">sum</span>; cam(m) &lt; *<span class="frn ">camb</span>; ammus 
&lt;<span class="frn ">ad-mess</span>.</p>
      
     
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">e</span>) after <i>l</i>, <i>n</i>, and <i>r</i>, where no intermediate vowel 
has fallen out, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> calma, ainm, cuirm.</p>
      
     
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">f</span>) in eclipsis of <i>b</i>, 
<i>b</i> &gt; <i>m</i> (written mb). In dán díreach unlenited m was classed 
for purposes of rhyme with <i>ll</i>, <i>nn</i>, <i>ng</i>, and <i>rr</i>, see Meyer, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MeyerPrim." title="eDIL Bibliography">Meyer Prim.</strong></span>, p. 7
          .</p>
       
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">ii</span> In other cases medial or final <i>m</i> represents lenited <i>m</i> 
(voiced spirant); in late Mid. and Mod.Ir. denoted by <span class="frn ">mh</span> 
or <span class="frn ">ṁ</span>.</p>
      
      <p class="result"> 
<span class="lbl">E.g.</span> sam (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> Mod.Ir. <span class="frn ">samhradh</span>, W. <span class="frn ">haf</span>), clam (W. <span class="frn ">claf</span>), dam 
`<span class="quote">ox</span>' (W. <span class="frn ">dafad</span> `<span class="ediltr">sheep</span>'), amles (W. <span class="frn ">afles</span>), damnae (W. <span class="frn ">defnydd</span>).</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
In dán díreach lenited m was classed for rhyming purposes 
with <i>bh</i>, <i>dh</i>, <i>gh</i>, <i>l</i>, <i>n</i>, <i>r</i>, see Meyer, <span class="lbl">loc. cit.</span>
      </p>
     
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">2</span> in Lat. loan-words initial m remains: midach (&lt;<span class="frn ">medicus</span>); 
mocol (&lt;<span class="frn ">macula</span>). In early loan-words medial m between 
vowels is lenited: sciam (&lt;<span class="frn ">schēma</span>); domnach (&lt;<span class="frn ">dominic</span>-); 
sechtmain (&lt;<span class="frn ">septimana</span>); but <i>mm</i> in damnaim (&lt;<span class="frn ">damn-are</span>), 
arm (&lt;<span class="frn ">arm-a</span>), comman (&lt;<span class="frn ">commun-ire</span>), <span class="lbl">etc.</span>
      </p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
Occas. Irish lenited <i>m</i> represents Lat. medial <span class="frn ">b</span>: promaim 
(mod. <span class="frn ">fromhaim</span>) &lt;<span class="frn ">probare</span>; conversely mebair (mebuir, 
memair) &lt;<span class="frn ">memoria</span>.</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">3</span> the initial combinations ml, mr which occur in Old and 
early Mid.Ir. give way in the Mid.Ir. period to <span class="frn ">bl</span>, <span class="frn ">br</span>; mlith 
(&lt;mel- `<span class="ediltr">grind</span>'), mrath (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> mairnid) &gt; blith, brath. See 
words given under <i>ml</i>, <i>mr</i>.</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">4</span> O.Ir. medial and final mb&gt;<span class="frn ">mm</span> in the Mid.Ir. period. 
<span class="lbl">E.g.</span> gsm. 
          caimb, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AU" title="eDIL Bibliography">AU</strong></span> 657
          
         (camm `<span class="ediltr">crooked</span>'). 
          cumbae, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AU" title="eDIL Bibliography">AU</strong></span> 829
          
         
(vn. of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=con-ben">com-ben-</a></span>), later cumma. 
          imbairecc, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AU" title="eDIL Bibliography">AU</strong></span> 700
          
        , 
          immbairecc, 
            709
          
         (&lt;imb-air-icc-), beside 
          immarecc, 
            696
          
        . O.Ir. 
imbi (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 imb">imb</a></span> + 3 s. m. pron.), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AU" title="eDIL Bibliography">AU</strong></span> 903
        , 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 8074
        , 
          8098
        , <span class="lbl">etc.</span>, later 
immi, imme.</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
This change had already begun in O.Ir., esp. in forms of 
the copula: ar mad (= armbad), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 26b31
        , commad (= combad), 
          17a23
         (past subj.), and in the prep. imb folld. by another 
proclitic word; see 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> i 118.
        
      </p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">5</span> medial lenited m and b are occasionally interchanged by 
scribes; see for exx. mebair and mimasc given below; Muir 
Robur (&lt;<span class="frn ">mare rubrum</span>), beside Muir Romair; cumrech 
(&lt;com-rig-), beside cuibrech; 
          rot-marmais, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 1680
          
         (= rot-marbais), beside robarbthar (= ro marbthar), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 7121
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BDD" title="eDIL Bibliography">BDD</strong></span> 
80
        ).</p>
