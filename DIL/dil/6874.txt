<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">Landowner</span>, <span class="def">hospitaller</span>, in later sources also <span class="def">farmer</span>, <span class="def">yeoman</span>. 
In legal texts the b.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
         is a rich landowner with a public function 
of dispensing unlimited hospitality to all persons in his 
hostel, which must be in an accessible position. For this he 
is given the same honour-price as the king of a túath
      </span>, 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Cr&#xED;thG." title="eDIL Bibliography">Críth G.</strong></span> p. 79.

       the same 
grade as the flaith or ruling noble if he has twice the qualifying wealth of the noble's grade,
