<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="20074">eneclann, aineclann</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. eneclann, aineclann or <a class="reference" href="20074">dil.ie/20074</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2013</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                enechlann, enechclann</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="gen n.">n.</span> (?) later <span class="gen f.">f.</span> and <span class="gen m.">m.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span>, 
Decl. § 12
      , 
        §10
      . Thurneysen (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xiv 342
      , 
        xvi 206
      ) 
brings the word from <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 enech">1 enech</a></span> and <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=glan">glan</a></span>, the original 
meaning being <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">face-clearing</span>
      </span> (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iv 318x
      , cited under 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 enech II">1 enech II</a></span>). According to Hamp 1 <span class="frn ">enech + </span>
      <span class="frn ">clú</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xxv 266-67
      , 
        see also <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;tudesCelt." title="eDIL Bibliography">Études Celt.</strong></span> xxxii 220-21
      . In later MSS. enechlann is sometimes 
found. 
        enechclann, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Rawl." title="eDIL Bibliography">Rawl.</strong></span> 118b39
        
      , <span class="lbl">etc.</span> </p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">compensation</span> for <span class="def">insult</span>, <span class="def">loss</span>, or <span class="def">injury</span>, <i>for anything constituting a violation of</i> enech (see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 enech">1 enech</a></span>), <i>pro- 
portionate to the status of the person to be compensated</i>.</span> 
In Laws Commentary it frequently renders lóg n-enech (<span class="lbl">e.g.</span> 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 516.10
        ). When the injury consisted in the death of 
a relative the eneclann was proportionate to the status of 
those to whom it was due, thus, although it occasionally 
renders díre (<span class="lbl">e.g.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 42.14
         
          v 50.5 Comm.
        ), it was in its 
early technical meaning distinct from corpdíre, which was 
proportionate to the status of the deceased. Distinguished 
from smacht: 
          cach uair i[s] smacht ictar ann, is a ic fo 
aicned inti icas, ┐ cach uair is eneclann, is a ic fo aicnedh 
inti risi n-ictar, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iii 496.10
          
        . From díre: is lomm a 
díre, is toll a eneclann (of one unworthy of trust), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Tec.Corm." title="eDIL Bibliography">Tec. Corm.</strong></span> § 33.6
        . 
          eneclann, arindi clantar i n-inchaib duine do 
neoch bess dīleas do beodil ┐ marbdile nad cosna ala lām 
fris, lān-lōg einig cāigh fo miad is ed atroillither and, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm.Y" title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm. Y</strong></span> 531
          
        . 
          teora fodla fuil for inchaib duine .i. enicland ┐ 
enechruice ┐ enechgriss (...enecland .i. i lan ┐ i leath), 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 96.5
          
        . 
          cetheora eirca...aithgin ┐ dire, tairgille ┐ 
enecland...it cetheora primfodlai fil forsuidib .i. lan ┐ leth, 
trian ┐ cethramthu, 
            i 258.26
          
        . secht cumala do righ 
tuaithe ina eneclainn, ocht cumala do ri mor-tuaithi, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span>, 509
         (= 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 1260
        ). 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 50
        . 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> ix 171
        . 

          comdire do rig ┐ epscop (.i. comeneclann do rig tuath ┐ 
d'epscop), 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 42.30
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OGr.Cat." title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Gr. Cat.</strong></span> 475.11
        ). in t-ollam 
filead .uii. cumala a eniclann, ┐ .uii. cumala log a aisde 

          v 58.5 Comm.
         (see also 
          116
        ). <span class="trans">
          e.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
           of <span class="def">craftsmen</span>, <span class="def">minstrels</span>, </span> 
<span class="lbl">etc.</span> 
          98 ff. Comm.
         <span class="trans">Of persons in <span class="def">holy orders</span>:</span> 
          nach fer gráid 
oc na bí dlighed na eolus timthirechta a gráid...ni dlig 
saire na eneclainn ḟir gráid i tuaith na i n-eclais, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> i 
218.13
          
        . See also 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 54 ff
        . 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> ix 171
        . For persons 
of a certain status their eneclann was determined by that of 
their lord and patron: 
          aes ciuil ┐ oirfidid oilcena...is a 
hincaib oga mbiad, is as direnaiter (.i. is as sin erniter 
eneclann doib), 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 108.35
          
        ; 
          516.13
        . cipé imress 
mela ┐ mertain...fort, ní ḟuil díri no eneclann duitsiu ind, 
acht na fil dam-sa, dáig fer ar tincur mná atatchomnaic 
<span class="ediltr">you are not entitled to any compensation-rate or honour-price 
except what is due to me, for</span>, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 48
        . <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
          fer cen selb 
cen thothchus, lasmbi bancomarba, a inchuib a mna direnar 
side (.i. a dualgus einig a mna eirnither enecland dosaide), 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 516.5
          
        -
          18
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
is andsin dorimgartseom | a eneclaind for Midir (for 
carrying off his wife), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> ii 4.50
        . ro dligustur 
Buide eineclann a marbhadh a ingine .i. aire foirgill 
meodunuch he ┐ sé cumula do ina eineclann. ro dligustur 
a derbbráthir eineclann do ina marbadh, aire ard hé, ┐ 
ceithri cumula do ina einuclunn `<span class="quote">B. was entitled to honour-price for the killing of his daughter, he was an a. f. of 
the middle rank and was entitled to six cumhals as honour-price. Her brother was also entitled to honour-price for 
her death, he was an a. a. and was entitled to four 
cumhals as his honour-price</span>' 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 76.19
        . 
          dlighidh O 
Néill íoc 'na ḟuil | gibé da mhuintir mharbhthuir | 's ní 
théid eineaclann uadh d'ḟior, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TD" title="eDIL Bibliography">TD</strong></span> 7.6
          
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
Some kindreds had the privilege of having their eneclann 
valued in precious metals: 
          Hui Dergmosaig...ór a ndire 
enechclainne, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Rawl." title="eDIL Bibliography">Rawl.</strong></span> 121a25
          
        . 
          Dal Messiṅ Corb...argat 
bruinte dire a n-enechclainne, 
            118b40
          
        . 
          athich cacha slointe 
di ṡhund huma a n-enechclann, 
            118b39
          
        . <span class="trans">
          e.<span class="oRef">¤</span>
           for <span class="def">injury</span> to 
<span class="def">property</span>:</span> 
          smacht a fidh neimhedh no co mbentar uile ┐ 
eneclann ind o bentar, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> iv 150.17
          
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
Claims involving eneclann were urgent: ni dam enecland anad `<span class="quote">honour-price does not admit of stay</span>' (<span class="ediltr">of distress</span>) 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 120.19
        ; but <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          228.17
        ; 
          ii 100.7
        , and cases of: 
eneclann ar treisi <span class="ediltr">stay of eneclann-claim for three days</span> 
          i 
162.19 Comm.
        
      </p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
In certain cases only fractions of the full value of 
eneclann were due: ac so sis...na heneclanna fuil isna 
cneadaib, in aenmad rann fichet d'eneclann isin mbainbeim 
aicenta, sechtmadh eneclainne isin cnocbeim, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 
694
        &lt;
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span>, 336
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
For eneclann due in regard to literary compositions 
see 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 230 ff
        .</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 

          im log n-enech n-oige .i. in inuclund dlighus in oig [<span class="lbl">sic</span>] 
ina sarugha, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 132.17 Comm.
          
         ni fuiben enecland arain 
dar daim techta `<span class="quote">when the company is above the lawful number 
refusal [to entertain] does not lessen one's honour-price</span>' 
          ii 388. 
1
        . da fichit décc bó tugadh i n-eneclann do Mac M. (as 
satisfaction for the usurping of his abbacy) 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AFM" title="eDIL Bibliography">AFM</strong></span> ii 910.5
        . 
rucadh da fichit dec bó fo tri, i n-ainechlann Muircertaich 
Hui Briain, for Desmumain (M. Ó B. had been slain by the 
king of Desmuma) 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AU" title="eDIL Bibliography">AU</strong></span> 1168
        . In these two exx. it is not 
clear from the context whether the word is used in the 
strict legal sense, or in sense (b).</p>
    
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) in wider sense <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">compensation</span>, <span class="def">satisfaction</span>:</span> 
        nach 
bíodh ar chumas carad an tí do marbhthaoi cúitiughadh 
ná eineaclann do bhuain don ti doghníodh an marbhadh, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Keat." title="eDIL Bibliography">Keat.</strong></span> i 68.37
        
      . <span class="trans">i n-e.</span> <span class="trans">in <span class="def">requital</span> for</span> (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> (<span class="edilb">a</span>), final citt.): i
