<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="39352">T</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. T or <a class="reference" href="39352">dil.ie/39352</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2013</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                tinne, tt, t, t, d, t, th, t, th, t, th, t, d, nt, mt, t, t</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">the seventeenth letter of the Irish alphabet, and in the 
Ogham alphabet the third letter in the second or h- group, 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Introd. § 4
      . Irish name tinne <span class="trans">`<span class="def">holly</span>' </span>: 
        tinne ... .i. 
cuileann, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Auraic." title="eDIL Bibliography">Auraic.</strong></span> 1180
        
      . Note also trían .i. aillinde sin 
aniu `<span class="quote">
        <span class="frn ">trian</span>, t, another thing the meaning of that today</span>,' 

        5549
      . ? Corruption of cuillend é sin aniu '<span class="quote">holly today</span>' 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xxxix 149
      .</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
Frequ. written tt in O. and Mid. Ir. (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/KZ" title="eDIL Bibliography">KZ</strong></span> xxxvi 202 ff.
      ). 
The group st is somet. written sd in Mid. and Mod. Ir.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
Lenited th is somet. confused with ch : 
        tothlugud, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> 
iii 35.12
        
       = 
        tochlugadh, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;tudesCelt." title="eDIL Bibliography">Études Celt.</strong></span> ii 298.10
        
      . t'eichsi, 
teithsi <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Decl. ex. 998
      . chreich, chreith, <span class="lbl">v.l.</span> 
        ex. 
1001
      . <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> sethnu later 
        sechnóin, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xii 287
        
      , and 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> 
Introd. 22.26
      .</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
In O. and Mid. Irish t(t), when initial or preceded by s, 
always represents voiceless t. In other positions it may 
also represent voiced d. In O. Ir. this value of t is regular 
after vowels (cét, mod. céad), optional after consonants. 
See 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thurn." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thurn. Hdb.</strong></span> § 29
      -
        §30
      .</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
Lenition of t is regularly marked in MSS. of all periods. 
The pronunciation of th as h dates (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thurn." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thurn. Hdb.</strong></span> § 119
      ) 
from the 11th century, but according to O'Rahilly, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Hermathena" title="eDIL Bibliography">Hermathena</strong></span> xliv 163 ff.
      , from the end of the 13th. Previous 
to this we must suppose a dental spirant of the type of Germanic þ. th also occasionally alternates with f (pron. h) : 
othrála (ofrála), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> vi 24 z
      , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        cona fuigsin (thuigsin), 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;tudesCelt." title="eDIL Bibliography">Études Celt.</strong></span> i 274.22
        
      , and Mod. Ir. tathaint (—f—). In the 
possessive <span class="per 2">2</span> <span class="number s.">s.</span> (see it) it is sometimes replaced by h— : 

        h'anora, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> vi 81.19
        
      .</p>
  
  
    
      <p class="result">
        t resists or loses lenition :</p>
     
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) When 
geminated (see <span class="lbl">infra</span>).</p>
        
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">b</span>) In the groups st, rt (but frequently —rth— in part., see 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thurn." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thurn. Hdb.</strong></span> § 714
          ), lt, cht.</p>
        
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">c</span>) In 
common with d, after final n of a preceding word.</p>
        
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">d</span>) Frequently before initial s- of a closely-following word : 
            in 
chrut-so, —sin, 
              <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 211b4
            
          , 
            63a14
          . 
            in slogattsa, 
              <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 398a18
            
          . 
Note also fáitsine `<span class="quote">prophecy</span>,' 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 30d23
          , in Ml fáissine 
fáithsine, <span class="lbl">q.v.</span> See also 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Introd. § 33
          , 
            § 34
          .</p>
      
     
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">e</span>) When 
a geminated sound results from contact of th and th or dh : 
-mitter &lt;*mid-ther, indnite &lt;*ind-nith-the. See 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thurn." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thurn. Hdb.</strong></span> § 134
          , Meyer 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xiii 191.
          
        </p>
      
    
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
Nasalisation of t is seldom marked in Old and Middle 
Irish, but note occasional spellings like 
        con·danicc, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 3c27
        
      , 
beside 
        con·tanic, 
          3a1
        
      , and the stereotyped oldaas (taas). 
The spelling tt is found occasionally in O. Ir., more frequently in Mid. Ir. In Mod. Ir. nasalisation is marked by a 
prefixed d-. <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> and trí naraid <span class="ediltr">their three charioteers</span>, 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-I1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-I¹</strong></span> 804
      . See 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xi 148
      -
        9
      .</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
t frequently results from original d in contact with lenited 
ṡ: díltud &lt;*
        dí-ṡlonduth, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thurn." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thurn. Hdb.</strong></span> § 128
        
      . intamail 
&lt;*indṡamail, int ṡúil, int ṡaeguil. This has given rise to the 
prefixing of t (where etymologically unjustifiable) as a 
sign of lenition : 
        rochōirigh a tsleg re thaeb, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xiii 220.1
        
      . 

        tainic crich tsaogail, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TTebe" title="eDIL Bibliography">TTebe</strong></span> 3718
        
      , 
        dar mullach tsleibi Parnars, 
          3714
        
      . Note also : 
        astsruth, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 1665
        
      . 
        istsruth, 

          1682
        
      . 
        riastṡluag, 
          2129
        
      . See 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Introd. 17.19
      . th arises 
from dh+h (&lt;ṡ) in miathamla &lt;miad+ṡamail, see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> 
i 408
      . t &lt;dh after l, n, is found in rélto (gs. of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=r&#xE9;lad">rélad</a></span>). ingantach (ingnad), <span class="lbl">etc.</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> i 417
      . Also after voiceless sounds : 

        macthi, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 12c9
        
       (—de), though in such cases etymological 
considerations often cause the original d to be restored : 
mucde <span class="frn ">suinus</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 37b9
      , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thurn." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thurn. Hdb.</strong></span> § 121
      . There are 
also some cases of th for d due, apparently, to variations in 
pronunciation, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> búaith (—d), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 11a7
      . 
        díthnad, 
          14b15
        
       
(do-dona), more usually dídnad. t also represents the 
voiced d arising from *nt, *mt in words like cotlud &lt;contuil, cét &lt;*kṃtom <span class="lbl">etc.</span>, and the voiceless t from k+d in 
etlae &lt;*ek(s)dāl-, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> </p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
In loanwords t represents W. <span class="frn ">t, th, dd</span> (exx. 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> i 23
      , 

        24
      , 
        509
      ), Lat. <span class="frn ">t, tt</span> ( 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> i 231
      , 
        4
      ). Also W. <span class="frn ">t=d</span> ( 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> i 24
      ), 
and intervocalic and final Lat. <span class="frn ">t</span> in words borrowed through 
W. ( 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> i 232
      , 
        3
      ).</p>
