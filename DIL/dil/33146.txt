<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="33146">2 ní</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 2 ní or <a class="reference" href="33146">dil.ie/33146</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2013</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                ní, neich, neith, ní, neche, neiche, neithe, neithinna, nídh, ní, neith, neithe, aen-, én-, ní</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   




    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos subst." style="color: #ff8000">subst.</span> <span class="gen n.">n.</span> and <span class="gen m.">m.</span> <span class="trans">a <span class="def">thing</span>
      </span>. The transition from indef. pron. 
to subst. is gradual and begins already in O.Ir., <span class="lbl">cf.</span> mór ní (in 
Glosses) <span class="ediltr">a great thing</span>, gen. used <span class="pos adverbial" style="color: #ff8000">adverbial</span>ly = <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">greatly</span>:</span> 
        mór 
ní bes n-adblamu foir ar soírad, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 2d14
        
       (<span class="ediltr">greatly will our 
salvation be readier on him</span> = <span class="ediltr">how much readier will he be to 
save us</span>). mór ní as dénti ní airriu <span class="ediltr">how much the more is something to be done for them</span>, 
        15a23
      . cia fíu .i. mór ní (gl. quam 
folld. by an adj.), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 17b13
      , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        138c14
      , 
        140b9
      , 
        146d1
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 
25a25
      . gl. quanto, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 80a5
      ; quanta, 
        82c4
      , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 16b12
      . Preceded by indef. pron. adj.: na ní ararogart som <span class="ediltr">anything which 
he has forbidden</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 5c23
      . na nní robu thol dó do frithoircnib 
 <span class="ediltr">whatever injuries he wished (to do)</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 33a18
      . condomroib . . . 
na ní ara ṁbáigiu <span class="ediltr">whatever I contend for</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> Ep. 360.
       <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
also the compd. nephní <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">nothing</span>
      </span> (see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=neimn&#xED;">neimní</a></span>). The transition 
was further aided by confusion with the O.Ir. demonstr. 
pron. <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=2 &#xED;">2 í</a></span>, in neut. with art. a n-í (siu, sin) <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">this</span>, <span class="def">that</span>
      </span>, which was 
later analysed as a-ní; aní is common in Mid.Ir. both as 
demonstr. and introducing rel. clause, being used indifferently 
with the forms anní, in ní. Or rather abstracted from na-nní, na ní: see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 nach">1 nach</a></span> (N 5.37), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> xxi 175-77
      .</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
As <span class="pos subst." style="color: #ff8000">subst.</span> in <span class="case d">d</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> after a prep. the form ní supersedes O.Ir. 
neuch (neoch), which is reserved for use as an indef. pron. 
(see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=nech III">nech III</a></span> above). <span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> neich, later neith (already in PH). 
<span class="number Dual">Dual</span> and <span class="number pl.">pl.</span> forms occur: <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number du.">du.</span> 
        dá ní
        , 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 110a30
        
      . 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 8392.
       
<span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number du.">du.</span> 
        dá neche
        , 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CCath." title="eDIL Bibliography">CCath.</strong></span> 1008.
        
       <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="case a">a</span>
      <span class="number p.">p.</span> neiche, later neithe (already 
in PH). 
        neithinna, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BCC" title="eDIL Bibliography">BCC</strong></span> 150.25
        
      . Mod.Ir. nídh (
        ní
      ), <span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> neith and 
neithe.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
 <span class="trans">A <span class="def">thing</span> (<span class="def">matter</span>, <span class="def">item</span>, <span class="def">fact</span>, <span class="def">circumstance</span>)</span> in wide sense, less 
definite and concrete than rét; employed in many cases in 
the same way as <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 n&#xED;">1 ní</a></span> but with more definite signification. 
Rarely accompanied by an adj. of quality: ni gua ēm a crand 
. . . atchuaid in fer-sa <span class="ediltr">a false thing is the tree of which this man 
has told</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> ii 120 § 59
       (Tenga Bithnúa). a ḟlaith Cermna can 
ni clé `<span class="quote">without partiality</span>', 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CRR" title="eDIL Bibliography">CRR</strong></span> 54.
       mór-ní do cenelchi <span class="ediltr">a great 
part of the kindred</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CCath." title="eDIL Bibliography">CCath.</strong></span> 804.
       <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> acht Issu de doinib | as 
amram ni genair <span class="ediltr">the most wonderful thing (being) born of men</span>, 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l.2" title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.²</strong></span> Sept. 24
       (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Rawl." title="eDIL Bibliography">Rawl.</strong></span> 505
      ), perh. an inversion for: ní as amram 
génair.</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
Preceded by art. or pron. adj.: 
        ni hingnath anni sin, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Hib.Min." title="eDIL Bibliography">Hib. Min.</strong></span> 5.154
        
       (perh. = a n-í). 
        ba saeth leissi in ní sin, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> i 124.24
        
      . 
adfes do M. ind ní <span class="ediltr">this (fact) was told to M.</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Mon.Tall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Mon. Tall.</strong></span> 34.
       
        ro 
fitir cech ní for bith, aingil oc indisi cech neich do, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 1976.
        
       
cethri rigshuide déc . . . co mbet fo thorud cech ni dib <span class="ediltr">each 
one of them</span>, 
        1255
      . forémdid nach ní dó <span class="ediltr">he was not able to do 
anything to him</span>, 
        4891
      . d'fhoillsiughadh an neith seo <span class="ediltr">to demonstrate this fact</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Keat." title="eDIL Bibliography">Keat.</strong></span> i 224.56
       (an neithe-si <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>). In late lang. 
the sense <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">anything</span>
      </span> is rendered by the compd.

      : 

        adubairt . . . nach d'iarraidh ēn-neth ele tanic, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> vi 63.30
        
      . 

        d'iarraidh aoin-neithe ar Ghuaire, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Keat." title="eDIL Bibliography">Keat.</strong></span> iii 981.
        
      
    </p>

    <p class="result">

or hearing): co cualatar aní na ndochum. boscairi na n-ech 
 <span class="ediltr">they heard something approaching (namely)</span> . . .,
