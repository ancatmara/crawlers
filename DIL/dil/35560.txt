      <span class="itype o">o</span> (acc. to 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> ii 621 derived
       from the verbal root sech- 
compounded with ro; <span class="lbl">cf.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=cosc">cosc</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=t&#xE1;sc">tásc</a></span>, <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aithesc">aithesc</a></span>) apparently</p>
   
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) 
<span class="trans">a short <span class="def">poem</span>, <span class="def">ode</span> or <span class="def">chant</span>
        </span>, of which two supposed exx., taken 
from a 16th cent. MS., are given in 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> i 133
        ; both composed 
in syllabic verse, without rhyme but strongly alliterated. 
(The abbrev. .r. often added in the margin of MSS. over 
against a rhapsodical utterance, and by 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wi.W&#xF6;rterbuch" title="eDIL Bibliography">Wi. Wörterbuch</strong></span> 
p. 748
         assigned to this word, more probably stands for 
retoiric, see 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Heldensage" title="eDIL Bibliography">Heldensage</strong></span> p. 54
        , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> p. 108
        . The word does 
not seem to occur in this sense in early Mid.Ir. lit., and 
Thurneysen (<span class="lbl">l.c.</span>) suggests that the use is due to a misunderstanding of the abbreviation):
           itbert an rosg, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 3533
          
         
(Stowe) = rábert na briathra, LL. 
          dichetal for otrach .i. 
adhbhal-cantainn le rosg nó orrtha, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Triads" title="eDIL Bibliography">Triads</strong></span> p. 39.4
          
         (gloss 
on

        ).

          tus ruisc,
