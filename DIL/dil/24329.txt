<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="24329">fri</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. fri or <a class="reference" href="24329">dil.ie/24329</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2019</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                frimm, rimm, frim, frím, frium, frimsa, friumsa, frit, fritt, friutt, fritsu, rut, rutt, fris, friss, frissom, f., frie, friae, frise, frie, frinn, rinn, rind, friunn, riund, frind, frinni, raindi, frinnai, frib, frithib, fribsi, friu, friú, friu, friü, frithiu, friithu, friusom, riom, rum, riot, rut, ris, ria, rinn, ruinn, ribh, ruibh, riú, r, frim, frit, fria, friar, fribar, fria, frim, frim, frim, ram, frit, frit, frit, rit, rat, rot, friar, friar, fribar, fribar, fria, fria, fria, re-na, rena, fria, fris(s)a, frisi-, risi-, fris-, ris-, frismbiat, fris-mbruchta, fris, fris, fris, risa, frisin-, frisnid, risnad, ris, fria, frisa, re n-, fria-, re, ra-n-adh, re, re, ré, freisin, FRI</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   

    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos prep." style="color: #ff8000">prep.</span> with acc. For etymology see Thurneysen, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> 
§ 839
      , Fraser 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> viii 37
      , J. E. C. Williams, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> iii 
126
      . In later spelling prefixes h- to following initial 
stressed vowel; does not leníte following consonant (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> 

        frinna aill, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 45a16
        
      . 
        fri nnach ..., 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 75a9
        
      , fri nnech 

        23c20
      ; but fri nech further on in same gloss and regularly 
elsewhere); the initial s- of the article following fri is 
preserved. In Middle Irish gradually replaced by the 
forms RI, RE (already in Ml: 
        oc eregim re Abisolon, 
          44b4
        
      , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        fre, 
          90c27
        
      ); RA (common in 
some LL texts, see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CRR" title="eDIL Bibliography">CRR</strong></span> 54
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 499
      , 
        602
      , 
        740
      , <span class="lbl">etc.</span>); rarely 
RO (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 1990
       
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xiii 204 15
      ); FRIA, RIA, RE also common. 
Hence in Early Mod. Ir. there is occasionally confusion with 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=6 r&#xE9;">ré</a></span>, RÍA `<span class="quote">before</span>', see Bergin's note 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Stor.Keat." title="eDIL Bibliography">Stor. Keat.</strong></span> p. xix
       , and section <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=fri IX (e)">IX (e)</a></span> below. Apart from this 
the latter prep. sometimes replaces FRI after fáilid, fáilte 
and kindred words (as in Mod. Ir.). On the parallelism 
of some of the pronominal forms of FRI with those of LA 
see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> <span class="lbl">l.c.</span>
       (with Translator's 
        n. 178
      ). 
Though FRI still appears in some later texts, especially 
those of the archaising type, it had become obsolete in the 
classical language. Its later form (RI, RE) remains down 
to the end of the Early Mod. period and is recognized by 
the grammarians as distinct from LE (LA), see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span>, Introd. 
§ 75
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/24P8" title="eDIL Bibliography">24 P 8</strong></span>, 194
       (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> VI
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Bard.Synt.Tr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Bard. Synt. Tr.</strong></span> p. 7
      ). In the 
modern spoken lang. it is replaced partly by <span class="frn ">LE</span>, partly by 
<span class="frn ">CHUN</span> (O.-Ir. dochum). For a contrary development <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
Engl. `<span class="frn ">with</span>,' originally `<span class="frn ">against</span>,' which has absorbed 
the functions of A.S. <span class="frn ">mid</span> `<span class="ediltr">(together) with</span>.'</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
In development of meaning generally FRI shows a 
remarkable parallelism with Engl. `<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">with</span>
      </span>'; it also agrees 
with `<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">against</span>
      </span>' in several senses.</p>
