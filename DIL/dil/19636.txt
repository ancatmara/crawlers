<span class="edilb">III</span> In Laws (usually as <span class="pos vn." style="color: #ff8000">vn.</span> of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=in-cuirethar">in-cuirethar</a></span>) <span class="trans">a <span class="def">putting 
in</span>
        </span>, esp. of putting or bringing into a territory the symbols 
of taking possession, <span class="trans">making <span class="def">entry</span>
        </span> (see

Applied to the <span class="trans">putting in to the pound of distrained cattle</span>; 
the relation between the moment of actual impounding 
and the arrival of relief being of legal importance, three 
cases are distinguished: rianeacur, where the impounding 
is made only shortly before [after (?)] the intervention to stay 
it; ecor, where impounding and such intervention are simultaneous ;and iarnecor, where the cattle have been taken 
to the plaintiff's house before the intervention, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> ii 110
        - 

          112 Comm.

          iar n-a breith cu ruice in eacur .i. cu

            112.23 Comm.
