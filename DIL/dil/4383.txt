      <span class="pos v" style="color: #ff8000">v</span> (*
        ess-ber-, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> ii 466
        
      ). 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Verbs § 6
      . In the 
prot. -epir there is some fluctuation between e- and a-, 
see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> p. 54
      , 
        508
       and forms <span class="lbl">infra</span>.</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
 
In Mid. Ir. a form <span class="frn ">atbeir</span> (prot. <span class="frn ">-epir</span>, <span class="frn ">-ap(a)ir</span>) replaces 
 
<span class="frn ">as-beir</span>, see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> p. 268
      . Later this becomes 
<span class="frn ">a(t)deir</span> (prot. <span class="frn ">-apair</span>) and is the usual form in late Mid. 
and Mod. Ir. Occasionally adeir (sometimes deir) is given 
the endings of a simple verb, see <span class="lbl">infra</span>. The early conjugation 
(<span class="mood &#x101;-subj.">ā-subj.</span>, <span class="tns &#x113;-fut.">ē-fut.</span>, <span class="tns t-pret.">t-pret.</span>) continues with Mid. Ir. personal 
endings. In the pret. new stem-formations appear: adubart 
(&lt; adrubart); adebart, adébart (? conflation of adubart 
and -epert); adobart, adóbart. In depend. position -dubart; 
-ebert, -ebart, -ébart, -debart, -débart; -óbart, -dobart, 
-dóbart. In classical verse (IGT <span class="lbl">loc. cit.</span>) adubart, adobart 
alliterate with <span class="ediltr">d</span>; adóbart, adébart with a vowel and with <i>d</i>.</p>
