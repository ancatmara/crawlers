<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="14880">dechenc</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. dechenc or <a class="reference" href="14880">dil.ie/14880</a></span><br/></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   


    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype o">o</span>. <span class="trans">A <span class="def">pair</span> of <span class="def">persons</span>.</span> Cf. <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=trecheng">trecheng</a></span>. The 
want of eclipsis, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> Mar. 6
       (decheng deodae), points 
to <span class="gen masculine">masculine</span> gender. The frequent writing drecheng after 
prím and mór does not prove <span class="gen neuter">neuter</span> gender, as it may 
be = threcheng.—By Irish grammarians associated with 
cing `<span class="ediltr">a champion</span>' or with cenn `<span class="ediltr">a head' (`a person</span>'): 
decheng .i. dá ching nó dá chend, O'Curry Transcr. LB. 

        cing .i. trénfer <span class="frn ">ut est</span> inmain deceng deoda, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thr.Ir.Gl." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thr. Ir. Gl.</strong></span> 130.
16

        trecheng .i. tríar .i. trí cinn móra, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span>

        deicheng,

        trechenn, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BDD" title="eDIL Bibliography">BDD</strong></span> § 93
        
      . —The inflexion is against 
the derivation †<span class="frn ">dé-ching</span>. Besides dechenc can hardly be 
separated from 
        trethenc, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 29 c 5
        
      : 
        <span class="frn ">cui gloria honor et 
imperium sempeternum</span> .i. is trethenc comadas do <span class="ediltr">it is a 
fitting triad to him</span>. The relations between this and 
trechenc, dechenc are obscure. In Mid.-Ir. <span class="frn ">treicheng</span> and 
<span class="frn ">treitheng</span> would render one and the same pronunciation 
(†<span class="frn ">treheŋ</span>).—Dech-enc and treth-enc either cpds. with 
†<span class="frn ">cenc</span>, †<span class="frn ">tenc</span>, or derivatives of Pre-Celt. and Indo-Eur. 
†<span class="frn ">dueiko-</span>, <span class="frn ">duiko-</span>, and †<span class="frn ">tṛt[i]o-</span>, <span class="frn ">trit[i]o-</span>; the former is represented by Teut. <span class="frn ">†tvī˘ha(n)</span>- <span class="ediltr">consisting of two</span> (also of persons 
as O. Eng. <span class="frn ">mid unc twîh</span> <span class="ediltr">between us twain</span>), Scr. <span class="frn ">dvika-</span>; 
the latter by Lat. <span class="frn ">tertius</span>, Teut. <span class="frn ">þridjan-</span>, Gk. <span class="frn ">τρισσός</span> and 
W. <span class="frn ">trydydd</span>. Apart from the gender trethenc seems identical 
with ON <span class="frn ">priðjungr</span> <span class="ediltr">a third</span>, dechenc with O. Eng. <span class="frn ">twēoung</span> 
(†<span class="frn ">twīhungō-</span>). For the treatment of unaccented -nk, <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 cumang">1 cumang</a></span> (con-icc) and crobang `<span class="quote">a fistful</span>' which seems a 
derivative of crob.</p>
