    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos v" style="color: #ff8000">v</span> 
      domnaid (rhyming with fognam SR).</p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
 <span class="edilb">I</span> <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">Ties</span>, <span class="def">fastens</span>, <span class="def">binds</span>
        </span>: Stokes, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm.Tr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm. Tr.</strong></span> 164. 8
        . Meyer, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Contribb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Contribb.</strong></span> 587
        . 
          damnais .i. ceanglais, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/StoweGl." title="eDIL Bibliography">Stowe Gl.</strong></span> 83
          
        . </p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
 <span class="edilb">II</span> Usually like Gk. <span class="frn ">δάμνημι</span>, <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">subdues</span>, <span class="def">conquers</span>; of animals 
 <span class="def">breaks</span> in.</span> sluaig síl Adaim ... rodomnad ... do ḟognam Demuin 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 2675
        . 
          rodamnad ... dadrad hídal is arracht, 
            <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 2679
          
        . 

          rodamnad co sáthib slóg Ramath cathir na nAmmon, 
            <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 
6731
          
        . damainti <span class="ediltr">he subdues him</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 689
         Y. 
          rodamnaid 
┐ roslechtaid saermilid andsin, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Alex." title="eDIL Bibliography">Alex.</strong></span> 270
          
        . 
          Lomna domnais 
drechnatha (<span class="lbl">v.l.</span> drecht ndatha), 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 87 <span class="lbl">upper margin</span>
          
         
(see 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xxii 394
        ); Meyer translates: <span class="ediltr">L. bound together 
poems (staves</span>). 
          Luan Deaa damnus (.i. chenglus) cach

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> v 485. 17

      <p class="result"> 
Sense I is not well supported. The passages in 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm." title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm.</strong></span> 
<span class="lbl">s.v.</span> udmad
        ; 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 311 b 11
        : domnais (gl. .i. cenglais, whence 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/StoweGl." title="eDIL Bibliography">Stowe Gl.</strong></span> 83
         might have been taken) giallu gall co coic 
assa Alpion (
          co coictaib arda Alpeoin, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Rawl." title="eDIL Bibliography">Rawl.</strong></span> 115 b 19
          
        ); 
 
and 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> v 485. 17
         (cited above), are not sufficient to prove 
the meaning <span class="ediltr">tie</span>. In fact, the meaning <span class="ediltr">tie, bind</span> would not 
be expected with this root, whether one sees in <span class="frn ">damnaim</span> 
the Celtic equivalent of Gk. <span class="frn ">δάμνημι</span> (as Strachan proposes, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/VSR" title="eDIL Bibliography">VSR</strong></span> p. 61
        ), or, as might also be suggested, a 
loanword from Lat. <span class="frn ">dominari</span> (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> the <span class="itype o">o</span>-form domnaim, not 
found with <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 damnaid">1 damnaid</a></span>), which in time fell together with 
<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 damnaid">1 damnaid</a></span> (from Lat. <span class="frn ">damnare</span>). The true Irish cognate of 
<span class="frn ">δάμνημι</span> is <span class="frn ">daimim</span>, Celtic agreeing with Scr. and Germ. in 
forming the present with <span class="itype io">io</span>. The complete absence of 
primary inflexion and of a vn. formed directly from the 
root is against the comparison <span class="frn ">damnaim</span>: 
          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 150
        .</p>
