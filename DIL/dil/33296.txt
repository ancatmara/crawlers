<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="33296">2 nómad</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 2 nómad or <a class="reference" href="33296">dil.ie/33296</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                nómaide, naemhaidhe, naemhaidhe, nómhaidi, nómaidhe</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype &#x101;">ā</span>, <span class="gen f.">f.</span> and nómaide <span class="itype i&#x101;">iā</span>, <span class="gen f.">f.</span> (later and commoner form): 
in early Mod.Ir. also with diphthong naemhaidhe. naemhaidhe 
        nómhaidi, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Dec. § 3.31
        
      . <span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> 
        nómaidhe, 
          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> ex. 
145
        
      .</p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">in strict sense <i>a specified</i> <span class="def">period</span> <i>of time</i>
        </span>, defined by Loth, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xxv 134 fg
        . as <span class="trans">9 days and 9 nights</span>; by Stokes, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xii 122
         
as <span class="trans">9 periods of 8 hours</span> (or 3 days); by Meyer, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MacCongl." title="eDIL Bibliography">MacCongl.</strong></span> Gloss.
         
as an ennead of <span class="trans">9 hours (= 3½ days)</span>; and by Thurneysen, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xiv 348
         as an ennead of <span class="trans">12 hours (= 4½ days)</span>; this last 
definition is supported by 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> ii 240.19 fg.
        , a passage in 
which the processes in preparing malt are given with the 
time allotted to each: la co n-aidchi (.i. laithe aicinti) i folc 
┐ tri la (.i. laithe co leith aicinta) for dibuirsin ┐ nomad (.i. 
nomad saerdha sain, secht laithe aicinta uile sain) a comlugad 
fo cotuige; from which it appears that 1+1½ `natural' days 
(days of 24 hours) together with a `nómad' made up 7 natural 
days, hence a `nómad' = <span class="trans">4½ natural days</span>. The term also 
appears equated with noínden: 
          conid de baí in cess for 
Ulltaib fri re nomaide, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Dinds.</strong></span> 94
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xvi 45.15
        ); see also 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> 
iii 240.7
         quoted below.</p>
     
      <p class="result"> 
In literature the word seems to be used rather loosely for 
<span class="trans">a period of 3 days</span> or somewhat more; freq. mentioned as 
the time for which a person dangerously wounded or ill 
lingers on or conversely as the time within which a cure is 
effected; <span class="lbl">cf.</span> mod. 
          naomhaidhe `<span class="quote">a period (usually nine days) allowed by surgeons,<span class="lbl">etc.</span> for resting an injured limb . . . the period after which a sick person is declared out of danger</span>', 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Dinn." title="eDIL Bibliography">Dinneen</strong></span>
          
        . 
          ro aínius nómaid, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 1350
          
         = 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Imr.Brain" title="eDIL Bibliography">Imr. Brain</strong></span> ii 292.9
         
(<span class="pos advbl." style="color: #ff8000">advbl.</span> <span class="case acc.">acc.</span>). 
          ro batar co cenn nómidi ann . . . ic fledugud, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> i 129.6
          
        . co cend nómaide ro an | 'sin tṡíd glóraide glé-glan 
`<span class="quote">till three days were out</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Metr.Dinds." title="eDIL Bibliography">Metr. Dinds.</strong></span> iii 350.37
         (naemaide <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>). 

          inti assa teilced-side fuil, is marb re cind nomaide, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 5955
          
         
= 
          ba m. re ndé nomaide, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-I1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-I¹</strong></span> 1489.
          
         <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 2925.
         
          conid 
erbalad ria ndē nomaide, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Fianaig." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fianaig.</strong></span> 36.12
          
        . 
          a righan . . . do éc 
dia cumaidh ria cenn nomaidhe, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AU" title="eDIL Bibliography">AU</strong></span> ii 52.4
          
         (<span class="lbl">a.</span> 
          1093
        , of Margaret queen of Scotland who died on the fourth day after her 
husband's death, see 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xxv 134
        ). 
          na filid dott'aorad . . . co 
rabuit i talmain ria nomaide, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ACL" title="eDIL Bibliography">ACL</strong></span> iii 325.11
          
        . dosbēraind do 
chorp i talmain . . . re nómaide anocht `<span class="quote">before long to-night</span>' 
(i.e. before the time for which you might yet linger has expired?), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/MacCongl." title="eDIL Bibliography">MacCongl.</strong></span> 107.10
         (H. 3.18 text). 
          da mbethea nómhaidhe gan bhiadh, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Duan.F." title="eDIL Bibliography">Duan. F.</strong></span> i 82
          
         (
          xxxi 5
        ; <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          atú gan 
biadh teóra tráth, 
            <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 1
          
        ). 
          `Ca ḟat beither ic á leighes?' `Ré 
nómaide' ar Libra primliaig, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Acall." title="eDIL Bibliography">Acall.</strong></span> 5260

          ré naemaide, 
            5263

§ 11
        . With poss. pron. (later use): ro fhagaib . . . nā comaillfed a nomaidhi 'na beathaidh in tuata na [=no] blaisfed feoil,

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/BB" title="eDIL Bibliography">BB</strong></span> 233a29

          a n-ég araon a cend a naomuidhi,
