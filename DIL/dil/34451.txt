<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="34451">pólaire</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. pólaire or <a class="reference" href="34451">dil.ie/34451</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2013</span><br/></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> a loan-word through British or Vulgar Latin (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xxxiv 37-38
      ) from Lat. <span class="frn ">pugillare, -ia</span>, 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> i 222
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thurn." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thurn. Hdb.</strong></span> 517
      , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> Mid.W. <span class="frn ">peullawr</span>; meaning 
doubtful, <span class="trans">apparently some article forming part of the equipment of a bishop (or priest?) in early Christian times; generally by edd. transld. <span class="def">writing-tablet</span>(s)</span>, a sense borne by Lat. 
<span class="frn ">pugillarius</span> and Lat. <span class="frn ">pugillares</span> (see Du Cange, who also gives exx. 
of the latter word used for tubes for imbibing the sacrificial 
wine).</p>
   
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">1</span>) 
          dubbert Pátricc cumtach du Fíacc. idon clocc ┐ menstir 
┐ bachall ┐ poolire, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> ii 241.17
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ardm." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ardm.</strong></span> 18a1
        ) = 
          dobert P. 
cumdach do Ḟ. .i. clocc, meinistir, bachall, pólairi, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Trip." title="eDIL Bibliography">Trip.</strong></span> 
190.14
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Rawl.B512" title="eDIL Bibliography">Rawl. B 512</strong></span>, f. 22b1
        ); transld. <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">tablets</span>
        </span> in both texts. 
Acc. to McCarthy, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AU" title="eDIL Bibliography">AU</strong></span> iv p. cvii fg.
        , the ` polaire' referred to 
here was `<span class="quote">the native equivalent of pugillare in the sense of 
Paschal Table</span>', the <span class="number pl.">pl.</span> ` polairi' being used in the sense of 
writing-tablets.</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">2</span>) iarndóe inna ndegaid ┐ gaile fora gúalaind . . . Benen 
ina ndegaidh ┐ a ḟolaire fora muin `<span class="quote">tablets</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Trip." title="eDIL Bibliography">Trip.</strong></span> 46.33
         (&lt; 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Eg.93" title="eDIL Bibliography">Eg. 93</strong></span> f. 3b1
        ); Bénen in the rear of Patrick and his company 
appears to Loegaire's men as a fawn and the ` polaire' as a 
spot (gaile = O.Ir. caile) on its shoulder. In the extract from 
this text in H. 3.18 the word is glossed .i. ainm do tēig liubair, 
i.e. <span class="ediltr">a book-satchel</span> (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ACL" title="eDIL Bibliography">ACL</strong></span> iii 16.9
        , 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Trip." title="eDIL Bibliography">Trip.</strong></span> p. li
        ), but in ex. 4 
below, polaire is distinguished from tiag lebuir. The corresp. 
passage in LB gives: 
          ēnloeg allaid ina ndiaid ┐ én find fora 
gualaind .i. Binen sin ┐ polire Patraic fora muin, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LB" title="eDIL Bibliography">LB</strong></span> 27a43
          
         
(
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Trip." title="eDIL Bibliography">Trip.</strong></span> 458.4
        ).</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">3</span>) 
          sood a pólaire ina etun, iss é comartha bías fair, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> 
xxi 386.11
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 1424
         = 
          soud a pholaire, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/YBL" title="eDIL Bibliography">YBL</strong></span> 121a34
          
        ); the sign 
by which Antichrist, who will come in the guise of a 
messenger of the Gospel, will be known; Dottin translates: 
<span class="quote">une inscription qui tourne dans son front, voilà le signe qui 
sera sur lui</span> (?). The sense may be `<span class="ediltr">the turning of his p. to 
front of him</span>', <span class="lbl">i.e.</span> his carrying it in front of him instead of on 
his back, as in ex. 2; but <span class="lbl">cf.</span> tomm liath hi certmedón a édain 
(of Antichrist), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 7270
        . </p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">4</span>) 
          ba bés dosum [<span class="lbl">sc.</span> Colum C.] crossa ┐ polaire ┐ tiaga 
lebor ┐ aídme eclastacda do dénum, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LB" title="eDIL Bibliography">LB</strong></span> 32b50
          
        , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Lism.L." title="eDIL Bibliography">Lism. L.</strong></span> 969
         
(`<span class="trans">
          <span class="def">writing-tablets</span>
        </span>'). <span class="case g">g</span>
        <span class="number p.">p.</span> 
          cét polaire, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LB" title="eDIL Bibliography">LB</strong></span> 32b53
          
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">5</span>) doarfaidh doib iarsin pollere Brenaind . . . ro-do-slechtatur-som uile don pollaire `<span class="quote">tablet</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> x 72.10
         (polaire <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>).</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">6</span>) polaire .i. comhardha <span class="trans">a <span class="def">sign</span>, <span class="def">token</span>
        </span>, O'Cl.; perh. due to 
misunderstanding of ex. 3 above, or a different word (&lt; pol); <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
polaire .i. comhartha `<span class="quote">a mark or sign, <span class="lbl">i.e.</span> the polar constellation</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/P.OC." title="eDIL Bibliography">P. O'C.</strong></span>
