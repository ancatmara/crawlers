<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="1">A</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. A or <a class="reference" href="1">dil.ie/1</a></span><br/></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
  
  
    <p class="result">
      <span class="trans">first letter of the Irish alphabet, and first in the <i>a</i>-group 
in Ogam alphabet. Irish name <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=ailm">ailm</a></span>, <span class="lbl">q.v.</span>
      </span>
    </p>
  
  
    <p class="result">In later lang. <i>ă</i> is used as glide with <i>e</i> before neutral cons. 
(see under e), and is used itself with follg. glide -<i>i</i>- before 
palatal cons. with increasing regularity from the O.Ir. period 
on; also as glide in O.Ir. before final -<i>e</i>, -<i>i</i> preceded by 
neutral cons. (daltae, daltai, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>), see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> § 87
      , 

        § 98
      . <i>ă</i> appears for <i>o</i> in certain phonetic environments, 
        § 81
      . 

        § 82
      , 
        § 95
      ; on the alteration <i>a/e</i> (daig, dego; saigid, segait) see 

        § 83
       and 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Celtica" title="eDIL Bibliography">Celtica</strong></span> iii 182
      ; on the spellings <i>au, ai</i> before u-quality 
consonants see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> § 80
      . In the classical lang. <i>ă</i> 
alternates for metrical purposes with <i>ŏ</i> and <i>ŭ</i> (even in stressed 
syllables with certain limitations), and <i>á</i> with <i>ó</i>, see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TD" title="eDIL Bibliography">TD</strong></span> Introd. 
lxvi
      , 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Introd. § 141
      , 
        § 143
      . Long <i>á</i> is sometimes spelt 
<i>aa</i> in O.Ir., see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> § 27
      .</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
In native words <i>ă</i> represents Indo-European *<span class="frn ">ĕ</span> and *<span class="frn ">ə</span>, 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> § 50
      ; original *<span class="frn ">ā</span> in final unstressed syllables, 

        § 93
      ; original *<span class="frn ">e</span> in certain proclitics (la, amm, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>), 
        § 115
      . 
It appears as a secondary vowel after syncope and loss of 
final syllables with nasals, <i>l</i> and <i>r</i> (immainse, cétal, arathar), 

        § 112
      . It also arises with original vocalic sonants (other 
than *<span class="frn ">i</span> and *<span class="frn ">u</span>) in certain phonetic contexts, 
        §§ 213, 215
      .</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
Long <i>á</i> represents Indo-European *<span class="frn ">ā</span> and *<span class="frn ">ō</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> § 51
      , and also *<span class="frn ">ă</span> after compensatory lengthening, 

        § 125
      .</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
The diphthong <i>aí, ái</i> represents Indo-European *<span class="frn ">ai</span> and 
*<span class="frn ">oi</span>, 
        §§ 66 ff.
      , and <i>aú, áu</i> (archaic) Indo-European *<span class="frn ">au</span>, *<span class="frn ">ou</span>, 
<span class="lbl">etc.</span>, 
        § 69
      .</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
In Latin loanwords <i>ă</i> represents <i>ă</i>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> § 121
      , and somet. 
<i>ŏ</i>, 
        § 122.
       Long <i>á</i> represents <i>ā</i>, 
        § 126
      ; and occasionally <i>ă</i>, 

        § 121
      . The diphthong <i>aí</i> goes back to <i>ae</i> in 
        saígul, 
          § 132
        
      .</p>
