<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="259">ad-aig</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. ad-aig or <a class="reference" href="259">dil.ie/259</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2019</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                a-tom-aig, a-tob-aich, a-tn-agat, adagar, adagatar, a-tn-aig, ad-aigtis, ad-n -agad, atagtha, a-tan-ebla, adeblad, adacht, adachtatár, atachtsat, adacht, adachta, at(n)aigid</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
  
  
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos v" style="color: #ff8000">v</span> Usually given as compd. of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aigid">aigid</a></span> (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> ii 451
      ), but in 
conjunct position -aig only is found. Perh. orig. ad-aig, -aig, 
a verb of the type described 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> § 543 (a)
       
(ro-cluinethar <span class="lbl">etc.</span>). Hence ad-aig here with deut. forms only 
and without vn. In mid. Ir. very common with tautological 
inf. pron. 3 s.m. (atn-aig). See <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=aigid">aigid</a></span>.</p>
