    <p class="result">(-bech) (<span class="pos vn." style="color: #ff8000">vn.</span> of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=for-boing">for-boing</a></span> ?) the instances suggest 
<span class="trans">an <span class="def">extraordinary</span> <span class="def">levy</span>, `<span class="def">surtax</span>';</span> <span class="lbl">e.g.</span>, when a client is 
wounded and the victim or surviving kindred is powerless to enforce the legal penalty, the lord (<span class="frn ">flaith</span>) intervenes and levies it. As a result, his lord is entitled to a third of the fine, so not only 
can the lord levy his own coirpdire but also a proportion of 
another's; the latter is a forbach (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 1623
      , cited below. This 
third, however, is apparently levied out of the amount due to 
the injured man, so does not increase the payment due from 
the defendant) (see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Stud.Hib." title="eDIL Bibliography">Stud. Hib.</strong></span> xvi 23
      ): f.<span class="oRef">¤</span>, forbuain `<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">exaction</span>, <span class="def">extortion</span>, <span class="def">additional</span>
