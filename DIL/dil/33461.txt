        oclachsa,

      <span class="trans">The <span class="def">condition</span> of being an `óclach'</span>, hence</p>
   
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">the <span class="def">age</span> of <span class="def">man-

        </span>; in Ml. possibly of adolescence, later of ripe manhood,

homine</span>
         (gl. aetate prima), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 44c26
        . ind oclachsa nó ind oited

(gl. iuventutis), 
          46b22
        . 
          colamna ais . . . .i. naoidendacht ┐

macdacht, gillacht ┐ oglachass, sendatu ┐ diblideta, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm.Y" title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm. Y</strong></span> 322
          
        . gníma óclachais (oppd. to macgníma), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 983.
         
          i

n-aís macantachta . . . i n-aeis a oglachais, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> viii 539.23
          
        .</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">b</span>) hence <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">manly</span> <span class="def">vigour</span>, <span class="def">valour</span>, <span class="def">quality</span> or <span class="def">functions</span> of a

<span class="def">warrior</span>
        </span>: 
          baí dia nertmaire ┐ dá óclachas dasfucastar in
