<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="28196">3 in-, </h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 3 in-,  or <a class="reference" href="28196">dil.ie/28196</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2019</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                in-, in-, in-, in-, ínnair, hinarmaighe, incathe, h-ion-Con-cculaind, hingallru, inrigh</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">in adjectival formation. Adjj. are formed by composition of the particle in- (which 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> § 358 (f) regards as probably an intensive <span class="pos prefix" style="color: #ff8000">prefix</span> but see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> xx 83
      ) 
with (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=3 in- (a)">a</a></span>) a substantive (the term ionshamhlughadh is applied 
to this type of adj. in 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Introd. 33 § 151
      , 
        § 152
      ) or vn. 
or (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=3 in- (b)">b</a></span>) a participle in -t(a)e, -th(a)e. These adjectives are 
indeclinable and those in group (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=3 in- (a)">a</a></span>) have the meaning 
<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">capable of</span>, <span class="def">fit for</span>, <span class="def">proper for</span>, <span class="def">worthy of</span>
      </span> (the action, thing 
or person expressed by the substantive): with the vn. 
the sense is usually passive: `<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">fit to be</span> ...</span>.' If formed with 
the part. they have nearly always the force of the Lat. 
gerundive.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
in- lenited the following initial, but in some later examples 
sh- is replaced by ts-.</p>
