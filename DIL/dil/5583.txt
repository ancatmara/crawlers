t-pret. is often replaced in mid. Ir. by an s-pret. as well as 
by mixed forms of t- and s-prets. Later the perfective form 
ruc- (also in some contexts tuc-, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 2935
       <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>) completely 
replaces preterite forms as a past tense. For exx. 
of conj. forms see also <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=as-beir">as-beir</a></span> and <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=do-beir">do-beir</a></span>.</p>
