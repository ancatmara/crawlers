<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="5034">aurrae, aurrad</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. aurrae, aurrad or <a class="reference" href="5034">dil.ie/5034</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2019</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                aurrai, urra, orra, earra, urradh, orradh, earradh, urrada, urrad, urradha, urraidhe, uraidhe</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype t">t</span>, <span class="gen m.">m.</span>, also <span class="itype o">o</span>.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> Not freq. in the earliest texts. Oldest ex. prob. aurrai (<span class="lbl">sic</span> MS), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Cr&#xED;thG." title="eDIL Bibliography">Críth G.</strong></span> 457
      , where used in the apparently secondary sense of <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">leader</span>, <span class="def">chief</span>
      </span>. If the form 
aurrad is old it could be &lt; *ar-fo-rath, <span class="lbl">cf.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=deorad">deorad</a></span> &lt; *di-fo-rath, with which it is frequently contrasted in legal texts 
        (thus <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wortk." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wortk.</strong></span> viii § 175
      ). But derived by Binchy from <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 r&#xE1;th">1 ráth</a></span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xxxiii 88
      . 
The inflexion and meaning are against treatment of aurrae 
as <span class="pos vn." style="color: #ff8000">vn.</span> of a compd. of <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=renaid">renaid</a></span>. urra, orra, earra <span class="gen f.">f.</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Decl. 
§ 7
