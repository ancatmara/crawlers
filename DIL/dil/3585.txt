<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="3585">anfolad</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. anfolad or <a class="reference" href="3585">dil.ie/3585</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                anfolta</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
  
  
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos adj" style="color: #ff8000">adj</span> (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=folud">folud</a></span>). Predominantly in <span class="number pl.">pl.</span> (anfolaid, anfolta). 
Later (in sense II) with <span class="number sg.">sg.</span> anfola as <span class="itype dental">dental</span> stem (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=2 fola">2 
fola</a></span>).</p>
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">I</span> <span class="trans">Legal. Lit. <span class="def">bad</span> `folud' (see Thurn. 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xiv 374
          ). Always 
of improper conduct or behaviour by one party to a contract, 
settlement, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> In Laws and Plummer MS notes translated <span class="def">disqualification</span>
        </span>, by Thurneysen <span class="quote">schlechtes Gehaben</span>, <span class="lbl">etc.</span>: 
da ndernu in flaith indligedh marbtha res in cele is dilus do 
. . . imsgar res tre anfoltuib flatha <span class="lbl">i.e.</span> <span class="ediltr">if the lord commits a 
murder which injures the client the latter may withdraw 
from his relationship with him</span>, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> ii 274.9 Comm.
         tria 
anbfoltaibh flatha (of a lord who brings a larger retinue to 
the house of a tenant than he is entitled to), 
          232.8 Comm.
         
imscarad imtoga . . . cen anfolta nechtar da lina <span class="ediltr">
          <span class="lbl">i.e.</span> a 
separation by mutual choice of `<span class="frn ">flaith</span>' and `<span class="frn ">céile</span>' without

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xiv 384 § 49

na bit anfolaid in ceili gaibter ann <span class="ediltr">
          <span class="lbl">i.e.</span> a separation which
