<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="33020">nechtar</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. nechtar or <a class="reference" href="33020">dil.ie/33020</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                nechtar</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos pron." style="color: #ff8000">pron.</span> <span class="trans">one of <span class="def">two</span>, <span class="def">either</span>
      </span> (= Lat. <span class="frn ">alteruter</span>), folld. by 
part. gen. or prep. DE. In O.Ir. treated as <span class="gen neut.">neut.</span> (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> i 392
      , 

        ii 64
      ), <span class="lbl">cf.</span> expression nechtar n-ái (in which, however, the n- 
appears fixed, see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 37b18
       quoted below). Sometimes 
treated as <span class="itype o">o</span>-st., but also invariable.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
Folld. by gen. of noun: co nechtar inna dese-si <span class="ediltr">to either of 
these twain</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 42a7
      . 
        nechtar na dā idan . . . .i. idu serci ┐ idu 
eóit, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> i 122.23
        
       (LU) = 
        n.<span class="oRef">¤</span> dā ní, 
          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 7
        
       (Eg.). 
        ní ro gluais in 
mac nechtar a dā chois, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> i 118 § 11.
        
       
        nechtar dá neche, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CCath." title="eDIL Bibliography">CCath.</strong></span> 1008
        
       (n.<span class="oRef">¤</span> do na nechi <span class="lbl">v.l.</span>). In legal texts, nechtar dá 
lína <span class="trans">
        <i>one of the two parties</i> (concerned in a transaction):</span> 
        cin 
folluga do nechtur da lina, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> ii 72.9
        
      , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        312.23
      , 
        318.6
      . 
 <span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> cor nechtar da lina <span class="ediltr">the contract made by either party</span>, 
        356 z
      .</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
Folld. by gen. of pron.: ni ain nechtar n-áii <span class="ediltr">he will not protect either of them</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 25d14
      . ó nechtar n-ái, gl. a neutro, 

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 37b18
      . ni techta nechtar n-ae urtechta <span class="ediltr">neither party is 
entitled</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> ii 406.20
      . ni dergene nectar n-ai olc fri anali 
[<span class="lbl">leg.</span> arali]. 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> iv 46.21
       = 
        nechtar de, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBFr." title="eDIL Bibliography">TBFr.</strong></span> 420.
        
       
        nechtar 
fathar, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> i 336.13
        
       (see below).</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
With DE, esp. in stereotyped expression n.<span class="oRef">¤</span> de <span class="trans">one of the <span class="def">two</span>
      </span> 
(de = prep.+neut. s. pron.): ni rubi nechtar de cen alail 
 <span class="ediltr">neither of them (<span class="lbl">sc.</span> man and woman) can be without the other</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 
11c17
      . <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 35d24
      ; 
        42b2
       (n.<span class="oRef">¤</span> dae). is nectar de, gl. vel Terentius vel Cicero, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 27b6
      , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        173b6
      . 
        ni bá la nechtar de, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/FB" title="eDIL Bibliography">FB</strong></span> 73
        
       
= ni ba la nechtar fathar <span class="ediltr">neither of you shall have it</span>, Eg. (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> 
i 336.13
      ). 
        conná cumaic nechtar de cobair araile, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> 
          viii 
314.32
        
      .</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
Used as quasi-<span class="pos adv." style="color: #ff8000">adv.</span>: roga duit . . . a n-imtheclamad no a 
n-imscothad nechtar de (= <span class="ediltr">you shall have your choice either 
of gathering or of cutting them</span>), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TBC-LL1" title="eDIL Bibliography">TBC-LL¹</strong></span> 1407.
       láech aile for 
áth dō-som nō nechtar de longphort do gabail d'ḟeraib hErend 
(<span class="ediltr">to send) another warrior to the ford to (fight) him or alternatively</span> 
. . ., 
        1785
      .</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
In Mid.Ir. the <span class="number pl.">pl.</span> <span class="pos pron." style="color: #ff8000">pron.</span> is also used with DE: 
        ni thibre 
nechtar dib-so a chuid fen, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PH" title="eDIL Bibliography">PH</strong></span> 7590.
        
       
        turbada . . . ar neachtar 
dib, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> ii 304.28 Comm.
        
       
        ni thucc M. nechtar dib doib, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ann.Conn." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ann. Conn.</strong></span> 1235 § 11.
        
      
    </p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
Nechtar de comes to be looked on as a single word and is 
somet. folld. by a part. <span class="case gen.">gen.</span> or DE, as in follg. exx. from Laws 
Comm.: ac nechtar de in da nannad [<span class="lbl">leg.</span> in da n-imad] <span class="ediltr">either 
of the two parties</span>, 
      
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> ii 318.18
      ; similarly 
        358.15
      , 
        366.14
       (where 
for: in da naimad, read: in da n-imad). do n.<span class="oRef">¤</span> de don lanamain 
 <span class="ediltr">for either of the married couple</span>, 
        ii 360.24
      . ma ta n.<span class="oRef">¤</span> de dib aice 
 <span class="ediltr">if he has either of them</span>, 
        332.3
      , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        406.27
      , 
        iii 210.14
      .</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
In class. Ir. also with prep. oc (ic): 
        dā aingel . . . long thúissi 
a laimh nechtair aca, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Fl.Earls" title="eDIL Bibliography">Fl. Earls</strong></span> 234.38
        
      . 
        neachtar acu sin, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ITS" title="eDIL Bibliography">ITS</strong></span> 
xiv 58.3
        
      .</p>
