<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="32723">muincend</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. muincend or <a class="reference" href="32723">dil.ie/32723</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                muinchend</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="itype &#x101;">ā</span>, <span class="gen f.">f.</span>, (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 muin">1 muin</a></span> and <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=muince">muince</a></span>) also muinchend (a form 
due to association with <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 cenn">1 cenn</a></span>? occas. treated as <span class="gen neut.">neut.</span> or <span class="gen masc.">masc.</span>).</p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) the strict sense seems to be <span class="trans">a <span class="def">narrow</span> <span class="def">expanse</span> of <span class="def">sea</span>, 
<span class="def">whether</span> <span class="def">inlet</span> or <span class="def">strait</span>
        </span>. muinchend cumuṅg etarru (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> between 
an island and the mainland), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 79.
         
          tria muincinn Mara 
Romuir, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 3987.
          
         
          co muncend mara Romair, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 135a47
          
        . 

          for muincind Mara Rúaid, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> iv 142.9
          
        . 
          tar muincheann 
mhara Romhair, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Keat." title="eDIL Bibliography">Keat.</strong></span> ii 441
          
         (poem). ra chumtaig da cholomain immon muncend Gaditánda (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> the strait of Gibraltar), 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 223b18
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TTr." title="eDIL Bibliography">TTr.</strong></span> 466
        ) = 
          immon muincind nG., 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/TTr.2" title="eDIL Bibliography">TTr.²</strong></span> 43
          
        . 

          go muncenn nGaididen, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Rawl." title="eDIL Bibliography">Rawl.</strong></span> 76b13
          
        . 
          tar muncinn Gaididoin, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Nenn." title="eDIL Bibliography">Todd Nenn.</strong></span> 54.
          
         This seems to be the sense in the follg. exx. 
from CCath., where Stokes translates `surface': for muincind 
mara Icht, 
        
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CCath." title="eDIL Bibliography">CCath.</strong></span> 2382
        . do mhuincinn mara Torrein ota inis Seston 
. . . co hinis Abido (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> the strait between Sestos and Abydos), 

          3452
        . <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
          2617
         where `in muincenn muiridhi' refers to the 
strait separating the island of Salona from the coast.</p>
    
  
  
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">b</span>) <span class="trans">in wider sense <span class="def">surface</span> or <span class="def">expanse</span> (<i>of</i> <span class="def">sea</span>);</span> common in 
poetry. 
          for muncind mara Caisp, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Nenn." title="eDIL Bibliography">Todd Nenn.</strong></span> 234
          
         (

        ). 

          do múnud eóluis dar muncind mara ┐ mórḟairge, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CRR" title="eDIL Bibliography">CRR</strong></span> 8
