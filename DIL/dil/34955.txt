      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="gen f.">f.</span> (Lat. <span class="frn ">reliquiae</span>) <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number p.">p.</span> reilce. <span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number p.">p.</span> 
        relec, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 4040
        
      . 

        -reilce, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/F&#xE9;l." title="eDIL Bibliography">Fél.</strong></span> Oct. 1
        
       (in sense b). Later forms are roileg and 
reilg. roileg reileag <span class="gen f.">f.</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Dec. § 54.11

        relgi, 
          ex. 1404
        
      .</p>
   
    
      <p class="result"> 
(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">a <span class="def">burial-place</span>
        </span>, used both of pagan and Christian cemeteries: 
          
            <span class="frn ">fecerunt fossam rotundam (in) similitudinem</span> fertae, 
<span class="frn ">quia sic faciebant (Scotici) homines. Nobiscum hautem</span> reli(c) 
<span class="frn ">uocatu(r) id est reliquiae, et</span> feurt, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Trip." title="eDIL Bibliography">Trip.</strong></span> 317.18
          
         (
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ardm." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ardm.</strong></span> 12b1
        ). 

          relic .i. <span class="frn ">a reliqui[i]s sanctorum</span>
          , 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm.Y" title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm. Y</strong></span> 1096
          
        . reilic, gl. 
coemeterium, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ir.Gl." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ir. Gl.</strong></span> 691
        . rupu sí a rreilic, gl. quorum cadavera 
prostrata sunt in deserto, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 33a22
        . 
          combad he bad reilec

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 4109
          
        . for son reilgce, 
`<span class="quote">nach Art eines Friedhofs</span>', 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wortk." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wortk.</strong></span> 201

        ). 
          rob í Tailltin tres primh-reileag na hEirenn,

oratory, somet. including the latter: 
          in scuap bís immon corp

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 161a

          roileg uasal

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> xii 393.24
          
        . 
          nach

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> ii 196 § 12
          
        . 

          dolluid desel relci,

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AFM" title="eDIL Bibliography">AFM</strong></span> iii 8.16

          <span class="ediltr">he

        </span>,

          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> p. 64
         and 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> ix 114
