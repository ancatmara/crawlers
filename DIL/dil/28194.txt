    <p class="result"> 
The forms int, -nt, -(s)int are used before ṡ, except int 
of <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> <span class="gen masc.">masc.</span> which occurs before vowels.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
The forms na, inna <span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span>
      <span class="gen fem.">fem.</span>, <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number p.">p.</span> <span class="gen fem.">fem.</span> and <span class="gen neut.">neut.</span>, and <span class="case a">a</span>
      <span class="number p.">p.</span> 
of all genders, geminate follg. consonants and prefix h to 
vowels (but this h is very rarely written in O.Ir. <span class="lbl">Cf.</span>, however, 
        
          inna hirise, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 209b24
        
      ).</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
Before the <span class="number dual">dual</span> the art. always has the form in, -n except 

        
          ind da la, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> ii 33.21
        
       (Bv.).</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
The <span class="case dat.">dat.</span> forms without preposition, in, ind, int, occur 
in the formation of advv., see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 in II (a)">II (a)</a></span> below.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
Irregularities are not uncommon, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> ind for int, int for 
ind, <span class="lbl">etc.</span> See Strachan, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> iv 62
      -
        3
       and 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Zeuss" title="eDIL Bibliography">Zeuss</strong></span>, <span class="lbl">loc. cit.</span>
       </p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
The original initial s- appears only in the <span class="case dat.">dat.</span> and <span class="case acc.">acc.</span> 
after prepositions which originally ended in a consonant, 
with which it unites to produce -ss-, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> 
        cossin n-óin, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 
2a21
        
      . 
        isin anmim, 
          9c30
        
      . The s- does not remain after 
OC though this does not lenite a follg. subst. TRE lenites 
a subst. but the s- of the art. remains, perhaps through 
analogy. After FOR, which did not originally lenite, occur 
forms both with and without -s-, those with -s- being the 
more usual: 
        forsin dígail, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 1d2
        
      . 
        fornaib gnímaib, 

          5a5
        
      . After prepositions originally and in O.Ir. ending in 
vowels both the initial s- and the vowel of the article are 
lost: 
        ón bunud, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 33b2
        
      . 
        dind insci, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 5c16
        
      . dont 
lathur, <span class="lbl">ib.</span> With OC occur ocin(d) (so always in Wb.) and 
ocon(d); with IMM, immin and immun.</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
In medial position <i>-nd-</i> had become <i>-nn-</i> by the O.Ir. 
period but in a few archaic examples, <i>-nd-</i> is written: 
        indá 
érrend gl. stigmata, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 20d5
       (<span class="lbl">a prima manu</span>). inda fodb 
gl. exuias, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> ii 47.24
       (see Thurneysen, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> iii 53
      ). 

        
          dundaib abstolaib, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Thes." title="eDIL Bibliography">Thes.</strong></span> ii 247.12
        
       (Camb.). 
        dendibh Airgiallaib, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/AU" title="eDIL Bibliography">AU</strong></span> i 178.8 (A.D. 726)
      . After prepositions the 
<i>-n-</i> is single even between vowels: cosnaib, frisna, donaib.</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
Occasionally even in the O.Ir. glosses the first syllable of 
the dissyllabic form is dropped, and this becomes the rule 
later: 
        na teora persana, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 9c30
        
      . 
        na cétne tuisten, 
          21b4
        
      . 

        na cetnae rainne, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 18b1
        
      . For other exx. see below. In 
the follg. instances from Sg., in (acc.) seems to have dropped 
out after <i>-r</i> and before a numeral, leaving an eclipsing <i>n</i>. 
etar ṅdi rainn `<span class="quote">between the two parts</span>,' 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 2b2
      . 
        etar ṅdá 
n-<span class="frn ">ulla</span>
        , 
          45b19
        
      . far n-óeṅdeilb `<span class="quote">according to the same paradigm</span>,' 
        90b2
      . 
        for n-óin ṅdeilb, 
          201b6
        
      .</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
The forms in <i>-d</i> (ind, -sind, <i>-nd</i>) remain only before 
vowels and before lenited <i>f</i>, <i>r</i>, <i>l</i>, <i>n</i> and, in Wb., sometimes 
also before lenited <i>m</i> and <i>b</i>.</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
In forms such as 
        dinab fochidib, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 11b4
        
      , 
        dinab gabalib, 

          13d33
        
      , 
        arnab uilib cumactib, 
          21a13
        
      , the quality of the final 
-b of the article is assimilated to that of the following 
initial. <span class="case d">d</span>
      <span class="number p.">p.</span> forms without the final -b developed from 
such examples as 
        donaballaib, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 12b2
        
      , 
        huana fochaidib, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 54a18
        
      , 
        hona mainénaib, 
          69c5
        
      , where the final of the 
article became assimilated to a following initial labial. 
These forms, however, are still infrequent in the O.Ir. 
period. Three times in the glosses the <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number p.">p.</span> <span class="gen fem.">fem.</span> and <span class="gen neut.">neut.</span> 
form occurs as <span class="gen masc.">masc.</span>: 
        
          inna leomain, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ml." title="eDIL Bibliography">Ml.</strong></span> 80a10
        
      . 
        
          na tri 
recte, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wb." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wb.</strong></span> 29a16
        
      . 
        
          na sothe, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Sg." title="eDIL Bibliography">Sg.</strong></span> 64a14
        
       (see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/GOI" title="eDIL Bibliography">GOI</strong></span> 
§ 309
      ).</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
The <span class="pos art." style="color: #ff8000">art.</span> is regularly sg. before collectives and personal 
numerals.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
When an ordinal numeral adj. follows the art., in the older 
texts the inflection is regular: 
        na dechmaide bliadna, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IT</strong></span> 
iii 54 § 99.

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> iii 25.35

          26.26

the <span class="gen masc.">masc.</span> form of the art. being somet. used before a fem.

      <p class="result"> 
The chief changes occurring in the form of the article in 
Mid.Ir. are</p>
     
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">i</span>) The <span class="case n">n</span>
          <span class="number p.">p.</span> <span class="gen masc.">masc.</span> forms ind, in, int gradually 
disappear and are replaced by the forms common to the 
<span class="gen fem.">fem.</span> and <span class="gen neut.">neut.</span>, inna, na.</p>
        
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">ii</span>) By 1000 A.D., a n- in the <span class="case n">n</span>
          <span class="number s.">s.</span> 
and <span class="case a">a</span>
          <span class="number s.">s.</span> <span class="gen neut.">neut.</span> has virtually disappeared, the only certain 
examples in SR being: 
            a lleth n-aill, 
          
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SR" title="eDIL Bibliography">SR</strong></span> 5152
          , 
            
              a mbé, 
              5974
            
           
(see Strachan, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Phil.Soc.Trans." title="eDIL Bibliography">Phil. Soc. Trans.</strong></span> p. 7
          ).</p>
        
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">iii</span>) -(s)naib in the <span class="case d">d</span>
          <span class="number p.">p.</span> 
gives way to -(s)na.</p>
        
      
        <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">iv</span>) the -d forms are less common.</p>
        
      <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">v</span>) the monosyllabic na tends to displace inna.</p>
     
  
    <p class="result"> 
In Mod. Ir. in, ind &gt; an, an t-.</p>

    <p class="result"> 
<span class="edilb">III</span> Usage.</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
The article is always proclitic and is used before a substantive to express the definite character of that substantive 
unless this is already apparent either from the nature of 
the substantive itself, as in the case of proper names, or 
from some accompanying defining word or phrase, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> 
when the substantive is followed by a definite genitive, or 
preceded by a possessive adjective.</p>
