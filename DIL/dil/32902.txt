<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="32902">4 ná</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 4 ná or <a class="reference" href="32902">dil.ie/32902</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                nach</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos conj." style="color: #ff8000">conj.</span> (in O.Ir. generally written na) <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">nor</span>
      </span>; used after a 
negative vb. or CEN to co-ordinate an additional member of 
a compd. (or elliptical) sentence. In O.Ir. na (ná) is used 
before an accented vowel or consonant, nach before an atonic 
vowel, see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> ii 251
      ; later the usage varies (see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=6 nach">6 nach</a></span>). In 
O.Ir. occas. geminates follg. consonant.</p>
