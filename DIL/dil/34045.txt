    <p class="result">Used in Glosses (1) to identify some word (subst., adj., rel.

pron.) with a noun or pers. pron. previously mentioned in

the sentence (freq. glosses the pron. <span class="frn ">ipse</span>; see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> ii 153
§ 490
      ); (2) to give an additional or explanatory detail about

some person or persons already mentioned in the sent.

(general use in Mid. and Mod.Ir., <span class="lbl">cf.</span> the Anglo-Irish idiom:

`<span class="frn ">Are you angry with me and I telling you the truth?</span>'). The

form osé is used referring to a neuter (see 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SP" title="eDIL Bibliography">SP</strong></span> iii 6

       (gl. quid iustus
