(<span class="edilb">b</span>) <span class="trans">
          <span class="def">free</span>, <span class="def">enfranchised</span>
        </span>, hence in Laws of an enfranchised 
person possessing legal status and a corresponding lóg-enech 
or eneclann; for various classes of whom see the 
          Uraicecht 
Becc, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 1 ff
          
        . See also below under compds. 
          saor ┐ daor, 

            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 40.11
          
        , glossed 
          saor .i. grad flatha, 
            42.2 Comm
          
        . Such 
`free' classes might be subdivided into those of higher and 
lower status, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> the class `nemeth' (person of free status) 
included saer- and daer-nemeth: 
          atat do neimid docuisin for 
talmain .i. saeirnemead ┐ daerneimead. Ite saernemead filead 
(= filet) and .i. ecalsi, flatha, filidh, feine. Daerneimead 
imorro aes gacha dana olchena, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 14.1ff. A
         `daer-' 
person might become `saer' by virtue of property or calling 
and vice versa a `saer-' might forfeit his status: 
          i[s] saer cid 
cach creanus a suiri dia dan, 
            v 14.28
          
        , with comm.: 
          is saer in 
cach cen[daig]us dan do da saerad no cendaigis saeiri do dun 
dan bis aige, amail ata gaba, 
            14.29
          
        , <span class="lbl">i.e.</span> who attains status by 
acquiring or practising a craft. 
          saer cach o mainib, daer cach 
o mbelaib, 
            v 18.12
          
        , <span class="lbl">i.e.</span> a man was s.<span class="oRef">¤</span> in virtue of his possession 
but might become `daer' by contracting as daercheile under 
a lord. The distinction may orig. have been racial, certain 
ruling tribes being s.<span class="oRef">¤</span>, the inferior ones `daer'. 
          tri cenela saera 
randsat in indsi so, 
            <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> i 78.12
