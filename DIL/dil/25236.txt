<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="25236">2 gairid</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 2 gairid or <a class="reference" href="25236">dil.ie/25236</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                garaid</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   

    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> (also gairig, garaid) <span class="trans">a <span class="def">youth</span> <span class="def">under age</span>, a <span class="def">minor</span> of the 
<span class="def">freeman</span> <span class="def">class</span>
      </span> (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Ir.Recht" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ir. Recht</strong></span> 86 f
      .): fodla febe tra: inol, ocus 
flesgach, ocus garaid <span class="ediltr">classes of worth</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 78.23
      . See also 

        80.5
       gloss, where he is said to be 8 years old. His honour-price was a sheep worth two `screpalls,' <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> :
        uan meich do 
fleascach ocus caera do gairid, dairt do fir mbidbadh, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> 
v 84.20
        
      ; with gloss: 
        caera .i. caera is fiu da screpall do gairid, 

          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 23
        
      . 
        a tri n-aesa (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> flescach, gairid and midbath) .viii. 
mbliadna ┐. x. mbliadna ┐. xii. bliadna, 
          86.2 Comm.
        
       </p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
The exact meaning of the word is uncertain. It may be 
the same in origin as <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=1 gairid">1 gairid</a></span>, with which some commentators 
seem to connect it: 
        garith .i. a edh no edh na hechlaisce, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 1981
        
       (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H4.22" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 4.22</strong></span>, p. 19
      ). do gairid .i. don ti bis ed a [ḟ]id 
ar a gair `<span class="quote">his switch convenient to him</span>' (?), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Laws" title="eDIL Bibliography">Laws</strong></span> v 84.23 
Comm.
       <span class="lbl">Cf.</span> 
        fleascach .i. is aici bis in fleasc bundsaidi, 

          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OCurry" title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Curry</strong></span> 559
        
       (
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span>, 277
      ; 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/C&#xF3;icCon." title="eDIL Bibliography">Cóic Con.</strong></span> p. 69
      ).</p>
