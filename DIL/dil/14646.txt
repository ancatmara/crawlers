                2013</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                darba, dærba</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   


    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span>  <span class="trans">a <span class="def">bondwoman</span>
      </span>, syn. cumal. 
        darba .i. cumal .i. 
bantraill, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span>, p. 68 c
        
      . = 
        darb .i. cumul .i. bantraill, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/CIH" title="eDIL Bibliography">CIH</strong></span> ii 612.10
        
      . 
        darba .i. imat .i. cumal amail 
atberr a Cáin techta bered darba mucha anfis .i. cumal ó 
chách tar éisi, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ODav." title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Dav.</strong></span> 629
        
      . rohír dam...deich ndairbé, 
Amra Conrói, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> ii 3. 8
       (H 3. 18 gl. .i. bancumala; darba 
YBL. ndarba, Eg. 88). 
        atgaith dom...deich ndærba, 
          <span class="lbl">ib.</span> 
4. 7
        
       (H 3. 18 gl. cumala; <span class="lbl">v.l.</span> ndarba, Eg. darba YBL). 
The 
        deich narba, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span> , p. 651
        
       (gl. .i. deich cumala) 
stands for deich ndarba and the glossator's arba .i. cumal 
is a vox nihili.</p>
  
  
    <p class="result"> 
All these forms seem to originate from an old compound dáir-bé, dáir-bae, dáir-ba (<span class="lbl">cf.</span> <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=derm&#xE1;r">dermár</a></span>: <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=dermair">dermair</a></span>). 
It is hardly necessary with Stokes, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> ii 9
      , to assume two 
etymologically different words dáir-be and darba (Stokes' 
darb, Ériu <span class="lbl">l.c.</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xxvii 88
      , is an imaginary <span class="number sg.">sg.</span> to darba, 
H 3. 18 reads darba); for `darba' may be ascribed to the 
attempt of the grammarians to bring the no longer understood `dairba' (<span class="lbl">i.e.</span> daorbha) into accordance with the rule 
`leathan la leathan.' That dáir-bé, as might be suggested, 
is only an etymologizing transformation of darba is strongly 
contradicted by the writing dærba, Amra Conrói H 3. 18 .</p>
