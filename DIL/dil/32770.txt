<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="32770">1 muire</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. 1 muire or <a class="reference" href="32770">dil.ie/32770</a></span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                muire, murib, muirig, muire, muiri, muireach, muirech, murra</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   
    <p class="result">
      <span class="pos n" style="color: #ff8000">n</span> 
      <span class="gen m.">m.</span>, <span class="itype gutt.">gutt.</span> (but <span class="case d">d</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> 
        do muire
        , 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/&#xC9;riu" title="eDIL Bibliography">Ériu</strong></span> i 215.29
        
       = 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span> 
p. 25 a
      ; <span class="case d">d</span>
      <span class="number p.">p.</span> 
        murib, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LU" title="eDIL Bibliography">LU</strong></span> 3413
        
       = 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/SCC" title="eDIL Bibliography">SCC</strong></span> 18
      ).</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
A term denoting rank applied to individuals; the precise 
sense is uncertain and prob. varied in different ages. As a 
techn. term in legal tracts it seems to denote <span class="trans">a <span class="def">specific</span> <span class="def">social</span> 
<span class="def">grade</span>, prob. <span class="def">headman</span> of a district or `fine'</span>. In the plan of 
the Tech Midchuarta in 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/LL" title="eDIL Bibliography">LL</strong></span> 29
       the `muirig' are seated next to 
the `airig forgaill' and receive the same portion (they are not 
given in the plan in 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/YBL" title="eDIL Bibliography">YBL</strong></span> 418
      ); but in 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/C&#xE1;inAd." title="eDIL Bibliography">Cáin Ad.</strong></span> § 51
       it is said 
that the fine for slandering the wife of an `aire désa' was 
seven half-cumals, that for the wife of a `muire' only seven 
ounces. Some of the exx. given below suggest the sense of 
<span class="trans">a <span class="def">military</span> <span class="def">leader</span> or <span class="def">officer</span> in command of a division</span>. Occurs 
several times in AU between the years 1159 and 1200, apparently to denote <span class="trans">the <span class="def">head</span> of a <span class="def">clan</span> or <span class="def">sept</span>
      </span> (always wrongly 
translated `steward'); the corresponding term in FM is in all 
cases `<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=tigerna">tigherna</a></span>'. In lit. often used loosely in sense of <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">leader</span>, 
<span class="def">chief</span>
      </span>. Stokes, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/RC" title="eDIL Bibliography">RC</strong></span> xviii 76
      , considers it cognate with A.S. 
<span class="frn ">mære</span> `<span class="quote">noble, famous</span>'.</p>
