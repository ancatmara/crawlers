    <p class="result"> 
During the Mid.Ir. period the <span class="case a">a</span>
      <span class="case d">d</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> form rainn, freq. written 
roinn, comes into use as <span class="case n">n</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> and gradually supplants rann in 
the sense of <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">part</span>
      </span>; rann however persists in early Mod.Ir. 
(chiefly in the sense of <span class="trans">
        <span class="def">party</span>, <span class="def">side</span>, <span class="def">adherent</span>
      </span>), being treated as 
<span class="itype o">o</span>, <span class="gen m.">m.</span> roinn <span class="gen f.">f.</span> (<span class="case g">g</span>
      <span class="number s.">s.</span> roinne, ronna), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/IGT" title="eDIL Bibliography">IGT</strong></span> Dec. § 42.9
      . rann (carad) 
<span class="gen m.">m.</span> 
        § 73
      .</p>
   
    <p class="result"> 
Oft. confounded with rind.</p>
