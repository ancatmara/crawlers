<div class="row result"><div class="col-sm-3 result_headword"><h3 headword_id="32121">mí-</h3><span class="citation">Cite this: eDIL s.v. mí- or <a class="reference" href="32121">dil.ie/32121</a></span><br/><span class="revision">Last Revised:
                2019</span><br/><p style="margin-top: 4px" class="small">Forms:
                míochoingiolla, mmíchomlabrai, mignímu, mí-imberta, miarlig, mibésaig, micerdaigh, miceirdighi, míchlodcha, michlothaigfetar, michlothaigthi, míchóirighthe, miochóruighedh, mídúthrastar, mignethi, mi-tn-imret, nómíonoraigeadh, mitomnadar</p></div><div class="col-sm-9 result_content">
   


    <p class="result">pejorative <span class="pos particle" style="color: #ff8000">particle</span>, possibly (as suggested by 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/PedersenVgl.Gr." title="eDIL Bibliography">Pedersen Vgl. Gr.</strong></span> 
ii 10
      , <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Wortk." title="eDIL Bibliography">Wortk.</strong></span> § 9
      ) orig. a <span style="color: #ff8000">compar.</span> mis `<span class="trans">
        <span class="def">less</span>
      </span>', akin to 
second syll. of Lat. <span class="frn ">nimis</span>, a view supported by the compds. 
miscais `<span class="ediltr">hatred</span>' (&lt;cais `<span class="ediltr">love</span>'), misimbert (see <span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=m&#xED;imbert">mí-imbert</a></span> 
below) and perhaps misceird `<span class="ediltr">evil step</span>' (?), 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ODav." title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Dav.</strong></span> 392.
       In 
O. and earlier Mid.Ir. always pejorative, often opposed to 
compds. with caín-; in later lang. often a mere negative. 
Used in composition with (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=m&#xED;- compds. I">I</a></span>) nouns, (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=m&#xED;- compds. II">II</a></span>) adjs. derived from 
nouns or participial, (<span class="ref"><a class="normal" href="search?search_in=headword&amp;q=m&#xED;- compds. III">III</a></span>) occasionally with vbs.; often used 
in etymol. glosses, <span class="lbl">e.g.</span> 
        milliud <span class="frn ">quasi</span> mí-shilliud .i. drochshilliud, 
          <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Corm.Y" title="eDIL Bibliography">Corm. Y</strong></span> 858.
        
       The follg. list is illustrative only.</p>
