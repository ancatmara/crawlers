    <p class="result">(<span class="edilb">a</span>) <span class="trans">An <span class="def">ollave</span>, the highest grade of `fili'</span> (in O.Ir. <span class="trans">
        <span class="frn ">ollam</span> may have denoted an office as much as a grade of learning; later used to denote the grade alone</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/UR" title="eDIL Bibliography">UR</strong></span> 91-94
      ),

      (ollum).

        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/ZCP" title="eDIL Bibliography">ZCP</strong></span> v 499 § 7. 
      
      secht ngrad filed

.i. ollam, ansrut[h], <span class="lbl">etc.</span>, 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/OMulc." title="eDIL Bibliography">O'Mulc.</strong></span> 537. 
      He was entitled to a

Druim Cet in 590: 
        no bid .xxx. i cléir cac[h] olloman,

      = 
        [o]lldam,

      An ollave was attached to the

court of each of the provincial kings, often to those of sub-

kings; and there seems (at some periods) to have been an

`ard-ollam' who exercised authority over all the provincial

`filidh'. 
        int ollam filedh iarna uirdned ag rig tuath,

The inferior grades of `filidh' had to discharge various offices

for the ollave, such as keeping his two dogs (task of the `cana')

and fostering his children (that of the `drisech'); and the

erenaghs (airchindig eclaisi) had to maintain his horses,


        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/H3.18" title="eDIL Bibliography">H 3.18</strong></span> p. 133
