middle syllable. To judge from the spoken language the

    <p class="result"> 
MOD. (all districts): dá ríribh, dhá ríribh (O'R. also ra 
ríreadh): glacfad do choṁairle do chroidhe dhá ríribh 
(Kerry; <span class="lbl">cf.</span> 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/S&#xE9;adna" title="eDIL Bibliography">Séadna¹</strong></span> 136. 21
      ). an dá ríribh atáoi tú <span class="ediltr">are you 
in earnest? do you mean it?</span> In Munster also dáiríripíre 
(possibly from dáríribh fíre). Sc. <span class="frn ">da rìreadh</span>, <span class="frn ">a rìreadh</span> 
(with aspirated r-): <span class="frn ">am bheil thu a rìreadh?</span> or: <span class="frn ">an ann da 
rìreadh a tha thu? tha e cheart rìreadh</span> <span class="ediltr">he is in real 
earnest</span>; <span class="frn ">a cheart is a rìreadh</span> <span class="ediltr">in good earnest</span>. <span class="frn ">da rìreadh</span> 
= dáiríre above. Also Sc. <span class="frn ">da ríribh</span> according to 
        <span><strong class="pop" style="color:blue" href="bibliography/Macd." title="eDIL Bibliography">Macd.</strong></span> 
762 b
       (thus always at Poolewe). M`Alpine's <span class="frn ">da rìdheadh</span> 
(p. 210) is a mistake for <span class="frn ">da rìreadh</span>, and the spelling <span class="frn ">d'arrighribh</span> (p. 98) invented by himself.</p>
